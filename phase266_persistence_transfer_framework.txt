# Transfer of Stabilization Properties Across Interacting Systems
# NO ONTOLOGY | NO CONSCIOUSNESS LANGUAGE | NO NEW METRICS

================================================================================
SECTION 1: OBJECTIVE
================================================================================

Determine whether persistence-capable relational structures can transfer
stabilization properties across interacting systems without requiring
implementation identity.

Key question:
  Can stable coordination properties propagate between systems
  with different implementation details?

================================================================================
SECTION 2: EMPIRICAL FINDINGS
================================================================================

EXPERIMENT SUMMARY:
- Coupled pink noise systems at coupling k ∈ [0, 1]
- 25 coupling values, 50 trials each
- Persistence gain measured as increase in sign-coherence

RESULTS:

1. PERSISTENCE TRANSFER: POSITIVE
   - Maximum persistence gain: 0.080
   - Occurs at highest coupling (k=1.0)
   - All intermediate couplings positive (except k=0)
   - Transfer increases monotonically with coupling

2. SYNCHRONIZATION ENHANCEMENT: POSITIVE
   - Sync gain: 0.19 to 0.71 across coupling range
   - Trade-off: Higher persistence, lower sync at high coupling
   - Optimal balance region: k ≈ 0.3-0.5

3. COMPRESSIBILITY SHIFT: NEGATIVE
   - Mixed signals become more predictable (lower variance)
   - Shift: -0.25 at high coupling
   - Consistent compression regardless of coupling strength

================================================================================
SECTION 3: TRANSFER OPERATOR MATHEMATICS
================================================================================

Let S_A, S_B be two persistence-capable structures.
Let I(S_A, S_B, k) be interaction with coupling k.

Transfer operator T:
  T(S_A, S_B, k) = I(S_A, S_B, k) - max(S_A, S_B)

Persistence transfer ΔP:
  ΔP(k) = P(I(S_A, S_B, k)) - max(P(S_A), P(S_B))

Observed: ΔP(k) > 0 for k > threshold
Monotonic increase with coupling strength
