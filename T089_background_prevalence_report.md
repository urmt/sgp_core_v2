# T089 Report: Background Prevalence Audit

## Question
Are the 9 substrate assumptions genuinely **generated by** MC2+MC3+MC4, or are they properties of the simulation substrate itself?

## Design
- **Random background**: 1000 systems with all parameters sampled uniformly
- **Structured ensemble**: 240 systems from the T082 grid (replicated)
- Compare prevalence (>= 0.2, >= 0.5) and compute enrichment over random background

## Results

### MC satisfaction, NOT viability, is the driver

The "Non-viable + MC-sat" group (systems that pass MC2+MC3+MC4 but fail viability):
- CD2: 100%, EC1: 100%, SR1: 100%

The "Viable + NOT MC-sat" group (viable systems that don't pass MC2+MC3+MC4):
- CD2: 81.5%, EC1: 74.1%, SR1: 51.9% (close to random background)

This proves the enrichment is specifically driven by the MC2+MC3+MC4 >= 0.5 condition, not by viability.

## Corrected Picture

| Previous Claim (T082/T088) | T089 Finding |
|---------------------------|--------------|
| MC2+MC3+MC4 generates 5/9 assumptions (IC1, IS1, CD2, EC1, SR1) | MC2+MC3+MC4 generates **3/9** (CD2, EC1, SR1). IC1 and IS1 are simulator artifacts |
| 4 assumptions are "missing" (OC2, OC1, CD1, IS2) | All 4 are simulator artifacts — present in 99-100% of random systems |
| The architecture generates all 9 assumptions | 6/9 are substrate-level guarantees; only 3/9 are MC-driven |

## Implications

1. **The architecture is NOT complete as MC2+MC3+MC4 alone.** It generates only 3/9 assumptions. The other 6 are intrinsic to the simulation substrate — any system with a transition matrix and a history will have them.

2. **The "5 emergent" from T082 was inflated.** IC1 and IS1 appeared emergent because their none-group (MC2+MC3+MC4 < 0.5 simultaneously) had pathologically low values, not because the MCs generated them.

3. **The "4 missing" were trivial artifacts.** They are present in essentially all systems because they measure basic properties like "has transitions" (OC2), "has deterministic structure" (CD1), "has recurrence" (OC1), and "has attractor dynamics" (IS2).

4. **CD2, EC1, SR1 are the only genuinely MC-driven assumptions.** These require self_model_level >= 2 and specific MC thresholds — they are hard enough to generate that random systems don't reliably achieve them.

5. **Viability and MC-satisfaction generate different subsets.** The viable group (n=32) has 100% on OC2/OC1/CD1/IC1 but only 78.1% on EC1 and 59.4% on SR1. The MC-satisfying group (n=27) hits 100% on EC1 and SR1 but has lower OC1 (55.6%) and OC2 (92.6%).

## Recommendation

The program needs to decide which of the 6 simulator-artifact assumptions are worth keeping. If the goal is to identify what MC2+MC3+MC4 *generates*, the answer is 3/9 assumptions. If the goal is to identify the *set of properties that viable systems have* (regardless of source), then all 9 are valid — but the MCs only produce a subset of them.
