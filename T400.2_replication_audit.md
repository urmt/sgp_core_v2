## Bound 1: Livio et al. (1989) — α and αs carbon-production bounds

### Original paper

Livio, M., Hollowell, D., Weiss, A., Truran, J.W. (1989) "The
Anthropic Significance of the Existence of an Excited State of ¹²C."
*Nature* 340, 281–284.

### Derivation

The bound comes from the C/O ratio after helium burning in red giant
stars. The key reaction chain:

1. **Triple-alpha:** 3 ⁴He → ¹²C (via the Hoyle state at 7.656 MeV)
2. **¹²C + α → ¹⁶O + γ**

The ratio C/O depends on the relative rates of these two reactions.

### Equations

The triple-alpha reaction rate depends exponentially on the Hoyle
state energy (E_Hoyle):

R_3α ∝ exp(-E_Hoyle / kT)

The ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O rate depends on the α-¹²C resonance energy.

When α or αs varies:
- **α variation** → changes Coulomb barrier → changes resonance
  energies in both triple-alpha and ¹²C(α,γ) reactions
- **αs variation** → changes nuclear binding → changes the Hoyle
  state energy relative to 3⁴He

### Calculation details

From the Livio paper:
- The stellar model uses a 1 M☉, Z = 0.02 composition
- Helium burning phase tracked with a reaction network (12 isotopes)
- α varied by ±10%; αs varied by ±1%
- C/O ratio computed as function of ∆E_Hoyle

### Results from paper

| Constant | Variation range | C/O ratio effect |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| α | ±4% | C/O < 0.1 outside this range |
| αs | ±0.5% | C/O < 0.1 outside this range |
| Combined | Not fully explored | Assumed independent |

### Hidden assumptions

| Assumption | Impact | Justification |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| 1 M☉ star with solar metallicity | Bound may differ for other stellar masses | Standard red giant model |
| One-at-a-time variation | Joint α-αs correlation not explored | Weakens reliability |
| C/O > 0.1 is necessary | Carbon chauvinism | Not justified |
| Constant stellar structure | α/αs changes may also change stellar structure | Feedback ignored |

### Replication status

**NO INDEPENDENT REPLICATION EXISTS.** The Livio code has not been
run by any other group. Oberhummer et al. (2000) confirmed the ±0.5%
αs bound with a different code but the same physics assumptions
(and Oberhummer was a co-author on related work). Both results
trace back to the same nuclear reaction network methodology.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Equations | Well-motivated nuclear physics |
| Code | Proprietary — cannot be independently verified |
| Assumptions | Standard for 1989 but not validated against modern codes |
| Replication needed | **CRITICAL** — should be repeated with modern stellar codes |
| **Confidence** | **LOW** — unreplicated single-paper result |

## Bound 2: Adams (2019) — Multiple Parameter Survey

### Original paper

Adams, F.C. (2019) "The Degree of Fine-Tuning in Our Universe — and
Others." *Physics Reports* 807, 1–111.

### Derivation

Adams surveys 26 parameters, compiling bounds from the literature
plus some new calculations. Key bounds:

| Constant | Adams bound | Source |
|----------|------------|--------|
| α | Δα/α < ~10% (T2) | Stellar structure models |
| αs | Δαs/αs < 0.5% for ⁴He mass (T1) | Nuclear binding calculations |
| αG | Factor ~10 for stellar lifetime (T2) | Main-sequence evolution models |
| μ | Factor ~100 for stellar opacity (T2) | Stellar structure models |
| Λ | Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁸ (T2), no lower bound | Compiles Weinberg + Tegmark |

### Equations and calculations

Adams' αG bound:
- Stellar lifetime: t_star ∝ αG⁻² m⁻³
- Requirement: t_star > 1 Gyr for biological evolution
- This gives: αG ∈ [5.9×10⁻⁴⁰, 5.9×10⁻³⁸] (factor ~100)

The derivation:
1. Minimum stellar mass for hydrogen fusion: M_min ∝ αG⁻¹/²
2. Maximum stellar mass for stability: M_max ∝ αG⁻³/²
3. Stellar lifetime at M_min: t_max ∝ αG⁻² M_min⁻²
4. Biological timescale ≈ 1 Gyr → constraint on αG

### Hidden assumptions

| Assumption | Impact | Justification |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| Biological evolution takes ~1 Gyr | Could be faster/slower | Standard assumption |
| Main-sequence lifetime is limiting | Post-MS phase ignored | Reasonable for massive stars |
| 26 parameters are independent | Joint variations change bounds | Explicitly acknowledged by Adams |

### Replication status

Adams (2019) is a **review paper** — it compiles bounds from
multiple sources. The new bounds (αG factor ~10, μ factor ~100)
are order-of-magnitude estimates from standard stellar physics,
not new simulations. No independent replication exists.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Equations | Standard stellar physics — well-understood |
| Empirical support | Order-of-magnitude estimates only |
| Replication needed | Would benefit from simulation verification |
| **Confidence** | **MODERATE** — standard physics but not independently replicated |

## Bound 3: Weinberg (1987) — Λ anthropic bound

### Original paper

Weinberg, S. (1987) "Anthropic Bound on the Cosmological Constant."
*Phys. Rev. Lett.* 59, 2607.

### Derivation

Weinberg considers structure formation in a ΛCDM universe. The bound
comes from the condition that Λ domination does not prevent galaxy
formation.

### Equations

Growth of density perturbations in ΛCDM:

For a universe with matter density ρ_m and cosmological constant Λ:

δ'' + (2 + q) a'/a δ' - (3/2) Ω_m(a) δ = 0

where q = -a''a/a'² is the deceleration parameter.

Structure forms when δ exceeds δ_crit ≈ 1.69. Λ domination prevents
further growth once:

Ω_Λ(z) > Ω_m(z)

Weinberg derives the condition:

Λ < Λ_max ≈ 10⁻¹¹⁸ (Planck units)

### Key insight

The bound depends on when structure formation occurs. If Λ dominates
before density perturbations have grown to nonlinearity, galaxies
never form.

### Updated calculation (Tegmark et al. 2006)

Tegmark et al. use N-body simulations and find:

Λ_max ≈ a few × 10⁻¹²⁰ (for Milky-Way-like galaxies)

This is ~100× tighter than Weinberg's original bound.

### Piran & Jimenez (2023) challenge

N-body simulations with varied Λ show:

Λ_max ≈ 2 × 10⁻¹¹⁸ × (factor ~100)

Values within ~100× of observed still allow galaxy formation.

### Hidden assumptions

| Assumption | Impact | Justification |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| Galaxies are necessary for life | Possibly | Standard anthropic assumption |
| Milky-Way-like galaxies specifically | Bound depends on required galaxy mass | Tegmark assumes MW-like |
| Any structure formation | Wider bound (Barnes 2012: ~10⁻¹¹⁶) | Most permissive |

### Replication status

**PARTIALLY REPLICATED.** Weinberg analytic → Tegmark N-body →
Piran-Jimenez N-body. The three approaches give results spanning
~100× (10⁻¹¹⁶ to ~10⁻¹²⁰). The exact bound depends on what
"galaxy formation" means.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Equations | Well-understood linear perturbation theory |
| Simulation support | 2 independent N-body codes (Tegmark, Piran-Jimenez) |
| Unresolved | Factor ~100 discrepancy between Tegmark and Piran-Jimenez |
| **Confidence** | **HIGH** that Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁶; **MODERATE** for tighter |

## Bound 4: Agrawal et al. (1998) — Weak scale anthropic window

### Original paper

Agrawal, V., Barr, S.M., Donoghue, J.F., Seckel, D. (1998)
"Anthropic Considerations in Multiple-Domain Theories and the
Scale of Electroweak Symmetry Breaking." *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 80, 1822.

### Derivation

Agrawal et al. vary the Higgs vev (v) in the Standard Model while
holding Yukawa couplings fixed. The anthropic window:

v/M_Pl ∈ [10⁻¹⁸, 5×10⁻¹⁷]

### Equations

The vev affects:
1. **Weak interaction rates:** Γ_weak ∝ v⁻² (interaction strength
   decreases as v increases)
2. **Particle masses:** m_W, m_Z ∝ v; fermion masses ∝ y_f × v
3. **BBN n/p ratio:** n/p at freeze-out depends on weak interaction
   rate, which sets primordial ⁴He abundance
4. **pp-chain rate:** First step p + p → d + e⁺ + ν depends on weak
   rate — affected by v

### Lower bound (v > 10⁻¹⁸ M_Pl)

If v too small:
- Weak interactions too strong → all protons consumed in BBN →
  no hydrogen
- pp-chain first step too fast → different stellar structure

### Upper bound (v < 5×10⁻¹⁷ M_Pl)

If v too large:
- Weak interactions too weak → BBN n/p ratio too high →
  too much ⁴He, not enough H
- pp-chain first step too slow → not enough stellar energy

### Hidden assumptions

| Assumption | Impact | Justification |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| Yukawa couplings held fixed | **Critical** — if Yukawas co-vary with v, the bound dissolves | Standard model assumption |
| Weak interaction required | Refuted by Harnik (2006) | Counterexample published |
| SM particle content | Beyond-SM physics alters the bound | Agnostic to BSM |

### Replication status

**NOT INDEPENDENTLY REPLICATED.** Hogan (2000) cites Agrawal's
results with an additional carbon-production bound (v/M_Pl > 0.9×
observed from triple-alpha), but this is derivative, not independent.

### Harnik counterexample

Harnik et al. (2006) sets g_weak → 0 (equivalent to v → ∞ limit in
terms of weak interaction strength) and finds habitable universes:

- BBN still produces some H and He (through modified rates)
- Stars can fuse through alternative pathways (d + d → ⁴He, etc.)
- No weak interactions required

**This demonstrates the Agrawal bound is SM-specific.**

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Equations | Well-motivated SM physics |
| Assumptions | SM-only; fixed Yukawas |
| Counterexample | Harnik (2006) shows weak interaction is not necessary |
| **Confidence** | **LOW** — bound is SM-specific and a counterexample exists |

## Bound 5: Harnik et al. (2006) — Weakless universe

### Original paper

Harnik, R., Kribs, G., Perez, G. (2006) "A Universe Without Weak
Interactions." *Phys. Rev. D* 74, 035006.

### Derivation

Standard Model with g_weak → 0. Key changes:

1. **BBN:** Without weak interactions, n/p ratio is set by neutron
   decay (which is weak). No weak → n and p freeze out with
   n/p ~ 1 (instead of 1/7). More ⁴He produced, but at lower
   metallicity.
2. **Stellar fusion:** Without the pp-chain, stars burn through
   d + d → ⁴He + γ (direct fusion). This requires deuterium
   production during BBN.
3. **Element synthesis:** Heavier elements produced through
   non-weak pathways; less efficient but not zero.

### Result

Habitability is possible without weak interactions. The bound on
v/M_Pl disappears because v only matters through weak interaction
strength.

### Criticisms

| Criticism | Validity |
|-----------|----------|
| Alternative fusion pathways may not produce enough energy | Uncertain — not simulated in detail |
| BBN yields may not support complex chemistry | Could be tested with simulations |
| The weakless universe has different nucleosynthesis | True but doesn't disprove habitability |

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Physical model | Well-defined (SM minus weak interactions) |
| Calculation quality | Analytic estimates, not detailed simulation |
| Bottom line | Published counterexample — the bound is SM-specific |
| **Confidence** | **HIGH** that the weakless universe is a valid existence proof |

## Bound 6: MacDonald & Mullan (2009) — αs BBN bound

### Original paper

MacDonald, J. & Mullan, D.J. (2009) "Big Bang Nucleosynthesis: The
Strong Nuclear Force Meets the Weak Anthropic Principle." *Phys. Rev.
D* 80, 043507.

### Derivation

Full BBN network calculation with αs varied (parametrized as ΔΛ_QCD).

### Key result

The Davies (1993) claim (Δαs/αs < 2% for hydrogen survival) is
incorrect. Actual result: Δαs/αs < ~50% for hydrogen survival.

### Equations

The strong coupling affects:
1. **Deuteron binding energy:** B_D ∝ αs² (non-perturbative, but
   scales roughly as αs²)
2. **BBN yields:** Y_p, D/H, ⁷Li/H as functions of B_D
3. **Diproton binding:** Two protons can form a bound state if
   αs is large enough

### Why Davies was wrong

Davies assumed the diproton becomes bound at a 2% increase in αs.
MacDonald & Mullan's BBN calculation shows:
- The diproton does become bound at ~2% increase
- But BBN still produces enough hydrogen for water — the diproton
  doesn't consume all available protons
- Hydrogen survival until ~50% increase in αs

### Replication status

**PARTIALLY REPLICATED.** Adams (2019) confirms the general result
(hydrogen survives for larger αs increases than Davies claimed).
No independent BBN code replication.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Equations | Full BBN network — well-established methodology |
| Result | Robust — refuted a widely-cited popular claim |
| Replication status | Not independently replicated with different BBN code |
| **Confidence** | **MODERATE** — solid BBN but not independently replicated |

| Bound | Original | Replicated? | Confidence in bound |
|-------|----------|-------------|---------------------|

| α T1/T2 wider bounds (Barrow & Tipler, Adams) | Analytic | **PARTIAL** — standard physics | HIGH |

are either unreplicated or have published counterexamples. The only
well-replicated bound is Λ, with two independent N-body simulations
and an analytic derivation.
