# arXiv Submission Abstracts

## Paper 1: Empirical Spectral Geometry of MEC Covariance Structure

**Categories:** physics.data-an, q-bio.NC

**Abstract:**
We characterize the spectral geometry of covariance matrices from rodent medial entorhinal cortex (MEC) recordings during spatial navigation. Across 21 MEC sessions and 22 synthetic dynamical conditions, we find a reproducible separation in the space defined by spectral decay rate α and participation ratio PR. MEC covariance spectra follow slow exponential decay (α = 0.039 ± 0.018, PR = 37 ± 20), while synthetic stabilized systems collapse to α > 0.5, PR < 3. A geometric-series reference curve provides a baseline: MEC spectra are systematically more concentrated than this reference, indicating richer structure than simple exponential decay. Preprocessing-matched null ensembles (smoothed Poisson, circularly shuffled, sparse) fail to reproduce MEC covariance geometry under Mahalanobis distance with permutation testing (D_M > 3σ, p < 0.001 for all nulls). The broad-spectrum organization resides in instantaneous correlation geometry rather than temporal propagation. These results establish a reproducible empirical characterization of MEC covariance structure and demonstrate that the observed spectral geometry is robust to preprocessing-matched null construction.

## Paper 2: Finite-Size Sensitivity of Spectral Comparison in Neural Covariance Data

**Categories:** physics.data-an, q-bio.NC

**Abstract:**
Comparing the spectral properties of neural covariance data against random matrix ensembles requires careful attention to finite-size effects and null-model selection. We analyze 21 MEC recordings and find that at fixed N ≈ 120, eigenvector statistics match a sparse Erdős–Rényi ensemble (d = 0.86σ), suggesting possible finite-size coincidence. Subsampling scaling analysis reveals that the MEC/sparse ensemble distance grows as d(N) ~ N^{2.2 ± 0.1}, strongly rejecting convergence at larger system sizes. We compare four null ensembles (GOE, shifted Wigner, sparse ER, Ising criticality) and show that eigenvalue and eigenvector statistics can give contradictory ensemble distances. The instantaneous precision matrix (α ≈ 0.33, PR ≈ 98) carries richer spectral structure than temporal propagation operators (α ≈ 0.01). These results establish methodological requirements for spectral comparison in neural covariance analysis and demonstrate that finite-size effects can produce misleading ensemble agreement at experimentally accessible dimensions.

## Keywords

MEC, covariance geometry, spectral analysis, participation ratio, random matrix theory, finite-size scaling, null models, neural dynamics, preprocessing controls
