## References

### 1. Fields (2011)

**"If physics is an information science, what is an observer?"**

arXiv:1108.4865

**Relevance:**

- Observer as physical system
- Interaction and observation

**Relationship to RD:**

Fields proposes that observers are physical systems that extract information from their environment through interaction. This aligns with RD's finding that interaction is present at every level of the compositional ladder, and that explanatory power migrates into relationships among descriptions rather than isolated objects.

---

### 2. Knuth (2014)

**"Information-based physics"**

arXiv:1310.1667

**Relevance:**

- Observer-centered reconstruction of physics

**Relationship to RD:**

Knuth reconstructs physics from information-theoretic foundations, placing the observer at the center. This resonates with RD's discovery that the observer itself became a hidden fixed variable (SR-32), and that observer independence may be a stronger criterion than observer diversity.

---

### 3. Brukner (2018)

**"A no-go theorem for observer-independent facts"**

**Relevance:**

- Observer dependence
- Quantum observers

**Relationship to RD:**

Brukner demonstrates that observer-independent facts may not exist in quantum mechanics. This parallels RD's finding that explanatory power repeatedly migrates into structures previously held fixed, and that the observer itself may be the deepest fixed variable.

---

### 4. Butterfield (2011)

**"Less is Different: Emergence and Reduction Reconciled"**

**Relevance:**

- Reduction vs emergence

**Relationship to RD:**

Butterfield argues that emergence and reduction are compatible rather than contradictory. This connects to RD's open question of whether explanatory reductionism is sufficient for persistent multi-scale systems, and whether ontological reductionism differs from explanatory reductionism.

---

### 5. Palacios (2022)

**"Emergence and Reduction in Physics"**

**Relevance:**

- Formal definitions of reduction

**Relationship to RD:**

Palacios provides formal definitions of reduction in physics. This is directly relevant to RD's investigation of whether lower-level descriptions are sufficient explanations for higher-level persistence, and whether explanatory sufficiency differs from ontological reducibility.

---

### 6. Abbott (2006)

**"Emergence Explained"**

arXiv:cs/0602045

**Relevance:**

- Explanatory power from multiple descriptions

**Relationship to RD:**

Abbott argues that emergence arises from explanatory gaps between levels of description. This aligns with RD's finding that multiple descriptions often reveal explanatory structure that single descriptions do not, and that explanatory power repeatedly migrates into relationships among descriptions.

---

### 7. Fields (2018)

**"Sciences of Observation"**

**Relevance:**

- Observer-relative science

**Relationship to RD:**

Fields develops a framework for observer-relative science. This connects to RD's discovery that the observer itself became a hidden fixed variable, and that observer invariance may be a stronger criterion than observer diversity.

---

### 8. Chibbaro, Rondoni & Vulpiani (2014)

**"Reductionism, Emergence and Levels of Reality"**

**Relevance:**

- Cross-scale explanatory structure

**Relationship to RD:**

This work examines how explanatory structure spans multiple levels of reality. This is directly relevant to RD's finding that higher levels continue depending on lower levels after emergence, and that cross-scale interactions may be necessary for persistence.
