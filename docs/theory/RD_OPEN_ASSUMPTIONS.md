## Assumption A: Experience Is Present From the Beginning

**Statement:** Experience is present from the beginning. It does not emerge at some threshold of complexity.

**Status:** UNDER TEST

**Implication:** If true, the research question is not "how does experience arise?" but "what structures maximize coherent, fertile experience?"

---

## Assumption B: Interaction ≈ Observation ≈ Primitive Experience

**Statement:** Interaction, observation, and primitive experience are three descriptions of the same event.

**Status:** UNDER TEST

**Implication:** Any system that interacts is, at the instant of interaction, observing. The intrinsic side of that interaction is primitive experience.

---

## Assumption C: Coherence and Fertility Jointly Influence Experienced Reality

**Statement:** Experienced reality is jointly shaped by coherence (how well parts fit together) and fertility (how much further experience the structure enables).

**Status:** UNDER TEST

**Possible symbolic compass:**

> Ψ ≈ f(C, F, I)

where:

- Ψ = experienced value
- C = coherence
- F = fertility
- I = interaction

No functional form established.

---

## Assumption D: Reflection Is Experience Modeling Experience

**Statement:** Reflection occurs when experience turns back on itself and models its own structure.

**Status:** UNDER TEST

**Implication:** Higher-order cognition may be recursive reflection — experience modeling experience modeling experience.

---

## Assumption E: Complexity Increases Through Persistent Fertile Interaction

**Statement:** Complexity increases when persistent structures enable further coherent experience, which in turn creates new persistent structures.

**Status:** UNDER TEST

**Implication:** The universe may become more complex not by accident but because fertility is self-amplifying.
