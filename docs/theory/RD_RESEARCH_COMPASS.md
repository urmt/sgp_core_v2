# RD Research Compass

## Purpose

Prevent theory drift.

This document records:

- what question the project is asking
- what has been learned
- what remains unknown
- what directions are justified

---

## Original Question

The project is NOT primarily asking:

> "What fundamentally exists?"

The project is asking:

> "What can persist in sentient reality, and why?"

Operational form:

> "Which explanatory structures survive changes in observer, representation, decomposition, and scale?"

---

## Sentience Position

Sentience is treated as self-evident first-person reality.

The project does NOT attempt to prove sentience.

Instead it investigates:

> "What structures can persist within sentient reality?"

---

## Working Hypotheses (UNDER TEST)

**H1:** Interaction may constitute minimal observation.

**H2:** Observation may possess an intrinsic aspect ("experience" or "feeling").

**H3:** Complex sentience may emerge from recursive layers of observation.

No proof claimed.

## Research Compass

Before launching any study ask:

1. Does it reduce observer bias?
2. Does it introduce independent observers?
3. Does it test decomposition sensitivity?
4. Does it distinguish interaction from observation?
5. Does it distinguish observation from experience?
6. Does it test cross-scale persistence?
7. Does it preserve falsifiability?

If NO to all:

Do not run the study.

---

## Current Blockers

No new survivor promotion until:

- independent observers exist
- missing artifacts are recovered
- observer dependence is measured
- decomposition independence is externally tested

---

## Frozen Claims

NOT proven:

- interaction is fundamental
- persistence is fundamental
- observer invariance is fundamental
- experience is fundamental
- reductionism fails

All remain under test.

---

## Compass Lock

The project studies what structures remain stable as perspectives change.

Working intuition:

> "Reality may be generated through interacting observers, and persistence may be what survives those interactions."

This is a compass.

Not a conclusion.
