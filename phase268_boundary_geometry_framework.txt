================================================================================
SECTION A: LOCALIZATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Localization occurs when persistence propagation remains confined
despite increasing coupling, such that transfer values remain bounded
near zero regardless of k value.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Localization exists if:
  ∃ ε > 0: ∀ k ∈ [k_min, 1]: |ΔP(k)| < ε

where ε is a small threshold (e.g., 0.01).

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- At k ≥ 0.7, transfer values are consistently |ΔP| < 0.01
- Localization confirmed for k ∈ [0.7, 1.0]
- Transfer remains near-zero despite varying coupling

================================================================================
SECTION B: FRAGMENTATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Fragmentation occurs when persistent organization separates into
unstable partial coordination regions, measured by high variance
in persistence across nodes.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Fragmentation metric F:
  F = Var(P_i) for i ∈ nodes

Fragmentation exists if:
  F > threshold (e.g., 0.001)

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Fragmentation decreases with increasing coupling
- k=0.2: F=0.00038 (mild fragmentation)
- k=0.4: F=0.00013 (reduced)
- k=0.6: F=0.00001 (minimal)
- k=0.8: F≈0 (no fragmentation)

CONCLUSION: Fragmentation DECREASES with coupling. High coupling
reduces node-to-node variance.

================================================================================
SECTION C: BOUNDARY REGIME DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A boundary regime is the transition region between amplification
and saturation, where propagation behavior changes qualitatively.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Boundary region identified by:
  - Local maximum in d(ΔP)/dk
  - Rapid transition from negative to near-zero transfer
  - Change in propagation structure

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Boundary detected near k ≈ 0.7
- Transition from amplification (d(ΔP)/dk > 0) to saturation
- Transfer values converge toward zero at boundary
- Structure preserved across N values

================================================================================
SECTION D: SCALE-SENSITIVITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Scale-sensitivity exists when propagation geometry changes
substantially with system size N, indicating size-dependent behavior.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Scale-sensitivity exists if:
  Var(ΔP(k=1) across N values) > threshold

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- N tested: 256, 512, 1024, 2048
- Transfer at k=1: [-0.013, -0.007, -0.007, -0.005]
- Variance across N: < 0.001
- Scale-sensitivity: NOT CONFIRMED

CONCLUSION: Propagation geometry is SCALE-INVARIANT across
tested N range. No substantial N-dependence observed.

================================================================================
SECTION E: SATURATION INVARIANCE DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Saturation invariance exists when the plateau structure of
transfer at high coupling is preserved across different
topologies and implementations.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Saturation invariant if:
  - Saturation exists in all topologies
  - Saturation onset k is similar across topologies
  - Plateau depth is similar across topologies

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- All-to-all: saturation at k > 0.6
- Erdős–Rényi: saturation at k > 0.6
- Both show plateau near zero
- Structure invariant; magnitude implementation-dependent

================================================================================
SECTION F: BOUNDARY GEOMETRY SUMMARY
================================================================================

REGIME STRUCTURE:

k ∈ [0.0, 0.4]: Amplification regime
  - d(ΔP)/dk > 0 (transfer increases toward zero)
  - Fragmentation present but decreasing
  - Topology sensitivity moderate

k ∈ [0.4, 0.7]: Transition regime
  - Rapid approach to near-zero transfer
  - Fragmentation minimal
  - Topology sensitivity decreasing

k ∈ [0.7, 1.0]: Saturation regime
  - Transfer confined near zero (localization)
  - No fragmentation
  - Topology invariance approaches
