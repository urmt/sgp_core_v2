## Conclusion

**T082 IS OVERTURNED. T086 IS CORRECT. Persistence is irrelevant for structural realization.**

## Source of Disagreement (Precisely Identified)

The disagreement is **entirely in the none-group definition** — not in scoring, measurement, thresholds, random seeds, or any other methodological component.

### T082's Grouping (Flawed)

T082's `run_assay` defines the none-group as the **complement of all**:
```
none = ALL constraints simultaneously below threshold
```

For the SP phase (MC2≥0.5, MC3≥0.5, MC4≥0.5, SP≥0.5):
```
none = ALL FOUR < 0.5
```

This requires finding systems simultaneously low on MC2, MC3, MC4, AND SP. Among 240 systems, **zero** satisfy this. The none-group is empty (n_none=0).

**Consequence**: `delta = all_mean - 0 = all_mean`. Every assumption with mean >= 0.2 in the all-group "emerges." But this delta measures the all-group's absolute scores, not the incremental contribution of SP.

### T086's Grouping (Correct)

T086 defines the none-group by **holding the base constant and only varying persistence**:
```
all:  MC2≥0.5 AND MC3≥0.5 AND MC4≥0.5 AND SP≥0.5
none: MC2≥0.5 AND MC3≥0.5 AND MC4≥0.5 AND SP<0.5
```

Both groups share MC2+MC3+MC4≥0.5. Delta isolates SP.

### The Contamination Mechanism

T082's ablation proves the point. From T082's own output:
- Removing SP from the best constellation: assumptions drop from 9→5 (MC2+MC3+MC4 alone contribute 5)
- The 4 "extra" assumptions in the SP phase are NOT generated by SP — they are already present in systems with high MC2+MC3+MC4

Proof: T086's marginal analysis compares MC2+MC3+MC4 systems with vs. without SP:
- SP group mean for OC2: 0.792
- No-SP group mean for OC2: 0.925
- Marginal delta: **−0.134** (SP HARMS OC2)

The T082 SP phase showed OC2 delta = +0.875 (emerged). That +0.875 is entirely the contribution of MC2+MC3+MC4, attributed to SP by the empty none-group artifact.

## Independent Adjudication Results

### 5a: Raw Population Means

Compare three groups directly (no grouping deltas, just raw means):

| Group | Definition | n |
|-------|-----------|---|
| A (SP) | MC2+MC3+MC4≥0.5 + SP≥0.5 | 5 |
| B (No SP) | MC2+MC3+MC4≥0.5 + SP<0.5 | 17 |
| C (All) | MC2+MC3+MC4≥0.5 (regardless of SP) | 22 |

**A−B Δ** (marginal effect of SP): Only OC1 exceeds +0.2. CD1=+0.180 (borderline). All others ≤+0.114 or negative.

**A−C Δ** (selection effect): Multiple assumptions show positive deltas (OC1=+0.169, CD1=+0.139, IC1=+0.088). This is because the SP group is a non-random subset of the base group — SP systems happen to score higher on some MC components too.

### 5b: Random Subsample Test

If SP marks genuinely generative systems, the SP group (n=5) should be systematically different from random draws of 5 systems from the base group (n=22).

**3/9 assumptions** outside the 95% CI of 1000 random draws — below the 5/9 threshold. The SP group is consistent with a random draw.

OC1 and CD1 are the two assumptions above CI (higher than random chance). IS1 is below CI (lower than random). This confirms the marginal analysis: SP weakly boosts OC1/CD1 but harms IS1.

### 5c: Score Confound Check

The SP group has HIGHER MC3 scores (+0.116) than the no-SP base group but LOWER MC2 scores (−0.131). MC4 scores are similar (+0.040). The SP group's mixed MC profile means any apparent emergence benefit is a selection artifact. SP tags systems with a particular MC balance, but does not independently generate structure.

## What Persistence Actually Does

Persistence is not irrelevant in all senses — it changes **which systems are selected**, not **what is generated**:

1. **Selection mechanism**: Systems with SP≥0.5 are a specific subset of MC2+MC3+MC4≥0.5 systems. They tend to have higher MC3 and lower MC2 scores.

2. **Negative effect**: Persistence systematically harms IS1 (internal sensitivity) across all 6 types. This confirms the T075 finding that stability and generativity are structurally opposed.

3. **Weak positive (OC1)**: Only OC1 shows consistent marginal benefit from persistence (~+0.22). This is theoretically consistent — persistence says "revisit states," and OC1 measures whether revisited states are diverse (1−recurrence). This is a thin edge: persistence generates recurrence, but OC1 measures its complement.

4. **MC5 and MC1 are not equivalent**: MC1's non-empty but contaminated none-group suggests MC1 has some genuine marginal effect, but the comparison is still confounded by low-MC scores in the none-group. A proper marginal analysis of MC1 would be needed to isolate its contribution.
