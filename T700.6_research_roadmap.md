## 3. Root Cause of the Outcome

Every prediction fails for the same structural reason:

> SFH-SGP predictions take the form C = Measured/Baseline where Baseline is asserted, not derived from SFH-SGP axioms.

This creates a universal vulnerability across all domains:

| Audit | Vulnerability |
|-------|--------------|
| **T700.3 Novelty** | Since the threshold is not derived from SFH-SGP mathematics, the prediction cannot be distinguished from a relabeling of standard measurements |
| **T700.4 Falsifiability** | Since the baseline is adjustable, the prediction can always be made to fit any measurement |
| **T700.5 Feasibility** | Even when experiments are feasible, they test standard science, not SFH-SGP |

The fiber bundle framework (Π: P → B) is presented as the mathematical basis of SFH-SGP but is never used to derive a single prediction threshold. The 18 predictions exist independently of the 6 mathematical formulas in the book (fₜ, gₛ, C definitions, fiber bundle definitions).

**This is the core finding of T700:** SFH-SGP contains mathematical machinery and empirical predictions, but the two are disconnected. The predictions could be deleted from the book, and the mathematics would remain unchanged. The mathematics could be deleted, and the predictions would remain unchanged.

---

## 4. What This Means for SFH-SGP

SFH-SGP is currently:

> An **interpretive framework** that applies a single metric (coherence ratio C) across multiple domains and interprets measured values above an asserted baseline as evidence of sentient field optimization.

It is not currently:

> A **predictive scientific theory** that derives specific, novel, falsifiable consequences from its axioms.

This does not mean SFH-SGP is wrong. It means SFH-SGP is currently not operating as a scientific theory in the Popperian sense. It can become one — but only if it produces predictions that satisfy all three criteria (novelty, falsifiability, quantitative specificity) from its own mathematical axioms.

## 5. Future Directions (T800 Candidates)

If the goal is to move SFH-SGP from interpretive framework to predictive theory, the following research directions emerge:

### Direction A: Derive Cross-Scale Correlations from SFH-SGP Axioms

**Status:** Highest-potential candidate from T700.

**Current state:** The concept is novel (no standard theory predicts correlations across quantum, neural, and cosmic scales). The book asserts R ≥ 0.85 based on Rmat = 0.6.

**What T800 would need to do:** Derive the expected correlation threshold from the fiber bundle parameters. If the structure group G for cross-scale systems is SU(2) × SO(N) × SO(3,1), does this imply a specific correlation coefficient? Can the projection map Π: P → B be used to compute an expected R?

**Success criterion:** A derived (not asserted) correlation threshold that can be tested against existing multi-scale datasets.

**Failure criterion:** The correlation threshold cannot be derived from SFH-SGP axioms and must remain asserted.

---

### Direction B: Derive the Toponium Lifetime Deviation from SFH-SGP Axioms

**Status:** Technically strongest candidate but pragmatically distant.

**Current state:** Toponium lifetime deviation Δτ ≥ 10⁻²⁶ s could, in principle, be falsified. But toponium requires future colliders.

**What T800 would need to do:** Derive Δτ from the fiber bundle parameters for quark systems. The book assigns G = SU(3) for QCD systems — can this structure group predict a specific lifetime modification?

**Success criterion:** A derived deviation value that differs from QCD prediction by a calculable amount.

**Failure criterion:** The deviation value cannot be derived; or toponium is found not to form; or QCD already accommodates the deviation.

---

### Direction C: Formalize the Coherence Metric

**Status:** Foundational prerequisite for either Direction A or B.

**Current state:** The coherence metric C has two inconsistent definitions in the book (ratio-of-means vs ratio-of-sums). Materialist baselines are asserted. The relationship between C and fiber bundle parameters is undefined.

**What T800 would need to do:** Resolve the definitional inconsistency. Show how C follows from the fiber bundle structure. Derive baselines from the theory, not from assertion.

**Success criterion:** A single, consistent definition of C with a mathematical link to Π: P → B.

**Failure criterion:** No consistent definition is possible; or C remains independent of the fiber bundle framework.

---

### Direction D: Accept Interpretive Status

SFH-SGP is not a predictive theory. It is an interpretive framework. This is not a scientific failure — many valuable frameworks (Darwinian evolution before Mendel, plate tectonics before continental drift mechanisms) began as interpretive and became predictive only after mathematical formalization.

If this direction is chosen, SFH-SGP would be presented as:

> A framework for measuring and interpreting coherence across scales, using a unified metric (C), with the hypothesis that coherence optimization reflects a fundamental organizing principle.

This is a defensible position, provided it is not claimed to be a predictive or falsifiable theory.
