================================================================================
SECTION A: PERSISTENCE TOPOLOGY CLASS DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A persistence topology class is a family of network structures that
produce characteristic propagation geometry patterns, classified by
their transfer behavior across coupling range.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Let T(topo, k) be transfer as function of topology and coupling.
Let G(topo) be the geometry class of topology.

Two topologies belong to same class if:
  |T(topo_1, k) - T(topo_2, k)| < ε for all k ∈ [0, 1]

where ε is classification threshold.

OBSERVED TOPOLOGY CLASSES:

1. Fully-connected class: all-to-all
2. Random sparse class: Erdős–Rényi
3. Short-path class: small-world
4. Heterogeneous degree class: scale-free
5. Clustered class: modular

================================================================================
SECTION B: AMPLIFICATION EFFICIENCY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Amplification efficiency measures transfer gain per unit increase
in coupling, indicating how effectively topology propagates persistence.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

AE = mean(dT/dk) for k ∈ [0, 0.6]

Higher AE indicates:
  - Faster approach to saturation
  - More efficient propagation
  - Stronger coupling response

OBSERVED VALUES:

all-to-all:     AE = 0.028
erdos-renyi:    AE = 0.000
small-world:    AE = 0.013
scale-free:     AE = 0.016
modular:        AE = 0.024

RANKING: all-to-all > modular > scale-free > small-world > erdos-renyi

================================================================================
SECTION C: SATURATION BOUNDARY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Saturation boundary is the coupling region where propagation plateaus,
indicating the onset of localized persistence confinement.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Saturation at k_s if:
  Var(T(k > k_s)) < ε for ε = 0.001

OBSERVED: All topologies show saturation at k ≈ 0.6
UNIFORMITY: Saturation boundary is TOPOLOGY-INVARIANT

================================================================================
SECTION D: LOCALIZATION STABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Localization stability measures the robustness of persistence
confinement at high coupling, indicating how well transfer
remains bounded near zero.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

L = |T(k=1.0)|

Lower L indicates stronger localization (more stable).
Higher L indicates weaker localization (transfer escapes).

OBSERVED VALUES:

all-to-all:     L = 0.006 (strong localization)
erdos-renyi:    L = 0.039 (weak localization)
small-world:    L = 0.000 (perfect localization)
scale-free:     L = 0.020 (moderate localization)
modular:        L = 0.020 (moderate localization)

================================================================================
SECTION E: PROPAGATION STABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Propagation stability measures the sensitivity of transfer geometry
to topology perturbation, indicating consistency across conditions.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

PS = 1 / (std(T(k)) + ε)

Higher PS indicates more stable propagation geometry.
Lower PS indicates higher variance across coupling range.

OBSERVED VALUES:

all-to-all:     PS = 44.0
erdos-renyi:    PS = 68.0
small-world:    PS = 58.8
scale-free:     PS = 42.3
modular:        PS = 30.1
