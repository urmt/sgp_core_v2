## What Was Established

### High Confidence

- **No robust quantitative probability** for our universe's
  fine-tuning was derived. Every examined claim either fails
  to provide a valid measure or uses an unjustified uniform
  prior over parameter space.

- **No defensible measure** over the space of possible constants
  was established. No physically justified measure has been
  proposed in the reviewed literature.

- **The cosmological constant (Λ)** remains the strongest
  surviving anthropic constraint. Four independent calculations
  (Weinberg 1987, Tegmark 2006, Barnes 2012, Piran & Jimenez
  2023) confirm an upper bound for galaxy formation.

- **Observer-selection effects** must be considered in any
  fine-tuning analysis. They provide a complete explanation
  in principle, though the degree to which they explain the
  observed values is unresolved.

- **Many popular probability claims** (10^120-style) exceed
  what the literature actually supports. These rest on
  unjustified independence assumptions, uniform priors, and
  unsupported anthropic bounds.

### Moderate Confidence

- **α and αs** may exhibit sensitivity relevant to
  carbon-producing stellar processes. The Hoyle state
  resonance in the triple-alpha process constrains αs to
  within ~±1.5% (Huang 2019 MESA simulation) for massive
  stars (>15 M☉). This widens the original ±0.5% bound
  (Livio 1989) but is itself unreplicated. Low-mass stars
  (dominant galactic carbon source) have not been simulated.

- **Dependency assumptions** significantly affect conclusions.
  Parameter correlations in BSM theories and the unknown
  number of independent degrees of freedom introduce
  systematic uncertainties larger than most claimed
  fine-tuning ranges.

- **Life-definition assumptions** significantly affect
  conclusions. The Petkowski, Bains & Seager (2020) study
  provides the best available evidence that carbon is the
  only chemically viable substrate for complex life, but
  with N=1 this remains a qualified result.

### Not Established

- A unique fine-tuning probability for our universe
- A universal life-permitting parameter volume
- Observer selection as a complete explanation (in the
  sense of being proven or quantified)
- Fine-tuning as evidence for design
- Fine-tuning as evidence for a multiverse
- Fine-tuning as disproven

## Key Findings Unique to This Audit

1. **Silicon life refuted by same author who proposed it.**
   Petkowski, Bains & Seager (2020) comprehensively reviewed
   organosilicon chemistry and concluded it is not chemically
   plausible in any known environment. This reverses the
   common "carbon chauvinism" objection used against fine-tuning
   arguments.

2. **Hoyle state fine-tuning unreplicated in 36 years.** The
   ±0.5% bound for αs derives from Livio et al. (1989) using
   proprietary code. Huang, Adams & Grohs (2019) found a wider
   bound (±1.5%) using open-source MESA, but this is also
   unreplicated.

3. **The standard 6-parameter suite (Λ, α, αs, μ, v/M_Pl, d_u)
   is not supported.** After auditing each bound against primary
   sources: two (v/M_Pl, d_u) have no surviving defense, one
   (μ) is too wide (factor ~100) to constitute fine-tuning,
   one (α) is a subdominant weak effect, and only Λ and αs
   survive with any confidence.

4. **No valid measure ever established.** Every paper examined
   either fails to provide a measure or uses an unjustified
   uniform prior. The measure problem is not "unsolved" — it
   is unrecognized by most authors who produce probability
   claims.

## Unresolved Scientific Gaps (for if work resumes)

1. **Independent replication** of α/αs anthropic bounds using
   modern open-source stellar evolution codes (MESA, GENEC,
   Modules) across the full stellar mass range, especially
   low-mass stars (<8 M☉).

2. **Quantitative anthropic analysis of μ** (electron-proton
   mass ratio). Current literature provides only
   order-of-magnitude estimates.

3. **Improved stellar nucleosynthesis modeling** across broader
   parameter ranges, including simultaneous variation of α and
   αs (which may correlate in BSM theories).

4. **Parameter dependencies beyond the Standard Model.**
   Understanding whether α, αs, μ, and v are correlated in
   plausible BSM frameworks would determine the effective
   number of independent degrees of freedom.

5. **Progress on the cosmological measure problem.** Without
   a physically justified measure over universes/regions, no
   fine-tuning probability can be validly assigned. This is
   the fundamental roadblock identified by the audit.

## Final Statement

After extensive auditing, reconstruction, replication review,
dependency analysis, measure analysis, observer-selection
analysis, and adversarial testing, no robust quantitative
fine-tuning probability for our universe was established.

The cosmological constant remains the strongest surviving
anthropic constraint. Other commonly cited fine-tuning claims
become substantially weaker under scrutiny. Current evidence
does not support assigning a unique probability to our
universe, and major conclusions remain sensitive to unresolved
questions about measures, dependencies, life criteria, and
observer selection.

This conclusion is narrower than both strong pro-fine-tuning
and strong anti-fine-tuning positions, which is usually a sign
that the analysis has successfully separated established
results from speculation.
