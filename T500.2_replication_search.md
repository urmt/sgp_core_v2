# T500.2 — Replication Search

## Mission

For every surviving bound, locate the original source, all independent
replications, and all published criticisms. Classify as Replicated,
Partially Replicated, Disputed, or Not Replicated.

No secondary citations. Primary literature only.

## Bound 1: Weinberg Λ bound (1987)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | Weinberg, S. (1987) "Anthropic Bound on the Cosmological Constant." *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 59, 2607. |
| **Method** | Analytic — linear perturbation theory in ΛCDM |
| **Result** | Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁸ (Planck units) for galaxy formation |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| Tegmark et al. (2006) *Phys. Rev. D* 73, 023505 | N-body simulation | Λ < ~10⁻¹²⁰ for MW-like galaxies (~100× tighter) | **PARTIAL — different methodology gives different bound** |
| Barnes (2012) *Publ. Astron. Soc. Australia* 29, 529–564 | Analytic (review) | Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁶ for any structure (~100× wider) | **PARTIAL — analytic, not independent calculation** |
| Piran & Jimenez (2023) arXiv:2308.12017 | N-body simulation | Λ varies by factor ~100 still allows galaxies | **PARTIAL — disagrees with Tegmark bound** |

### Critique

| Criticism | Source | Severity |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Bound depends on definition of "galaxies" and what counts as sufficient structure for life | Piran & Jimenez (2023) | MODERATE |
| Three calculations span ~10⁴× in bound value | This analysis | HIGH |
| Lower bound does not exist (smaller Λ → more structure) | Universal agreement | N/A — not a criticism |

### Classification: **PARTIALLY REPLICATED**

The existence of an upper bound is universally confirmed. The exact
value varies by ~10⁴× across three independent calculations.

## Bound 2: Livio α Tier 3 bound (1989)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | Livio, M., Hollowell, D., Weiss, A., Truran, J.W. (1989) *Nature* 340, 281–284 |
| **Method** | Stellar nucleosynthesis reaction network (proprietary code, 12-isotope network, 1 M☉, solar metallicity) |
| **Result** | Δα/α ±4% for C/O > 0.1; Δαs/αs ±0.5% for C/O > 0.1 |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| None | — | — | **NOT REPLICATED** |

### Derivative works (not independent)

| Source | Relationship | Why not independent |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Oberhummer et al. (2000) *Science* 289, 88–90 | Confirms αs ±0.5% using microscopic nuclear model | Uses Livio's stellar evolution model; Csótó & Oberhummer co-author overlap; same physics assumptions |
| Schlattl et al. (2004) *Astrophysics and Space Science* 291, 27–32 | Explores C/O ratio sensitivity | Same group; derivative methodology |

### Critique

| Criticism | Source | Severity |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| 1 M☉, solar-metallicity model may not generalize | Uzan (2011) *Living Rev. Relativity* 14, 2 | LOW |
| α-αs joint variation not explored | Livio et al. (1989) themselves — acknowledged limitation | MODERATE |
| C/O > 0.1 as life criterion is arbitrary | Stenger (2011) | HIGH |
| Hoyle state fine-tuning pre-dated anthropic framing | Kragh (2010) *Physics in Perspective* | LOW — historical, not physical |

### Classification: **NOT REPLICATED**

No independent research group has run an independent stellar code with
varied α and reproduced the ±4% bound. Every citation traces back to
Livio's 1989 code.

## Bound 3: Livio αs Tier 3 bound (1989) / Oberhummer αs bound (2000)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | Livio et al. (1989) *Nature* 340, 281–284; Oberhummer, Csótó, Schlattl (2000) *Science* 289, 88–90 |
| **Method** | Same stellar code (Livio); microscopic nuclear model + stellar code (Oberhummer) |
| **Result** | Δαs/αs ±0.5% for C/O > 0.1 |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| None | — | — | **NOT REPLICATED** |
| Ekström et al. (2010) *A&A* 514, A62 | Microscopic model for Hoyle state + Pop III stellar evolution | Derives sensitivity to nucleon-nucleon potential, not directly αs bound | **PARTIAL — different question** |

### Ekström et al. (2010) analysis

This is the closest to an independent replication. Key result:
- Uses a microscopic cluster model for ⁸Be and ¹²C (not Livio's code)
- Follows 15 and 60 M☉ Population III stars (not 1 M☉ solar-metallicity)
- Derives limits on variation of the nuclear interaction strength
- Result: "The triple-α process is extremely sensitive to any such
  variations" — consistent direction but not a direct replication of
  the ±0.5% number

**Why this is not a full replication:**
1. Pop III stars (zero metallicity) vs. solar-metallicity
2. Derives sensitivity to the nucleon-nucleon potential, not to αs
   directly
3. The mapping from N-N potential variation to αs variation is
   model-dependent

### Critique

| Criticism | Source | Severity |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| MacDonald & Mullan (2009) refute the diproton catastrophe claim (different bound) | *Phys. Rev. D* 80, 043507 | MODERATE — this refutes Davies, not Livio |
| Ekström et al. (2010) do not confirm specific ±0.5% | This analysis | HIGH |

### Classification: **NOT REPLICATED** for the specific ±0.5% value.
**PARTIALLY REPLICATED** in the sense that the triple-α sensitivity
to nuclear force parameters is confirmed by Ekström et al. (2010).

## Bound 4: MacDonald & Mullan αs BBN bound (2009)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | MacDonald, J. & Mullan, D.J. (2009) *Phys. Rev. D* 80, 043507 |
| **Method** | Full BBN network calculation with varied αs (parametrized as ΔΛ_QCD) |
| **Result** | Δαs/αs < ~50% for hydrogen survival |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| None | — | — | **NOT REPLICATED** |

### Derivative

| Source | Relationship |
|--------|-------------|
| Adams (2019) *Physics Reports* 807, 1–111 | Cites MacDonald & Mullan as definitive refutation of Davies; no independent calculation |

### Citations (evidence of influence)

The paper has been cited 13 times (APS citation count) — modest
citation count indicates limited independent follow-up.

### Classification: **NOT REPLICATED** but physics is standard BBN.

The BBN network methodology is well-established. The result is
plausible but has not been independently verified with a different
BBN code.

## Bound 6: Harnik weakless universe (2006)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | Harnik, R., Kribs, G., Perez, G. (2006) *Phys. Rev. D* 74, 035006 |
| **Method** | SM with g_weak → 0; analytic estimates of BBN and stellar fusion |
| **Result** | Weakless universe is habitable — v/M_Pl is not constrained |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| None | — | — | **NOT INDEPENDENTLY REPLICATED** |

### Critique

| Criticism | Source | Severity |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Alternative fusion pathways may not sustain long-term stellar burning | Question noted by Harnik et al. | MODERATE |
| BBN yields in weakless universe not simulated in detail | Harnik et al. acknowledge | LOW |
| Weakless universe requires adjusting multiple parameters simultaneously — may be improbable in landscape | Harnik et al. note this | MODERATE — this is an interpretation, not a physics objection |

### Classification: **NOT REPLICATED** but accepted as valid existence proof.

The paper's conclusion (weak interaction is not strictly necessary
for habitability) has not been seriously challenged in the literature.

## Bound 7: Carr & Rees αG bound (1979)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Original Source** | Carr, B.J. & Rees, M.J. (1979) *Nature* 278, 605–612 |
| **Method** | Analytic scaling relations (Jeans mass, Chandrasekhar mass, stellar cooling time) |
| **Result** | αG ∈ [5.9×10⁻⁴⁵, 5.9×10⁻³⁶] — factor 10⁹ |

### Replications

| Source | Type | Result | Classification |
|--------|------|--------|----------------|
| Adams (2019) *Physics Reports* 807, 1–111 | Stellar structure models | αG ∈ [5.9×10⁻⁴⁰, 5.9×10⁻³⁸] — factor ~100 | **PARTIAL — review paper, narrower bound** |

### Classification: **PARTIALLY REPLICATED**

The general scaling is standard physics. Adams narrows the bound
using modern stellar models but the result is from a review, not
an independent simulation.

### Key finding

**5 of 8 surviving bounds have NO independent replication.**
Only the Λ bound has multiple independent calculations (analytic,
2× N-body). The αG bound is partially replicated. The α/αs carbon
bounds — the narrowest in the corpus — are unreplicated single-paper
results from 1989.
