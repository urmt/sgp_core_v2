\section{Spectral Bound Derivation}

For a normalized covariance matrix $C$ with eigenvalues $\lambda_k$ sorted in
descending order, the exponential decay assumption $\lambda_k \sim e^{-\alpha k}$
gives the participation ratio:

\[
\mathrm{PR} = \frac{\left(\sum_{k=1}^N \lambda_k\right)^2}{\sum_{k=1}^N \lambda_k^2}
\]

Assuming the exponential form and taking $N \to \infty$:

\[
\mathrm{PR}(\alpha) = \frac{(1 + e^{-\alpha})}{(1 - e^{-\alpha})}
\]

This gives the bound $\alpha \cdot \mathrm{PR} \geq 2$ for all covariance matrices
with exponentially decaying spectra (see Paper~1, Eq.~4).

\section{Precision Matrix Estimation}

Precision matrices were estimated using graphical lasso
(Friedman, Hastie \& Tibshirani, 2008) with cross-validated regularization
parameter $\lambda$. The optimal $\lambda$ was selected by 5-fold cross-validation
maximizing test-set log-likelihood.

For the MEC recordings, the precision matrix yields:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Mean $\alpha \approx 0.33 \pm 0.11$
  \item Mean PR $\approx 98 \pm 53$
  \item Sparsity $\approx 78\%$
\end{itemize}
This contrasts with the lag-1 VAR operator ($\alpha \approx 0.01$), confirming that
the dominant statistical structure is instantaneous, not temporal.

\section{Scaling Separation Protocol}

For each recording with $N \geq 100$, neurons were randomly subsampled to sizes
$N \in \{20, 30, 50, 80, 100\}$. At each $N$, the MEC eigenvector statistics
(mean IPR, NNS ratio $r$, eigenvalue decay $\alpha$) were computed from the
correlation matrix and compared against:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{GOE ensemble}: $T \times N$ i.i.d.\ Gaussian matrices, same $T$ as MEC
  \item \textbf{Sparse precision ensemble}: Random sparse symmetric precision
    with sparsity $p=0.05$, diagonal-dominant, inverted to give sparse covariance
\end{itemize}

The ensemble distance $d(N)$ is the Mahalanobis distance between the MEC centroid
and ensemble centroid in the 3D metric space $(\alpha, \text{IPR}, r)$:

\[
d(N) = \left\| \frac{\vec{\mu}_{\text{MEC}} - \vec{\mu}_{\text{ens}}}{\vec{\sigma}_{\text{ens}}} \right\|
\]

Power-law fit: $d(N) = a N^b$ via log-log linear regression.

\section{Bootstrap Confidence}

Each subsampling was repeated 8 times with random neuron subsets to provide
bootstrap confidence intervals. The reported aggregate exponents are means
across 15 recordings with $N \geq 100$.
