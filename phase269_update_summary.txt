SECTION 2: ANSWERS TO PRIMARY QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q1: Do sparse networks produce distinct propagation geometry?
A1: YES - Erdős–Rényi shows lowest amplification (0.000) and weakest localization (0.039), creating distinct Class B

Q2: Do modular networks alter localization behavior?
A2: YES - Modular localization moderate (0.020), not strong like all-to-all (0.006)

Q3: Do hierarchical structures modify saturation boundaries?
A3: NO - Saturation boundary universal at k ≈ 0.6 across all topologies including modular

Q4: Do small-world structures alter amplification efficiency?
A4: YES - Small-world shows moderate amplification (0.013), similar to scale-free (0.016), distinct from all-to-all (0.028)

Q5: Do scale-free structures alter propagation stability?
A5: YES - Scale-free shows moderate propagation stability (42.3), between modular (30.1) and Erdős–Rényi (68.0)

SECTION 3: TOPOLOGY CLASS SUMMARY
================================================================================

CLASS A: all-to-all
  - High amplification (0.028), strong localization (0.006), high stability (44.0)
  - Geometry: High efficiency + strong confinement

CLASS B: Erdős–Rényi
  - Low amplification (0.000), weak localization (0.039), highest stability (68.0)
  - Geometry: Low efficiency + weak confinement + high stability

CLASS C: small-world
  - Moderate amplification (0.013), perfect localization (0.000), moderate stability (58.8)
  - Geometry: Balanced + zero escape

CLASS D: scale-free
  - Moderate amplification (0.016), moderate localization (0.020), high stability (42.3)
  - Geometry: Heterogeneous propagation

CLASS E: modular
  - High amplification (0.024), moderate localization (0.020), lowest stability (30.1)
  - Geometry: High efficiency + moderate confinement + variable
