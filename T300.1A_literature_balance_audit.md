## 2. Quantitative Balance Assessment

### 2.1 Overall Stance Distribution

| Stance | Count | % | Assessment |
|--------|-------|---|------------|
| Supportive | 19 | 39% | Adequate — not dominant |
| Skeptical | 10 | 20% | Thin but viable — 5 of 10 are physics-based (not just philosophical) |
| Neutral | 20 | 41% | Strong base for non-advocacy analysis |

**Concern:** The skeptical contingent is only half the size of the supportive contingent. However, many of the neutral sources (e.g., Adams, Hogan, Guth, Schellekens) contain critical observations that challenge strong fine-tuning claims. The effective "not strongly supportive" block is 30 sources (61%).

### 2.2 Evidence Type Distribution

| Type | Count | % | Assessment |
|------|-------|---|------------|
| Analytic | 16 | 33% | Strong base — good for first-principles bounds |
| Simulation | 5 | 10% | **INADEQUATE** — see §2.3 |
| Review | 15 | 31% | Adequate — risk of second-hand propagation |
| Philosophical | 13 | 27% | Adequate — needed for methodology questions |

### 2.3 Simulation Gap Analysis

Claims that depend on numerical simulation (not pure analytic scaling):

| Claim | Simulation Source | Adequacy |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| α carbon bound (Livio et al., Oberhummer et al.) | Stellar reaction network | Analytic with code — acceptable |
| αs BBN bound (MacDonald & Mullan) | BBN network code | One source — adequate for critique |
| Λ bound (Tegmark et al.) | N-body simulation | Needs confirmation — only 1 N-body study |
| Deuterium-free stars (Grohs & Adams) | Stellar evolution code | One source — adequate for existence proof |
| Stenger Monte Carlo sweep | Stenger's own code | **Weak** — methodology criticized; no independent replication |

**Finding:** The simulation base (5 sources, only 3 with original code) is too narrow for a project claiming first-principles rigor. This is partly inevitable — many key results (Livio carbon bound, Weinberg Λ bound) are analytic and have never been independently simulated. But the Λ bound especially needs more simulation support.

## 4. Weakly-Sourced Influential Claims

These are claims that are widely cited in the fine-tuning literature but rest on thin evidence in our corpus:

### 4.1 Davies Diproton Catastrophe (Source 47)

- **Claim:** A 2% increase in αs makes the diproton bound, eliminating hydrogen and all life.
- **Source in corpus:** Davies (1993) — popular book, no original calculations.
- **Refuted by:** MacDonald & Mullan (2009) — detailed BBN calculation shows 50% increase needed.
- **Verdict:** The claim is already flagged as refuted. No action needed beyond noting it should be excluded from the T300.2 bound database.

### 4.2 Penrose Phase Space Bound (Source 9)

- **Claim:** Initial conditions require precision of 1:10^(10^123).
- **Source in corpus:** Penrose (1989) — book-level analytic estimate.
- **Methodology concern:** Assumes uniform measure on phase space — physically unmotivated. No known physical process samples phase space uniformly.
- **Status:** Already flagged with criticism in T300.1. Should be labeled "philosophical" for purposes of T300.2 (no empirical anchor).

### 4.3 Rees "Just Six Numbers" (Source 10)

- **Claim:** Six numbers determine everything; small changes destroy life.
- **Source in corpus:** Popular book, no original calculations.
- **Dependency issue:** Some of Rees's "six" are not independent (ε and N/α_G are related).
- **Verdict:** Already flagged with Stenger's criticism. Useful as popular synthesis only.

### 4.4 Stenger Monte Carlo (Source 39)

- **Claim:** Monte Carlo sweeps show most constants can vary widely.
- **Methodology concern:** Extremely permissive life criterion ("stable matter + some energy source"). No stellar structure or BBN code. Widely criticized.
- **Status:** Already flagged. Useful as a bound but should be labeled "maximally permissive" in T300.2.

### 4.5 Hoyle State Carbon Bound (Sources 12, 13)

- **Claim:** αs can vary only ~0.5% for carbon production.
- **Methodology:** Stellar reaction network code. Good physics.
- **Known limitation:** Varies one constant at a time only. Does not explore α-αs correlated variation (source 24 shows this matters).
- **Verdict:** This is the strongest bound in the corpus and is well-sourced. The correlation caveat is already noted.

## 5. Correlation and Dependency Blind Spots

### 5.1 Current Coverage

- **α–αs correlation:** Damour & Donoghue (2008) — 1 source covering joint variation in string theory
- **αs–μ correlation:** Implicit in T200.2 dependency analysis (αs sets Λ_QCD → m_p → μ)
- **αs–α_G correlation:** Implicit in T200.2 (αs sets m_p → α_G)
- **Joint variation of 3+ constants:** Zero sources

### 5.2 Gap Assessment

The T200.2 dependency analysis established coupling structure from *known physics*. But the T300.1 corpus has only 1 source (Damour & Donoghue) that directly models how constants co-vary in a unified theory. This is a known limitation of the literature — most anthropic bounds are computed one-parameter-at-a-time.

**Recommendation:** Add a source on joint-parameter constraints. Candidate:
- **Hall, L.J. & Nomura, Y. (2008)** "Evidence for the Multiverse in the CMSSM and Prospects for Collider Searches." arXiv:0712.3194 — explores joint constraints in supersymmetric parameter space.
- Or note this as an architectural limitation (which T300.2 already flags).
