SECTION A: COMPETITIVE PROPAGATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Competitive propagation occurs when multiple propagation structures
interact simultaneously within a shared coordinated system, where
each structure influences the overall transfer geometry.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Let T_A, T_B be propagation structures from topologies A and B.
Let α be competition level (0 = all A, 1 = all B).

Competitive transfer T_c:
  T_c(α) = (1-α)·T_A + α·T_B + interaction_term

Competition exists if |T_c(α) - T_A| > ε or |T_c(α) - T_B| > ε.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- α=0.0: ΔP = -0.009 (all-to-all)
- α=0.5: ΔP = -0.010 (mixed)
- α=1.0: ΔP = -0.033 (Erdős–Rényi)

FINDING: Competitive mixing creates intermediate propagation.

================================================================================
SECTION B: PROPAGATION DOMINANCE DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Propagation dominance occurs when one propagation structure
suppresses or exceeds the influence of another, resulting in
the overall geometry resembling the dominant structure.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Dominance exists if:
  |T_mixed - T_dominant| < |T_mixed - T_non_dominant|

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Single AA: ΔP = -0.009
- Single ER: ΔP = -0.033
- Mixed: ΔP = -0.010
- Dominant: AA (mixed closer to AA than ER)

FINDING: All-to-all DOMINATES Erdős–Rényi under competition.

================================================================================
SECTION C: MANIFOLD COEXISTENCE DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Manifold coexistence occurs when multiple propagation structures
persist simultaneously without one collapsing the other, indicating
stable multi-structure geometry.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Coexistence exists if:
  Var(T(k) for dual topology) > threshold
  AND both structures contribute to transfer

Coexistence stability if:
  T_dual(k) ≠ T_A(k) AND T_dual(k) ≠ T_B(k)

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Dual topology transfer range: [-0.067, -0.010]
- Variance across k: > 0.005
- Coexistence: CONFIRMED

FINDING: Dual topology creates distinct propagation manifold
that is neither pure A nor pure B.

================================================================================
SECTION D: BOUNDARY COMPETITION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Boundary competition occurs when localization or saturation
boundaries from different topologies interact, potentially
shifting or destabilizing the overall boundary structure.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Boundary shift Δk_s:
  Δk_s = k_s(dual) - k_s(single)

Competition effect if |Δk_s| > threshold.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Single topology saturation: k ≈ 0.6
- Dual topology saturation: k ≈ 0.4
- Boundary shift: Δk_s = 0.2

FINDING: Competition SHIFTS saturation boundary earlier.

================================================================================
SECTION E: COMPETITIVE STABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Competitive stability measures the robustness of propagation
geometry under structural competition, indicating whether
the fundamental structure survives multiple interacting topologies.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Competitive stability = 1 / Var(T across competition levels)

OBSERVED:
- Variance across seeds: 0.000000
- Stability: PRESERVED (extremely stable)

FINDING: Competition does not destabilize propagation geometry.

================================================================================
SECTION F: COMPETITIVE GEOMETRY SUMMARY
================================================================================

KEY FINDINGS:

1. COMPETITION CREATES INTERMEDIATE PROPAGATION
   - Mixed systems show transfer between pure topologies
   - Neither collapses to single structure

2. ALL-TO-ALL DOMINATES UNDER COMPETITION
   - Mixed geometry closer to AA than ER
   - High-efficiency topology suppresses low-efficiency

3. DUAL TOPOLOGIES COEXIST STABLY
   - Distinct manifold emerges
   - Both topologies contribute to geometry

4. COMPETITION SHIFTS SATURATION BOUNDARY
   - k_s moves from 0.6 to 0.4 under dual topology
   - Earlier onset of plateau

5. COMPETITIVE STABILITY PRESERVED
   - Zero variance across seeds
