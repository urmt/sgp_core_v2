SECTION A: HETEROGENEOUS PROPAGATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Heterogeneous propagation occurs when persistence transfer proceeds
under nonuniform interaction structure, where coupling strengths
vary across edges rather than being constant.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Let k_ij be coupling from node i to node j.
Heterogeneity level: H = std(k_ij) / mean(k_ij)

Heterogeneous propagation when H > 0.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- k_std=0.0: ΔP = -0.058
- k_std=0.4: ΔP = -0.039 (LESS negative at moderate heterogeneity)
- k_std=0.6: ΔP = -0.041

FINDING: Moderate heterogeneity REDUCES persistence loss.

================================================================================
SECTION B: ASYMMETRIC INTERACTION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Asymmetric interaction occurs when propagation influence differs
directionally between nodes, creating imbalanced coupling structure.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Asymmetry A = |mean(k_ij) - mean(k_ji)| / mean(k)

Asymmetric interaction when A > threshold.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- asym=0.0: ΔP = -0.082
- asym=0.3: ΔP = -0.075
- asym=0.6: ΔP = -0.077
- asym=1.0: ΔP = -0.075

FINDING: Asymmetry DECREASES persistence loss (less negative).

================================================================================
SECTION C: PROPAGATION DELAY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Propagation delay occurs when interaction influence is transmitted
with temporal offset, where nodes receive information from past
states rather than current states.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Let τ be delay steps.
S(t) receives influence from S(t-τ).

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- delay=0: ΔP = -0.053
- delay=1: ΔP = -0.047
- delay=2: ΔP = -0.046
- delay=3: ΔP = -0.046

FINDING: Delays slightly IMPROVE propagation (less negative).
Saturation boundary NOT shifted by delays.

================================================================================
SECTION D: INTERACTION VARIABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Interaction variability measures the variance in coupling structure
across the system, indicating how diverse the interactions are.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

IV = Var(k_ij) across all edges i,j

Higher IV indicates more diverse interaction structure.

OBSERVED:
- Homogeneous (k_std=0): highest |ΔP|
- Heterogeneous (k_std=0.4): reduced |ΔP|
- Suggests: variability may stabilize propagation

================================================================================
SECTION E: HETEROGENEOUS STABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Heterogeneous stability measures the robustness of propagation
geometry under interaction diversity, indicating whether the
fundamental structure survives heterogeneous conditions.

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:

Stability S = 1 / Var(T) across heterogeneity variations

Higher S indicates more stable propagation.

OBSERVED:
- Variance across seeds: 0.000000 (extremely stable)
- Stability: PRESERVED
