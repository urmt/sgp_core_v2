## T720.4 — Adversarial Pass

For each top-20 consequence, ask: **Could standard science explain this without SFH?**

### #1: C42 (Cross-scale C metric correlates across domains)

**Standard explanation:** Complex systems exhibit universal scaling phenomena (power laws, criticality) because of underlying mathematical similarities (self-organization, central limit theorem, renormalization group). A single metric C could correlate across domains simply because all complex systems share mathematical properties — no SFH needed.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #2–5: C14, C46, C47, C48 (Consciousness affects quantum outcomes)

**Standard explanation:** Standard QM predicts no difference between human observation and automated measurement. The measurement problem is resolved by decoherence, not consciousness. Any observed difference would violate Born's rule and contradict a century of experimental confirmation.

**Verdict:** CANNOT be explained without SFH (or some equally radical framework). Score holds.

---

### #6–7: C57, C87 (Self-modeling systems show different behavior)

**Standard explanation:** Self-modeling systems differ from non-self-modeling systems for purely computational reasons — the model provides additional inputs to the decision process. No SFH needed. The difference is already studied in meta-cognition and meta-learning literature.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #8–9: C76, C77 (3D advantage for processing)

**Standard explanation:** 3D data has different statistical properties than 2D or higher-D data (e.g., spatial locality, adjacency structure). ML performance differences across dimensionalities reflect data statistics, not fundamental coherence. No SFH needed.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #10: C89 (Observers affect coherence measurements)

**Standard explanation:** If observers affect measurements via standard measurement theory (decoherence, quantum back-action), no SFH needed. The "beyond standard" claim is what makes this SFH-specific — but isolating that requires distinguishing known measurement effects from purported SF effects.

**Verdict:** PARTIALLY explicable without SFH. Score partially reduced — depends on experimental design.

---

### #11: C93 (Φ correlates with C)

**Standard explanation:** IIT's Φ measures integrated information; C is a ratio metric. Both measure "structure" in a system. Any correlation would reflect the mathematical fact that highly structured systems score high on both metrics — a mathematical artifact, not a physical discovery.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score significantly reduced.

---

### #12: C98 (Meta-analysis of existing quantum data)

**Standard explanation:** If existing data shows human-observed vs automated differences, the standard explanation would be unidentified systematic error in the historical experiments, not consciousness effects. Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence.

**Verdict:** PARTIALLY explicable. A positive result would require ruling out systematic errors first — which is standard scientific practice regardless of SFH.

---

### #13: C68 (Fractal dimensions cluster)

**Standard explanation:** Fractal dimensions cluster because natural systems are constrained by physics, geometry, and evolution — not by coherence optimization. Rivers, lungs, and trees have similar fractal dimensions because of branching constraints, not SFH.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #14–15: C70, C74 (Scale-mirroring architectures)

**Standard explanation:** Scale-mirroring architectures may learn better for purely computational reasons (multi-scale feature extraction, hierarchical representation learning). This is already studied in deep learning (U-nets, ResNets, transformers). No SFH needed.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #16: C49 (Wigner's friend)

**Standard explanation:** Different interpretations of QM give different predictions for Wigner's friend scenarios. The extended Wigner's friend paradox (Brukner 2018) shows that some interpretations can be ruled out experimentally. But this is a debate within standard QM foundations, not evidence for SFH.

**Verdict:** PARTIALLY explicable. Positive result would narrow QM interpretations but not specifically favor SFH.

---

### #17–19: C60, C61, C67 (+→×^ hierarchy, recursion attractors, 1/f noise)

**Standard explanation:** The +→×^ hierarchy is a property of mathematical structures, not physics. Recursion attractors are computational. 1/f noise correlations are debated as potential artifacts of analysis methods. All can be explained without SFH.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.

---

### #20: C69 (Same ML algorithms work across scales)

**Standard explanation:** This is already known — the same ML algorithms (gradient descent, transformers, CNNs) work across image, text, audio, and scientific data. This reflects the generality of the learning algorithms, not a physical principle.

**Verdict:** CAN be explained without SFH. Score reduced.
