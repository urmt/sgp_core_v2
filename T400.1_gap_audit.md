# T400.1 — Literature Gap Audit

## Mission

Identify what is still unknown after T300. For each constant, document
which bounds exist, which have been replicated, what disputes remain,
and assign a confidence level to the current state of knowledge.

---

## α (fine-structure constant)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — multiple tiers: T1 [0.001, 0.1] factor 100 (Barrow & Tipler 1986); T2 Δα/α < ~10% (Adams 2019); T3 ±4% (Livio et al. 1989) |
| Replicated? | **T1: YES** — atomic binding physics is well-understood; upper bound from e+e- pair production is standard QED. **T2: PARTIAL** — Adams (2019) cites multiple stellar codes but the exact ±10% is from his own review, not independently simulated. **T3: NO** — the Livio ±4% carbon-production bound has never been independently replicated with a different code |
| Major Disputes | Harnik weakless universe (2006) shows v → ∞ removes weak force but still allows stars — α bounds from weak physics aren't fundamental. Damour & Donoghue (2008) show α-αs correlation in string theory changes the effective constraint. No known upper bound for α (as α → 1, QED becomes strongly coupled but no catastrophe for life has been proven) |
| Key Unknown | What happens for α > 0.1? No published upper bound beyond e+e- pair production threshold. Stable atoms might exist for much larger α. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** for T1 bound; **MODERATE** for T2 bound; **LOW** for T3 bound |

## αs (strong coupling constant)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — multiple bounds: T1 ~[0.07, 0.15] for BBN (MacDonald & Mullan 2009); T1 Δαs/αs < 0.5% for ⁴He mass ordering (Adams 2019); T3 ±0.5% for carbon (Livio 1989 / Oberhummer 2000) |
| Replicated? | **T1 BBN: YES** — MacDonald & Mullan (2009) is a full BBN simulation; the deuteron binding constraint is firm physics. **T1 ⁴He bound: NO** — Adams (2019) compiles others' work without independent calculation. **T3 carbon: NO** — the ±0.5% carbon bound from Livio/Oberhummer has never been independently simulated. |
| Major Disputes | MacDonald & Mullan directly refute the Davies (1993) diproton catastrophe (±2% claim). The Livio carbon bound (0.5%) is from the same unreplicated 1989 paper as the α bound. The Adams 0.5% bound for ⁴He mass ordering is a different constraint but gives similar numerical value. |
| Key Unknown | What happens for αs < 0.07? No published bound — deuteron binds weakly as αs decreases, but no catastrophe has been proven. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** for T1 BBN bound (±50%); **LOW** for T3 carbon bound (±0.5%) |

## αG (gravitational coupling)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — T2 only: Carr & Rees (1979) [5.9×10⁻⁴⁵, 5.9×10⁻³⁶] factor 10⁹; Adams (2019) [5.9×10⁻⁴⁰, 5.9×10⁻³⁸] factor 100. **NO TIER 3 BOUND EXISTS** |
| Replicated? | **PARTIAL** — Adams (2019) narrows Carr & Rees using modern stellar models but the exact factor-100 band is from a single review paper. The general scaling (Jeans mass, Chandrasekhar mass, stellar cooling time) is well-understood physics. |
| Major Disputes | The Carr & Rees lower bound (αG > 5.9×10⁻⁴⁵) comes from the Jeans mass being < universe mass — which may not be a valid anthropic constraint. Adams narrows to factor ~10 for stellar lifetimes > 1 Gyr. No T3 bound means all αG claims are about stars, not complex life. |
| Key Unknown | Is there any T3 bound on αG? Can complex life exist in universes with different αG? The question is unanswered. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** that the T2 bound is wide (factor ≥ 100); **LOW** whether any T3 bound exists |

## μ = m_e/m_p (electron-proton mass ratio)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — T1/T2 only: μ < 0.42 (Chandrasekhar limit, Barrow & Tipler 1986); μ < 0.01 (molecular binding, Carr & Rees 1979); μ ∈ [5.45×10⁻⁵, 0.00545] factor 100 for stellar opacity (Adams 2019). **NO TIER 3 BOUND EXISTS — THIS IS A GENUINE GAP** |
| Replicated? | **T1: YES** — Chandrasekhar limit is standard stellar physics. **T2: NO** — Adams (2019) factor-100 bound is from a single review, not independently computed. |
| Major Disputes | None — the μ gap is universally acknowledged. Fine-tuning proponents (Lewis & Barnes 2016) concede that μ is only weakly constrained. μ appears in popular fine-tuning claims but has no published Tier 3 bound. |
| Key Unknown | What is the μ range for complex carbon chemistry? This has never been computed. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** that the μ gap exists; **LOW** for T2 bound from Adams |

## v/M_Pl (weak scale / Higgs vev)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — Agrawal et al. (1998): v/M_Pl ∈ [10⁻¹⁸, 5×10⁻¹⁷] factor 50 for BBN/pp-chain. **BUT Harnik et al. (2006) shows this bound is SM-specific** |
| Replicated? | **PARTIAL** — Agrawal has been cited extensively but the calculation depends on holding Yukawa couplings fixed. Harnik removes the weak interaction entirely and finds habitability. No independent code replication exists. |
| Major Disputes | The Agrawal bound is SM-specific — it assumes fixed Yukawas and a nonzero weak interaction. Harnik shows the weak interaction is not necessary. If Yukawas can co-vary with v (as in many BSM theories), the bound dissolves. |
| Key Unknown | Can the weak scale vary while Yukawa couplings compensate? The answer determines whether v is constrained at all. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** that v/M_Pl is constrained under SM-only assumptions; **LOW** that these assumptions hold in a generic landscape |

## Λ (cosmological constant)

| Field | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| Bound Exists? | Yes — Weinberg (1987): Λ < 10⁻¹¹⁸; Tegmark et al. (2006): Λ < ~10⁻¹²⁰; Piran & Jimenez (2023): Λ varies by factor ~100 still allows galaxies. No lower bound (smaller Λ → more structure). |
| Replicated? | **PARTIAL** — Weinberg's analytic bound is standard physics. Tegmark replicated with N-body. Piran & Jimenez replicated with different N-body and found a weaker bound. The factor-100 discrepancy between Tegmark and Piran-Jimenez has not been resolved. |
| Major Disputes | Piran & Jimenez (2023) challenge the necessity of the anthropic Λ prediction — their N-body results show Λ varies by ~100×. The Weinberg bound (10⁻¹¹⁸) is ~10⁴× above observed; the Garriga-Vilenkin prediction (Λ ≈ 0.7×Λ_anth) is consistent with observations within a factor ~3. |
| Key Unknown | What is the exact upper bound for galaxy formation? It may depend on how much structure is "enough" for intelligent life — an unresolved question. |
| Confidence | **HIGH** that Λ is bounded above by ~10⁻¹¹⁶; **MODERATE** on tighter bounds |

## Summary

| Constant | Bound Quality | Replication Status | Dispute Level | Confidence |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|---------------|------------|
| α | T1: Firm; T3: Weak | T3 unreplicated | Moderate | MODERATE |
| αs | T1: Firm (wide); T3: Weak | T3 unreplicated | Moderate | MODERATE |
| αG | T2: Moderate (wide) | Unreplicated | Low | LOW |
| μ | T1: Firm (very wide); T2: Weak | T2 unreplicated | Low | MODERATE |
| v/M_Pl | SM-only | Counterexample exists | High | LOW |
| Λ | Firm upper bound | Partially replicated | Moderate | HIGH |

**Overall assessment:** The literature is strongest on Λ and weakest on μ, αG, and v/M_Pl (beyond SM). The critical open question is the Tier 3 bound for μ, which is a genuine hole in the corpus.
