## Key Decisions
- **Data path**: re-ran 60 trajectories with stored seeds; cached binned time series. Re-measurement, not new intervention. Director confirmed acceptable via text response.
- **8 variants chosen** per Director's spec: E0 baseline, E1-E2 window, E3-E4 bins, E5 shifted, E6 bootstrap, E7 LOO.

- **Critical nuance**: Estimator explains 49% of C variance (more than friction). This is a level effect, not a validity effect — variants are monotone transformations. The 17.5% within-friction residual is the estimator-invariant signal that predicts recovery.

## Critical Context
- **C is robust on rank ordering, estimator-sensitive on level.** Mean C varies 0.37–0.59 across variants. This is the canonical behavior of a monotone transformation of an underlying signal.
- **The 17.5% within-friction residual is the smallest variance component but the only one that predicts recovery.** This is consistent with C being a real but small construct.
- **C's predictive power survives 8 different measurement pipelines.** It is not a calculation artifact.
- **The 3 falsifications (RD-019/020/021) were about absolute C manipulation.** If the level of C is estimator-sensitive but rank-ordering is robust, then interventions that change the level would be expected to fail, but interventions that change the rank-ordering of C_pre could still work.
- **RD-019 caveat**: Within-density ResC still predicts dip (p=0.033), restoration (p<0.001) — C is real and predictive, just not density.
- **Director's escalation pattern**: physical interventions → measurement audit. Next natural move is within-design analysis (no new simulation).
