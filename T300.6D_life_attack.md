# T300.6D — Adversarial Life Definition Attack

## Mission

Attack every conclusion by changing the definition of "life."
Determine which conclusions are criterion-dependent and which are
robust.

---

## Attack 1: The tier system already concedes the point

### The tier structure

| Tier | Label | Life criterion | Number of bounds available |
|------|-------|----------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Stable chemistry | Stable atoms and molecules exist | ~12 |
| 2 | Long-lived stars | Galaxies and stars form; stellar fusion | ~20 |
| 3 | Complex chemistry (carbon) | Carbon-based organic chemistry | ~8 |
| 4 | Information processing | Intelligent observers with technology | 0 |

### Attack: The engine's primary results use Tier 3 (carbon chemistry)

The engine computes results for tiers 1, 2, and 3. But the most widely
cited fine-tuning claims are about **life as we know it** — which is
Tier 3 (carbon-based). If the life criterion is relaxed to Tier 2
(stars only) or Tier 1 (chemistry only), the bounds dramatically widen:

| Constant | T1 bound | T3 bound | Widening |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| α | [0.001, 0.1] (factor 100) | [0.007, 0.0076] (±4%) | Factor ~25 |
| αs | [~0.07, ~0.15] (BBN) | [~0.117, ~0.118] (±0.5%) | Factor ~20 |
| αG | [5.9×10⁻⁴⁵, 5.9×10⁻³⁶] (factor 10⁹) | No T3 bound | N/A |
| v/M_Pl | [10⁻¹⁸, 5×10⁻¹⁷] (factor 50) | No T3 bound | N/A |
| Λ | [0, 10⁻¹¹⁶] (upper bound) | No T3 bound | N/A |

**Attack:** The strongest fine-tuning signals are all Tier 3 —
carbon-specific. At Tier 2, only αs (deuterium) and Λ (structure
formation) have narrow bounds. At Tier 1, all constants have wide
ranges. The entire fine-tuning program pivots on the assumption
that **carbon-based life is the only possible form of complex life**.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — if non-carbon life is possible, the Tier 3
bounds are irrelevant.

## Attack 2: Silicon life would change everything

### The alternative

Silicon-based biochemistry has been seriously discussed in the
astrobiology literature (e.g., Bains 2004, *Trends in Biotechnology*).
Silicon can form complex molecules, though with different bonding
patterns and stability.

### What changes

If life can be silicon-based:

| Constant | Why bound changes | New bound |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| α | Si chemistry uses different bond energies | Wider range (not computed) |
| αs | Si production in stars differs from C/O | Wider — Si primarily from oxygen burning, not triple-alpha |
| Hoyle state | Irrelevant for Si | **NO BOUND AT ALL** — the triple-alpha resonance is specific to carbon |
| μ = m_e/m_p | Si molecular bonding uses different electronic structure | Unknown (not studied) |

**Attack:** The Hoyle state requirement (the narrowest bound in the
corpus) is specific to carbon production. Silicon does not require
the triple-alpha resonance. If silicon life is possible, the
narrowest fine-tuning bound disappears entirely.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — this is the strongest single destruction
argument against Tier 3 fine-tuning claims.

## Attack 3: Alternative biochemistries multiply the possibilities

### Known alternatives

| Type | Example | Constants affected | Status |
|------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Silicon-based | Si-O chains | α, αs | Theoretical |
| Boron-based | B-N compounds | α, αs | Speculative |
| Ammonia-based solvent | NH₃ instead of H₂O | μ, α | Different temperature range |
| Methane-based | CH₄ solvent | μ, α | Different temperature range |
| Weakless universe | No weak force | v/M_Pl | **Existing bound: Harnik et al. 2006** |

### The argument

For each alternative biochemistry, a different set of bounds on the
fundamental constants applies. The "life-permitting range" is the
union of all possible biochemistries' ranges, not the intersection.

**The union is larger than the intersection.**

If there are N possible biochemistries (even N=2), the total
life-permitting volume in parameter space is substantially larger
than the carbon-only volume.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — the engine assumes a single life criterion
(carbon-based) and computes bounds against it. If multiple criteria
exist, the allowed region is the union, and all probabilities increase.

## Attack 4: Unknown alternative life

### The strongest attack

> We don't know what forms of complex organization are possible in
> universes with different physics.

If we cannot enumerate all possible forms of "life" in different
parameter regimes, we cannot compute the life-permitting volume.

**Attack:** This is not just about carbon vs. silicon. It is about
fundamental ignorance:
- We don't know if information-processing systems could exist with
  different chemistry
- We don't know if non-chemical information processors (plasma-based,
  gravitational) are possible
- We don't know if life requires baryonic matter at all

**This is the Haldane / Gould / Sagan objection:** we cannot compute
what we cannot imagine.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — this is a philosophical limitation, not a
technical one. No amount of improved simulation can solve it.

## Attack 5: "Carbon chauvinism" objection

### The term

"Carbon chauvinism" (Sagan) is the assumption that life must be
carbon-based because that's the only form we know.

### The rebuttal

> "Our imagination of what is possible is constrained by our
> experience of what exists."

There is no known physical law that carbon-based chemistry is the
ONLY possible substrate for complex information processing. The
chemical possibilities in universes with different constant values
have not been systematically explored.

**Attack:** The fine-tuning argument implicitly assumes carbon
chauvinism when it uses the C/O > 0.1 criterion as the primary
measure of life-permittingness. This is an empirical bias, not a
physical necessity.

**Severity:** HIGH — widely acknowledged even by fine-tuning proponents
(Lewis & Barnes call it our "best guess").

## Attack 6: A single criterion: "Stable atoms and some energy source"

### Stenger's criterion

Stenger (2011) uses the most permissive definition: "stable matter
exists and some energy source is available."

### Result under this criterion

| Constant | Allowed range | Fine-tuned? |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| α | [~10⁻¹⁰, 1] | No |
| αs | [~0.03, 1] (maybe wider) | No |
| μ | [~10⁻⁴, ~1] | No |
| αG | [0, 1] | No |
| v/M_Pl | [0, 1] | No |
| Λ | (-∞, ~10⁻¹¹⁶] (upper only) | Maybe — only Λ has genuine bound |

**Stenger's conclusion:** Only the cosmological constant is genuinely
fine-tuned, and that's because the QFT prediction is 120 orders of
magnitude larger than observed. But this is a naturalness problem,
not an anthropic problem.

**Attack:** Under the most permissive reasonable life criterion, 5 of
6 constants show no fine-tuning. This has been in the literature for
15+ years.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — Stenger's life criterion is arguably too
permissive, but it remains a valid logical possibility.

## Attack 7: Finite list of alternative life criteria

| Criterion | α | αs | μ | αG | v/M_Pl | Λ | Survives? |
|-----------|---|---|---|-----|--------|---|-----------|
| T1: Stable atoms | Wide | Wide | Wide | Wide | Wide | Wide | **No signal** |
| T2: Stars & galaxies | Moderate | Moderate | None | Wide | Wide | Moderate | **Weak signal** |
| T2: Only galaxies | Moderate | Wide | None | Wide | Wide | Wide | **No signal** |
| T2: Only stars | Wide | Moderate | None | Wide | Moderate | None | **Weak signal** |
| T3: Carbon chemistry | Narrow | Narrow | None | None | None | None | **Strong on α and αs only** |
| T3: Silicon chemistry | Moderate | Moderate | None | None | None | None | **Much weaker** |
| T3: Any complex chemistry | Moderate | Moderate | None | None | None | None | **Weak** |
| Stenger: stable matter + energy | Wide | Wide | Wide | Wide | Wide | Moderate | **Λ only** |

**Finding:** The fine-tuning signal is **entirely carried by the
choice of life criterion**. Different criteria give different
signals. The signal is maximized by the carbon-only criterion.

## Attack 8: Summary

| Attack | Target | Outcome | Severity | Survives? |
|--------|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Tier system concedes point | T1/2 much wider than T3 | CRITICAL | Only T3 shows narrowing |
| 2 | Silicon life | Hoyle state irrelevant | CRITICAL | No T3 α bound for Si life |
| 3 | Alternative biochemistries | Union > intersection | CRITICAL | All probabilities increase |
| 4 | Unknown alternatives | Cannot enumerate | CRITICAL | Permanently unknowable |
| 5 | Carbon chauvinism | Empirical bias | HIGH | Acknowledged limitation |
| 6 | Stenger criterion | 5/6 constants not fine-tuned | CRITICAL | Only Λ survives |
| 7 | Criterion comparison | Signal is criterion-dependent | CRITICAL | No criterion-independent signal |

### Final Verdict

The fine-tuning signal is **entirely contingent on the definition of
life**. If life can take forms other than carbon-based biochemistry
(the only form we know), the signal weakens substantially. If life
can take forms we haven't imagined, the signal may vanish entirely.

The engine correctly handles this by tiering. But the conclusion
that "the universe is fine-tuned" disappears if you relax the life
criterion from carbon-only to any-complex-chemistry.
