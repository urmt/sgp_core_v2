# T400 — Executive Summary

## After all audits, replications, dependency corrections, measure attacks, life-definition attacks, and observer-selection attacks, what conclusions remain defensible with high confidence?

No advocacy. No speculation. No headline probability. Only conclusions
supported by evidence.

---

### 1. The cosmological constant (Λ) has a genuine anthropic upper bound.

- **Evidence:** Three independent derivations (Weinberg 1987 analytic,
  Tegmark et al. 2006 N-body, Piran & Jimenez 2023 N-body) agree
  that Λ must be < ~10⁻¹¹⁶ (Planck units) for galaxies to form.
- **Confidence: HIGH** — this is the best-established result in the
  corpus. The exact bound varies by ~100× between methodologies.
  **Λ is the only constant where the anthropic bound is within
  striking distance (~10⁴×) of the observed value.**

### 2. No other constant supports a strong fine-tuning conclusion.

- **α and αs** have narrow bounds (±4% and ±0.5%) for carbon
  production — but these are single unreplicated papers from the
  1980s, and they depend entirely on the assumption that life must
  be carbon-based. For silicon-based life (Bains 2004), both bounds
  vanish. Confidence in T3 bounds: LOW.
- **μ (m_e/m_p)** has no Tier 3 bound at all — this is a genuine
  gap in the literature that popular fine-tuning claims ignore.
  The only fundamental bound is m_p > m_e (confidence: HIGH).
- **αG** has no Tier 3 bound. The existing T2 bounds (stars + galaxies)
  are wide (factor ≥ 100). The measure assessment is circular (prior
  lower bound set by the very bound it measures). Confidence: LOW.
- **v/M_Pl** has a SM-specific bound (Agrawal 1998) that is destroyed
  by a published counterexample (Harnik 2006 — weakless universe).
  Confidence in the bound: LOW.

### 3. Absolute probabilities are measure artifacts.

- Every probability in the corpus depends on an arbitrary choice of
  prior range. Different measures produce values spanning 10⁴⁰×
  for the same physical event. The normalization problem (McGrew,
  McGrew & Vestrup 2001) shows that infinite parameter ranges make
  all probabilities formally zero. **No defensible absolute
  probability exists.**

### 4. Observer selection provides a complete alternative explanation.

- The weak anthropic principle explains why we observe life-permitting
  constants — we cannot observe otherwise (Carter 1974, Bostrom 2002,
  Sober 2003). The Garriga & Vilenkin (2006) prediction of Λ near the
  anthropic bound is partially successful (correct order of magnitude,
  factor ~3000× discrepancy in the specific value). The Srednicki &
  Hartle (2010) causal-patch measure produces Λ naturally from a
  landscape. **Observer selection does not disprove fine-tuning, but
  it provides a complete alternative that requires no fine-tuning.**

### 5. The strongest defensible claim is conditional.

> **If life is carbon-based, then α and αs must lie within empirically
> determined narrow ranges. This is a statement about the conditions
> for carbon chemistry, not about the probability of our universe.**

### 6. The following claims cannot be defended.

- Any absolute probability number
- "Five constants fine-tuned to 1 part in 10ⁿ"
- v/M_Pl fine-tuning (counterexample exists)
- αG fine-tuning (circular measure, no T3 bound)
- Any claim based on single unreplicated 1980s papers (Livio α/αs)

### Final verdict

After T100–T400, the evidence supports **one** high-confidence
anthropic conclusion: **Λ must be cosmologically small for galaxies
to form.** Everything else is either weak, unreplicated,
criterion-dependent, or explained by observer selection. The
popular narrative of "the universe finely tuned for life at 10¹²⁰
to 1" is not supported by the current evidence.
