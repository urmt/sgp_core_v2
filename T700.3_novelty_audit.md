# T700.3 — Novelty Audit

**Status:** Complete
**Method:** For each prediction, determine whether standard science already predicts or expects the same phenomenon. A prediction is "novel" only if SFH-SGP predicts something that no standard theory predicts.

---

## 1. Novelty Criteria

A prediction is **novel** if it satisfies ALL of:
1. Not already predicted by an established theory (QM, QCD, ΛCDM, neuroscience, ecology, network science, etc.)
2. Not a relabeling of a known measurement with an SFH-specific threshold
3. Derives a specific value or relationship from SFH-SGP axioms (not from asserted baselines)

A prediction is **partially novel** if it:
1. Is not predicted by standard theory BUT
2. Depends on asserted (not derived) SFH-SGP parameters

A prediction is **not novel** if it:
1. Standard theory already makes the same prediction, OR
2. It is a relabeling of standard measurements with no new content, OR
3. It is not a testable prediction at all

### N46 — Toponium Lifetime Deviation (Δτ ≥ 10⁻²⁶ s)

**Standard theory prediction:** QCD predicts the toponium (top quark bound state) lifetime. Toponium decays primarily via t → Wb with lifetime dominated by the top quark decay width (~1.5 GeV, τ ~ 4×10⁻²⁵ s). QCD corrections to the bound state are calculable.

**SFH claim:** Toponium lifetime deviates from QCD prediction by Δτ ≥ 10⁻²⁶ s (C ≥ 0.8).

**Novelty verdict:** **PARTIALLY NOVEL.** If SFH specifically predicts a deviation from the QCD-calculated lifetime, and QCD predicts no deviation (or a different deviation), this is a testable, potentially novel claim. However:
(a) The specific deviation Δτ ≥ 10⁻²⁶ s is asserted, not derived from SFH-SGP axioms
(b) Toponium itself has not been experimentally observed (the top quark is too heavy to form bound states in standard QCD at current collider energies — this is itself an active research question)
(c) The claim "C ≥ 0.8" depends on the same coherence metric issues as all other predictions

## 4. Where Novelty Might Exist

Only three predictions survive as potentially novel:

| Prediction | Novelty | Vulnerability |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| **N46: Toponium lifetime deviation** | Potentially falsifiable measurement that could contradict QCD | Toponium not yet observed; deviation value asserted, not derived |
| **N40: Cross-scale correlations** | No standard theory predicts quantum↔cosmic correlations | Correlation threshold asserted; no derivation from SFH axioms |
| **N44: Quantum-cosmic correlations** | Same as N40 | Same as N40 |

**Critical note:** Even for these three, the prediction threshold is asserted, not derived from SFH-SGP mathematics. A truly novel prediction would require the numeric value to follow from the theory's axioms — here, it follows from the book author's choice of baseline.

This is the pattern across all 18 predictions: SFH-SGP identifies a domain where a measurement exists, asserts a baseline, and claims the excess proves SF optimization. The novelty audit finds that **no prediction derives its specific value from SFH-SGP mathematics**. Every numeric threshold is either: (a) borrowed from standard science and relabeled, or (b) asserted without derivation.
