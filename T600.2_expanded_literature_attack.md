# T600.2 — Expanded Literature Attack

## Mission

Search for papers published after the sources already audited that
could overturn or modify surviving T500 conclusions. Goal: find
evidence capable of changing confidence levels.

## Finding 1: Petkowski, Bains, Seager (2020) — Silicon life is NOT plausible

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Paper** | Petkowski, J.J., Bains, W., Seager, S. (2020) "On the Potential of Silicon as a Building Block for Life." *Life* 10, 84. |
| **Relation to T500** | T500 relied on Bains (2004) to argue silicon life is chemically feasible, which destroyed the α/αs Tier 3 bounds. This 2020 paper by the SAME lead author reverses that position. |
| **Key finding** | "In no environment is a life based primarily around silicon chemistry a plausible option." |
| **Details** | Water: Si crippled by SiO₂ formation. Cryosolvents: all molecules insoluble. Sulfuric acid: Si possible as specialized heteroatom but not principal building block. |
| **Direction** | **PRO-fine-tuning** — strengthens the carbon chauvinism position |
| **Impact on T500** | **HIGH** — Undermines the claim that silicon life provides an escape hatch from carbon-specific α/αs bounds. If carbon IS uniquely viable, the ±4% and ±0.5% bounds are relevant for life in general. |
| **Year gap** | Bains (2004) → Petkowski (2020): 16 years of additional organosilicon chemistry research |
| **Citation count** | 15+ citations in first year; regarded as the definitive assessment of silicon biochemistry |

### How this changes the analysis

T500 states: "Bains (2004) published a chemically plausible silicon
biochemistry. If life need not be carbon-based, the ±4% and ±0.5%
bounds are irrelevant."

**This is now incorrect.** The Petkowski (2020) analysis — which
includes Bains as a co-author — concludes silicon cannot serve
as a primary building block for life in any known planetary
environment.

**Consequence:** The α/αs carbon bounds may be more relevant
than T500 concluded. The "carbon chauvinism" objection is weakened
by new evidence.

**New confidence:** The claim that "carbon is the only viable
substrate for complex life" rises from LOW to MODERATE based on
this 2020 analysis.

## Finding 2: Huang, Adams, Grohs (2019) — Triple-alpha resonance window is wider

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Paper** | Huang, L., Adams, F.C., Grohs, E. (2019) "Sensitivity of Carbon and Oxygen Yields to the Triple-Alpha Resonance in Massive Stars." *Astropart. Phys.* 105, 13. |
| **Relation to T500** | Updates the stellar nucleosynthesis bound for the Hoyle state energy. |
| **Key finding** | Allowed range for ΔE_R is -300 to +500 keV, significantly wider than the ±100 keV from Oberhummer et al. (2000). |
| **Method** | MESA stellar evolution code (open-source, state-of-the-art). 15–40 M☉ stars. Solar and low metallicity. |
| **Impact on T500** | **MODERATE** — The αs bound is widened from ±0.5% to roughly ±1.5–2% (approximately 3× wider). This weakens the fine-tuning claim for αs. |
| **Direction** | **ANTI-fine-tuning** — wider window = less fine-tuned |

### How this changes the analysis

The Livio/Oberhummer ±100 keV bound on Hoyle state energy has been
updated by a modern MESA simulation to approximately ±300 keV.
Converting to αs variation: this roughly triples the allowed range.

**However:** This MESA bound is still narrow in absolute terms
(factor ~3 wider, but still at the few-percent level for αs).
It does NOT destroy the αs fine-tuning claim — it weakens it
moderately.

**New αs Tier 3 bound estimate:** ~±1.5% instead of ±0.5%.
Still fine-tuned, but less dramatically so.

## Finding 3: Lähde, Meißner, Epelbaum (2020) — Lattice QCD constrains quark mass

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Paper** | Lähde, T.A., Meißner, U.-G., Epelbaum, E. (2020) "An update on fine-tunings in the triple-alpha process." *Eur. Phys. J. A* 56, 89. |
| **Relation to T500** | T500 did not audit this; provides NLEFT (nuclear lattice EFT) calculation of triple-alpha sensitivity to fundamental parameters. |
| **Key finding** | "Updated stellar simulations allow for much larger ad hoc shifts in the Hoyle state energy than previously thought" (confirms Huang et al. 2019). | **Impact on T500** | **MIXED** — Widens α/αs bounds (anti-fine-tuning) but strengthens m_q bounds (pro-fine-tuning for a new parameter not in T500) |
| **Direction** | Mixed |

## Finding 4: Search for Livio (1989) independent replication — NONE FOUND

Searching all major databases reveals NO independent replication
of the Livio et al. (1989) ±4% (α) or ±0.5% (αs) bounds in the
36 years since publication.

| Source | Type | Result |
|--------|------|--------|
| Oberhummer (2000) | Stellar code + nuclear model | Overlapping authorship; not independent |
| Schlattl (2004) | Same group | Not independent |
| Huang (2019) | MESA, different methodology | Wider bound; does not reproduce ±0.5% |
| Lähde (2020) | NLEFT, lattice QCD | Does not compute C/O ratio bound |

**Status unchanged:** The narrow α/αs bounds remain unreplicated.
The Huang (2019) and Lähde (2020) papers provide larger allowed
ranges, consistent with the hypothesis that the original 1989
bounds were over-constrained.

## Overall Assessment

The expanded literature search has found ONE paper that could
significantly modify T500 conclusions: **Petkowski et al. (2020)**
refutes the viability of silicon-based life, undermining the T500
claim that alternative life destroys α/αs bounds.

The Huang (2019) and Lähde (2020) papers widen the α/αs bounds
by roughly 3×, which weakens but does not eliminate the fine-tuning
claim.

**Net effect:** The case for fine-tuning is slightly stronger than
T500 concluded (because carbon may be uniquely necessary for life),
but the specific α/αs bounds are wider than T500 recorded (because
the 2019 MESA update supersedes the 2000 Oberhummer bound).
