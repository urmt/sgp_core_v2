# T500.1 — Unknowns Ledger

## Mission

For every surviving uncertainty, record the question, why it is
unresolved, what evidence is missing, and estimated solvability
with existing physics literature.

---

## U1 — μ (electron-proton mass ratio) anthropic bounds for complex chemistry

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | What range of μ = m_e/m_p permits complex carbon-based chemistry? |
| **Why Unresolved** | No published study has computed chemical reaction networks under varied μ. Existing bounds (Adams 2019 factor ~100, Carr & Rees 1979 μ < 0.01) are order-of-magnitude analytic estimates, not simulated. |
| **Evidence Missing** | Quantum chemistry calculations with varied m_e/m_p. The effect on molecular binding energies, reaction rates, and solubility (for water, ammonia, methane) has not been systematically explored. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **HIGH** — Quantum chemistry codes (Gaussian, NWChem) could compute this today. The relevant physics (Born-Oppenheimer approximation, electronic structure) is well-understood. Estimated: one PhD thesis. |

## U2 — α and αs Tier 3 bounds replication

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | Are the Livio et al. (1989) α ±4% and αs ±0.5% carbon-production bounds reproducible with modern stellar codes? |
| **Why Unresolved** | No independent replication exists. The original code is proprietary and has not been run by any other group. Oberhummer et al. (2000) confirmed the αs bound but with overlapping authorship. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A modern stellar nucleosynthesis code (MESA, GENEC, or similar) with varied α and αs, computing C/O ratios after helium burning. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **HIGH** — MESA is publicly available and widely used. The calculation is a matter of implementing variable reaction rates. Estimated: one MS thesis. |

## U3 — α and αs joint parameter space

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | What is the allowed region of joint α-αs variation for carbon production? |
| **Why Unresolved** | Only Damour & Donoghue (2008) address α-αs correlation, and that is from string phenomenology, not nucleosynthesis. The Livio paper notes the joint space is under-sampled. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A 2D parameter sweep (α × αs) with a stellar nucleosynthesis code. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **HIGH** — Same code as U2, with 2D parameter sweep instead of 1D. Estimated: one MS thesis (same as U2). |

## U4 — αG Tier 3 bound for complex life

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | Is there any bound on α_G for complex (non-carbon) life? |
| **Why Unresolved** | No study has addressed this. Existing bounds (Carr & Rees 1979, Adams 2019) address stellar/galactic scales (Tier 2). The question "does αG matter for complex chemistry?" is unanswered. |
| **Evidence Missing** | This may be fundamentally unanswerable — αG affects star formation but not directly chemistry. The question is whether stars can form with the right properties for any kind of life, which depends on what "life" means. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **VERY LOW** — Depends on unresolved question of alternative biochemistries (U5). If life requires stars, αG bounds exist but are wide. If life can exist without stars, αG is unconstrained. |

## U5 — Alternative biochemistry viability

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | Is non-carbon biochemistry (silicon, etc.) physically possible and capable of Darwinian evolution? |
| **Why Unresolved** | Bains (2004) argues for silicon's chemical feasibility, but no detailed simulation of silicon-based evolution exists. The question is partly philosophical (how much complexity is "enough"?). |
| **Evidence Missing** | A theoretical chemistry study demonstrating that silicon (or another substrate) can form self-replicating, information-carrying molecules with mutation and selection. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **LOW** — Experimental prebiotic chemistry is the only real test, and that requires laboratory work, not literature analysis. |

## U6 — Dependency structure beyond the Standard Model

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | How many effective degrees of freedom do the parameters have under a generic beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) theory? |
| **Why Unresolved** | The T200.2 dependency matrix assumes SM particle content. Under BSM (SUSY, string theory, extra dimensions), the correlations between constants change. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A survey of BSM theories' parameter correlations — which is theory-dependent and cannot be settled empirically. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **VERY LOW** — Depends on which BSM theory is correct, which is unknown. |

## U7 — Observer-selection measure problem

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | What is the correct probability measure for observer selection across a multiverse? |
| **Why Unresolved** | Multiple competing measures exist (causal-patch, comoving-volume, scale-factor cutoff). They give different predictions. No consensus. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A theoretical argument identifying the unique correct measure. Or, failing that, empirical discrimination between measure predictions. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **LOW** — The measure problem is widely recognized as unresolved after 20+ years. Some (e.g., Carroll) argue it may be unsolvable because different measures correspond to different physical theories. |

## U8 — Cosmological constant measure ambiguity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | What is the exact anthropic upper bound on Λ for galaxy formation? |
| **Why Unresolved** | Tegmark (2006, N-body) gives Λ < ~10⁻¹²⁰ for MW-like galaxies. Piran & Jimenez (2023, N-body) gives Λ varying by ~100×. Barnes (2012, analytic) gives Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁶ for any structure. The discrepancy is unresolved. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A controlled comparison of the N-body codes to determine which is correct, and a resolution of what "galaxy formation" means for life. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **MODERATE** — A direct code comparison (same initial conditions, same resolution) could resolve the factor-100 discrepancy. Published codes available. |

---

## U9 — v/M_Pl bound under generic BSM

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | Is the weak scale anthropically constrained beyond the SM? |
| **Why Unresolved** | The Agrawal bound is SM-specific (fixed Yukawas, weak interaction required). Harnik (2006) shows the weak interaction itself is not required. The general question — what range of v/M_Pl permits life if Yukawas can co-vary — has not been answered. |
| **Evidence Missing** | A BSM theory that specifies how Yukawas and the Higgs vev vary jointly. This is theory-dependent. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **VERY LOW** — Depends on unknown BSM physics. |

---

## U10 — Λ specific value prediction

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Question** | Why is Λ = 10⁻¹²² specifically, and not some other value within the anthropic window? |
| **Why Unresolved** | Observer selection predicts Λ near the anthropic upper bound (~10⁻¹¹⁸ to ~10⁻¹²⁰), but the observed value is ~10⁻¹²². Garriga & Vilenkin's prediction (Λ ≈ 0.7×Λ_anth ≈ 7×10⁻¹¹⁹) is off by ~3000×. |
| **Evidence Missing** | Either a more refined anthropic prediction (with better modeling of observer formation) or an alternative explanation from fundamental physics. |
| **Estimated Solvability** | **VERY LOW** — The factor ~3000× discrepancy has been known for ~20 years with no resolution. |
