## The RD's Suspect: Constrained Possibility Space

The RD hypothesized that the strongest consequence emerges from **constrained possibility space**, not consciousness-causes-collapse.

This maps to multiple Distance-2 consequences:

### C42: Cross-scale coherence metric correlates (Score: 23, Dist: 2)

**What it predicts:**
If reality is coherence-structured (A3) and principles repeat across scales (A6), then a single coherence/complexity metric C should show correlated values when applied to systems at different scales — biological, physical, social, cognitive.

**Why it's short inference:**
A3 says coherence is physically meaningful — it determines system organization.
A6 says the same organizational principles apply at all scales.
Therefore: the same coherence structure should appear at different scales.
Therefore: a metric that captures this structure should show correlated values.
The only assumption: the metric can be operationalized.

**Why C42 beats C57 (Distance 1) as a T800 candidate:**
C57 and C59 are too generic — "self-modeling systems behave differently" is true by definition and already studied. They don't specifically test SFH.
C42, by contrast, is not currently studied as a cross-domain coherence phenomenon. It can be tested without additional assumptions.

### Constrained Possibility Space — The Unified Hypothesis

The core idea, directly from A2+A3+A6:

> If reality is a projection (A2) of a deeper coherence-structured substrate (A3), and the same organizational principles apply across all levels (A6), then the number of highly coherent organizational configurations is small relative to total possibility space. Therefore, independently evolved or formed systems will converge on the same small set of highly coherent configurations.

This predicts:

1. **Statistical clustering** — natural systems should cluster around a small number of organizational archetypes (not be uniformly distributed across possibility space)
2. **Cross-domain recurrence** — the same organizational patterns should appear in physical, biological, social, and cognitive systems
3. **Coherence as a selection principle** — among all possible configurations, those with high coherence are preferred
4. **Convergent evolution of structure** — systems that appear independently (galaxies, ecosystems, neural networks, social organizations) should converge on similar structural parameters

### Empirical content of constrained possibility space

This makes a **specific, testable claim** that differs from standard science:

| Claim | Standard Science | Constrained Possibility Space |
|-------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Cross-domain structural similarities | Coincidental or explained by independent physical constraints | Reflect shared coherence principle |
| Distribution of organizational forms | Broad, constrained by local physics | Tightly clustered around coherence optima |
| Convergent evolution | Adaptation to similar environments | Coherence optimization plus adaptation |
| Fractal dimension values | Determined by local geometry/constraints | Cluster around SF-preferred values |

The divergence: **Standard science predicts broadly distributed structural parameters** determined by local constraints. Constrained possibility space predicts **clustering** around specific coherence-optimal values — even when local constraints differ.

After inference distance audit, the single strongest candidate is:

### C42: Cross-scale coherence metric correlates across domains

**Inference distance:** 2 (shortest among non-tautological consequences)
**T720 score:** 23/30
**Adversarial survival:** Partial — standard science can explain some cross-domain patterns, but C42 makes a specific claim about coherence as the organizing principle
**Domain independence:** Applies across all complex systems
**Testability:** High — can be tested with existing data across multiple domains

**The specific prediction:**
> A single operationalized coherence/complexity metric C, when applied to systems across physical, biological, social, and cognitive domains, will show significantly correlated values. The correlation is stronger than expected from null models that control for domain-specific constraints.

**Why this is the right answer:**
1. It follows directly from A3+A6 with minimal assumptions (Distance 2)
2. It avoids the consciousness-causes-collapse category error
3. It is quantifiable and testable with existing data
4. A null result would genuinely weaken SFH — it is not trivially true
5. It bridges complexity science, information theory, and SFH without requiring novel physics

### If the RD wants a single executable T800 research program, this is the candidate.
