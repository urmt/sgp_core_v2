## Literature Survey

### 1. Least Action Principle

**Classical Mechanics:** The path taken by a physical system is the one that minimizes the action (integral of Lagrangian over time).

**Key Works:** Maupertuis (1744), Euler (1744), Lagrange (1788), Hamilton (1834).

**Relation to RD:** RD asks whether persistent systems occupy efficient trajectories. Least action says physical systems follow extremal paths. Difference: RD focuses on persistence and adaptation, not just physical extremization.

**Status:** Related but distinct.

---

### 2. Free Energy Principle

**Neuroscience/Physics:** Biological systems minimize variational free energy, which is equivalent to maximizing model evidence.

**Key Works:** Friston (2010), Friston et al. (2017).

**Relation to RD:** FEP suggests organisms minimize surprise. RD asks whether persistent systems occupy efficient trajectories. Difference: FEP is about inference and model updating; RD is about developmental trajectories.

**Status:** Related but distinct.

## Key Finding

RD is not rediscovering existing ideas. The existing ideas focus on:
- Physical extremization (least action)
- Inference and model updating (FEP, active inference)
- Neural representation (efficient coding)
- Entropy production (dissipative adaptation)
- Fitness (evolution)
- Structure formation (self-organization)
- Historical contingency (path dependence)

RD focuses on:
- Developmental trajectories
- Persistence and adaptation
- Efficiency in possibility space

These are related but distinct concepts.
