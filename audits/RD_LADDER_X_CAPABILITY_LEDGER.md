# RD-LADDER.X — Capability Ledger

**Goal:** List observable capabilities at each rung and identify differences. No mechanisms. No explanations. No ontology. No naming of operations.

**Method:** For each transition, list only what can be observed.

---

## Transition 1: Photon → Electron

### Lower level capabilities (Photon)
- Propagates at c
- Carries energy
- Carries momentum
- Can be absorbed
- Can be emitted
- Cannot be at rest

### Higher level capabilities (Electron)
- Propagates at speeds < c
- Carries energy
- Carries momentum
- Can be absorbed
- Can be emitted
- Can be at rest
- Has mass
- Has charge
- Can be bound in potential wells
- Can occupy quantized energy levels

### Capabilities present in higher, absent in lower
- Can be at rest
- Has mass
- Has charge
- Can be bound in potential wells
- Can occupy quantized energy levels

## Transition 2: Electron → Atom

### Lower level capabilities (Electron)
- Localized excitation
- Has mass
- Has charge
- Can be bound in potential wells
- Can occupy quantized energy levels

### Higher level capabilities (Atom)
- Consists of nucleus + electrons
- Has shell structure
- Has chemical identity
- Can be excited to metastable states
- Can emit photons at specific wavelengths
- Can absorb photons at specific wavelengths

### Capabilities present in higher, absent in lower
- Consists of multiple particles
- Has shell structure
- Has chemical identity
- Can be excited to metastable states
- Emits/absorbs at specific wavelengths

## Summary Ledger

| Transition | New Capabilities |
|------------|------------------|
| Photon→Electron | Can be at rest, has mass, has charge, can be bound, quantized levels |
| Electron→Atom | Multiple particles, shell structure, chemical identity, metastable states, specific wavelengths |
| Atom→Molecule | Multiple atoms, 3D geometry, bond lengths/angles, functional groups, chirality, catalysis |
| Molecule→Replicator | Copy direction, template, complementarity, faithful copying |
| Replicator→Cell | Internal environment, boundary, metabolism, growth, division, homeostasis |
| Cell→Organism | Multiple cells, differentiation, body plan, tissues, organs, development |
| Organism→Mind | Internal models, absent state representation, prediction, planning, learning, symbolic communication |

---

## Observation

**Every transition adds capabilities that were not available at the lower level.**

The ledger is cumulative: each rung includes everything below it plus new capabilities.

**No capabilities are removed.**

The ladder appears to be addition-only, not replacement.
