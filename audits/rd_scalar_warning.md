## Definition

> Single scalar summaries may conceal important structural differences in temporal behavior, variance decomposition, or protocol sensitivity.

## Evidence

GS, RB, and AM:

- Partially similar variance ratios (1.04x, 1.05x, 1.24x)
- Dramatically different magnitudes (0.0075 to 0.1610)
- Different temporal signatures (peaked vs high→low→gradual)

## Implications

1. **Multiple quantities are needed.** No single scalar captures measurement behavior.

2. **Table E is insufficient alone.** Must be accompanied by temporal signature and temporal spread.

3. **"Stability" is not a single dimension.** Magnitude, variance structure, and temporal signature are partially independent.

## Required Output

All future trajectory audits must include:

1. **Table E — Variance Decomposition** (within, between, ratio, mean)
2. **Temporal Signature** (qualitative description of temporal evolution)
3. **Temporal Spread** (range of ΔC_rank across time)
4. **Magnitude Scale** (absolute sensitivity)
