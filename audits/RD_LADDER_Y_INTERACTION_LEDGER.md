# RD-LADDER.Y — Interaction Ledger

**Goal:** Locate where persistence appears to reside at each transition. Not to conclude what is fundamental.

**Method:** For each transition, record:
1. New Interaction: What interaction first becomes possible?
2. Stable Loop: Does a feedback or self-maintaining cycle appear?
3. Persistence Carrier: Where does persistence seem to reside?
4. Lower Levels Required?: Can higher level exist without lower level continuing to exist?
5. Cross-Level Interaction?: Do lower levels continue interacting with higher levels after emergence?

---

## Transition 1: Photon ↔ Electron

### New Interaction
Electron can absorb or emit photon. Photon mediates electromagnetic force between electrons.

### Stable Loop
No self-maintaining cycle. Absorption/emission is event-based, not cyclic.

### Persistence Carrier
**Object.** Electron persists as localized excitation. Photon persists as propagating excitation.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Electron cannot exist without electromagnetic field (photon field).

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Electrons continue to interact with photons after electron exists.

---

## Transition 2: Electron ↔ Atom

### New Interaction
Electrons interact with nucleus via Coulomb force. Electrons interact with each other via Pauli exclusion.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Electrons orbit nucleus in stable bound states. Coulomb attraction + kinetic energy + exclusion = stable orbit.

### Persistence Carrier
**Network.** Atom persists through electron-nucleus interaction network. No single component persists independently.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Atom cannot exist without electrons and nucleus continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Electrons continue to interact with nucleus after atom forms. Electrons also interact with other electrons.

---

## Transition 3: Atom ↔ Molecule

### New Interaction
Atoms share electrons via covalent bonds. Atoms interact via van der Waals forces.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Covalent bond = shared electron density between nuclei. Bond persists as long as energy input < bond energy.

### Persistence Carrier
**Network.** Molecule persists through atom-atom interaction network. No single atom persists independently in molecule.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Molecule cannot exist without atoms continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Atoms continue to interact within molecule. Atoms also interact with other atoms/molecules.

---

## Transition 4: Molecule ↔ Replicator

### New Interaction
Molecule serves as template for synthesis of complement. Template directs assembly of matching molecule.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Template → complement → template → complement. Self-sustaining copying cycle.

### Persistence Carrier
**Process.** Replicator persists through copying process. Template persists only if copying continues.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Replicator cannot exist without molecules continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Molecules continue to interact within replicator. Molecules also interact with environment.

---

## Transition 5: Replicator ↔ Cell

### New Interaction
Replicator interacts with membrane-bound environment. Metabolism provides energy for replication.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Replicator → metabolism → energy → replication. Self-maintaining cycle bounded by membrane.

### Persistence Carrier
**Boundary.** Cell persists through membrane maintaining distinct internal environment. Replicator persists only within boundary.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Cell cannot exist without replicators and molecules continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Replicators continue to interact within cell. Replicators also interact with metabolism.

---

## Transition 6: Cell ↔ Organism

### New Interaction
Cells interact via signaling molecules. Cells adhere to form tissues. Cells differentiate.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Cell signaling → differentiation → tissue formation → organism function. Multicellular coordination cycle.

### Persistence Carrier
**Network.** Organism persists through cell-cell interaction network. No single cell persists independently.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Organism cannot exist without cells continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Cells continue to interact within organism. Cells also interact with environment.

---

## Transition 7: Organism ↔ Mind

### New Interaction
Organism interacts with environment via sensory input and motor output. Brain generates internal models.

### Stable Loop
Yes. Perception → model → prediction → action → perception. Self-referential modeling cycle.

### Persistence Carrier
**Process.** Mind persists through recurrent modeling process. Self-model persists only if modeling continues.

### Lower Levels Required?
**Yes.** Mind cannot exist without organism continuing to exist.

### Cross-Level Interaction?
**Yes.** Organism continues to interact with environment after mind emerges. Mind also interacts with other minds.

## Observation

**Stable loops appear at every transition except the first.**

Photon↔Electron is event-based, not cyclic. All higher transitions involve self-maintaining cycles.

**Persistence carrier varies.**

- Object: Photon, Electron
- Network: Atom, Molecule, Organism
- Process: Replicator, Mind
- Boundary: Cell

**No single persistence carrier dominates.**

Persistence resides in different things at different levels.

**Lower levels are always required.**

No higher level can exist without lower levels continuing to exist.

**Cross-level interaction always occurs.**

Lower levels continue interacting with higher levels after emergence.
