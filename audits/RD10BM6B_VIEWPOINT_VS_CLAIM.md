## Interpretation

### The Fundamental Condition

The fundamental condition for tension-preservation is:

> Treat descriptions as VIEWPOINTS (different perspectives on the same thing), not as CLAIMS (competing answers to the same question).

### Why This Works

Viewpoint comparison preserves tension because:
- No description is "correct" — all are valid perspectives
- Explanatory power emerges from the space between descriptions
- Collapse is unnecessary because there is nothing to resolve

Claim comparison causes tension because:
- One description must be "correct" — the others must be wrong
- Explanatory power is attributed to the winning description
- Collapse is inevitable because there is something to resolve

### The Strongest Conclusion

> The fundamental operation is not comparison, translation, or distinction. It is the maintenance of descriptions as viewpoints rather than claims.
