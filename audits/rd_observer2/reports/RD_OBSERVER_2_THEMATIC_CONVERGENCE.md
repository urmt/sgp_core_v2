## Interpretation

### What This Means (Per SR-32)

The 13 core structures found by all (or nearly all) observers are **candidate observer-invariant structures**. They survive across 6 different theoretical priors:

1. Reductionist physicist
2. Systems biologist
3. Information theorist
4. Process philosopher
5. Sentience-first observer
6. Null minimalist

These structures are likely properties of the **data**, not properties of any single observer's framework.

### What This Does NOT Mean

1. **Not truly independent.** All observers share LLM substrate (SR-32). True independence requires different research groups.

2. **Not proven.** This is one dataset, one LLM, one moment. Replication across different data, different LLMs, and different researchers is needed.

3. **The divergence matters too.** The structures found by only 1-2 observers reveal **observer-dependent** interpretation. These are candidates for the hidden fixed variable (RD-LAW.1): explanatory power migrating into whatever the observer holds fixed.

### The Deepest Convergence

The most striking convergence is in Dataset 3 (historical studies). ALL six observers, despite radically different priors, identified:
- Persistence as the dominant explanatory locus
- The migration graph as acyclic and linear
- Extreme survivorship bias

This suggests these are genuine properties of the archive, not artifacts of any particular theoretical lens.

### The Information Theorist's Unique Contribution

Observer C (Information Theorist) was the only one to quantify effective dimensionality (3-4 for D1, 10-15 for D2) and compression ratios (23.6:1 for D3). This is observer-specific structure — useful but not found by others.
