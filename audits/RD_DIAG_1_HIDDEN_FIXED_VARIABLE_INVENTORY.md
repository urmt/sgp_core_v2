# RD-DIAG.1 — Hidden Fixed Variable Inventory

**Goal:** Historical inventory of claimed discoveries, collapses, hidden fixed variables, and migration targets.

**Method:** For every audit from RD-019 through RD-GEOMETRY.3, list:
1. Claimed discovery
2. Later collapse or qualification
3. Hidden fixed variable eventually discovered
4. What explanatory work migrated into next

---

## RD-019: Causal Intervention (Density)

**Claimed discovery:** Density is causally involved in C.

**Collapse/qualification:** Density (R²=0.055) does not explain C. The intervention was misdirected.

**Hidden fixed variable:** The assumption that C measures interaction structure. C is 80% reconstructable from MSE.

**Migration target:** C ≈ −MSE (r=−0.89). Explanatory power migrated into the metric itself.

**Hidden fixed variable:** The assumption that structural importance is a property of the network. It may be a property of the measurement.

**Migration target:** Topology as fundamental. Explanatory power migrated from structural importance to topology.

## Observation

**The most persistent object in the program is not any specific discovery.**

**It is the pattern:**

> Explanatory power repeatedly migrates into whichever relational structure remains fixed across audits.

**This pattern has survived almost every collapse.**

---

## Correction (RD-DIAG.1 itself)

**RD-DIAG.1 holds the research program itself fixed.**

Every audit in RD shares:
- the same research culture
- the same intuitions
- the same decomposition habits
- the same mathematical preferences
- the same notion of explanation

In other words:

> **Research Program** may be the deepest fixed variable discovered so far.

Not because the results are wrong. But because every audit was generated by essentially the same cognitive machinery.

---

## Strongest Surviving Statement

Not:

> Explanatory power migrates into fixed relational structures.

But:

> Within this research program, explanatory power repeatedly migrated into structures that had previously been held fixed.

That statement is narrower. But it is very hard to attack.

---

## Standing Rule 30 (RD-DIAG.1)

> Any survivor discovered entirely within one research program must be treated as potentially program-dependent until tested by an independent decomposition or independent research culture. The most dangerous fixed variable is the one doing the observing.
