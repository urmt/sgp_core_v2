# RD-SURVIVOR BIAS WARNING

**Source:** Research Director (RD-WELL.8A authorization)
**Status:** ACTIVE WARNING
**Date:** 2026-06-17

## Statement

**"Domains that survive repeated audits may become overrepresented in theory formation."**

MHD is currently receiving many audits because it is interesting. That itself can bias the program. A domain that attracts more attention may accumulate more evidence (both positive and negative) than domains that are less interesting.

## Evidence

MHD has received:
- RD-WELL.7A (Reconnaissance)
- RD-WELL.7A.R1 (Reconnaissance Validation)
- RD-WELL.7A.R2 (Volume vs Slice)
- RD-WELL.7B (C Computation)
- RD-WELL.7B.R1 (Replication)
- RD-WELL.7C (Representation Stability)
- RD-WELL.7C.R1 (RT Consistency — included MHD as reference)

Total: 7 audits. Other domains: 1-2 audits each.

## Implications

1. **Attention bias:** Interesting domains attract more audits.
2. **Evidence accumulation bias:** More audits = more evidence = stronger claims.
3. **Theory formation bias:** Theories may be shaped by the domains that received the most attention.

## Required Protocol

1. **Equal audit allocation:** When possible, allocate audit effort equally across domains.
2. **Novelty audit:** Periodically audit domains that have received less attention.
3. **Bias tracking:** Track which domains receive the most audits and why.

## Relationship to Other Warnings

- **RD-SMALL-N WARNING:** Few domains may generate attractive but unstable associations.
- **RD-CONSTRAINT WARNING:** Apparent robustness may derive from physical constraints.
- **RD-TEMPORAL WARNING:** Representation stability may evolve during system evolution.
