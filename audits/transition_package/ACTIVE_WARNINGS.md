# ACTIVE WARNINGS

**Purpose:** Preserve archive immune system.
**Created:** 2026-06-20

---

## Master Principle

> "The project studies the organization of measurements, not the ontology of reality."

---

## RD-PROTOCOL DRIFT WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Analysis protocol changes between measurement instances.

**Prohibited Inference:** Apparent contradictory measurements imply genuine physical differences.

**Corrective Action:** Document protocol version, test whether measurement differences survive protocol alignment.

**Historical Example:** P7 showed protocol variation affects magnitude CV=1.40 but ratio CV=0.07.

---

## RD-SCALAR WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Single scalar summary used to characterize complex measurement behavior.

**Prohibited Inference:** Scalar value represents complete system behavior.

**Corrective Action:** Report scalar alongside distributional summary. Never claim scalar captures full dynamics.

**Historical Example:** GS frame-0 estimate ≈ 0.07, frame-250–1000 estimate ≈ 0.18. Single frame not representative.

---

## RD-ASYMPTOTIC WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Apparent convergence within available replication depth.

**Prohibited Inference:** Convergence implies stabilization at true asymptote.

**Corrective Action:** Report convergence half-life, asymptotic floor estimate, and replication depth. Never extrapolate beyond available data.

**Historical Example:** P11 showed convergence half-life ~5–7 trajectories. None fully stabilized.

---

## RD-SIGNATURE WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Similar temporal signatures observed across domains or protocols.

**Prohibited Inference:** Similar signatures imply similar underlying mechanisms.

**Corrective Action:** Test signature robustness under perturbation. Report fragility as measurable property.

**Historical Example:** P10 showed most signatures FRAGILE under aggressive perturbation. MHD signature ROBUST only due to insufficient data.

---

## RD-STABILIZATION WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Apparent convergence under increasing replication depth.

**Prohibited Inference:** Convergence implies genuine stabilization.

**Corrective Action:** Test whether convergence reflects averaging-induced smoothing or genuine stabilization. Report convergence half-life.

**Historical Example:** P11 showed convergence partly sampling artifact.

---

## RD-CONSTRAINT WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Measurements applied to highly constrained systems.

**Prohibited Inference:** Apparent stability derives from universal measurement behavior.

**Corrective Action:** Test whether measurements from unconstrained systems fail in constrained systems. Report constraint sensitivity.

**Historical Example:** P6 (MHD) showed constraint structure altered absolute scale without altering variance structure.

---

## RD-PSEUDOREPLICATION WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Multiple measurements drawn from same underlying realization.

**Prohibited Inference:** Multiple measurements provide independent evidence.

**Corrective Action:** Identify primary replication unit (independently generated trajectory). Report replication ledger.

**Historical Example:** Central warning throughout P1–P19.

---

## RD-TEMPORAL WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Single-frame measurement used to characterize temporal behavior.

**Prohibited Inference:** Single-frame estimate is representative of full temporal behavior.

**Corrective Action:** Report temporal distribution, convergence half-life, and frame-dependence. Never claim single-frame representativeness.

**Historical Example:** P1 showed GS representation stability is temporally variable.

---

## RD-RATIO WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Similar variance ratios observed across domains.

**Prohibited Inference:** Similar ratios imply similar absolute measurement scales.

**Corrective Action:** Report absolute scale alongside ratio. Never assume ratio implies scale equivalence.

**Historical Example:** GS ratio=1.04×, RB ratio=1.05×, but absolute scales differ by 40×.

---

## RD-ANALOGY WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Similar measurement behavior observed across different physical systems.

**Prohibited Inference:** Similar behavior implies common mechanism.

**Corrective Action:** Test whether behavior survives transport across domains. Report analogy as observation, not explanation.

**Historical Example:** P8 showed CML convergence behavior replicates across implementations, but mechanism unspecified.

---

## RD-SMALL-N WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Cross-domain comparisons involving very few physical worlds.

**Prohibited Inference:** Small-N comparison provides strong evidence for universal behavior.

**Corrective Action:** Report N explicitly. Never claim universality from small-N comparison.

**Historical Example:** P4 cross-domain comparison with N=5 domains.

---

## RD-COVERAGE WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Apparently precise cross-domain comparisons.

**Prohibited Inference:** Precision implies genuine differences.

**Corrective Action:** Report replication coverage. Test whether precision arises from uneven coverage.

**Historical Example:** P8A replication provenance audit.

---

## RD-CANONICAL WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Multiple versions of same measurement exist.

**Prohibited Inference:** Different versions are interchangeable.

**Corrective Action:** Record provenance and audit history for every canonical value. Never assume version equivalence.

**Historical Example:** Multiple C computation versions across P1–P19.

---

## RD-METRIC-CASCADE WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Measurements derived from other measurements.

**Prohibited Inference:** Derived measurements inherit properties from source measurements.

**Corrective Action:** Report dependency chain. Test whether derived measurements preserve source properties.

**Historical Example:** MSE derived from sample entropy; TE derived from mutual information.

---

## RD-SURVIVOR BIAS WARNING

**Trigger Condition:** Domains that survive repeated audits become overrepresented.

**Prohibited Inference:** Surviving domains represent universal behavior.

**Corrective Action:** Report survivor bias explicitly. Test whether non-surviving domains would have shown different behavior.

**Historical Example:** GS, RB, CML overrepresented due to trajectory availability.
