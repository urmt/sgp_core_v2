# RD-OBSERVER.2 — Independent Observer Construction

**Goal:** Construct genuinely independent observers to subtract observer bias.

**Method:** Create multiple agents with different priors. Give all of them identical raw data. Measure overlap.

---

## The Problem

If every observer is generated by the same LLM family, same training corpus, same mathematics, same language, and same culture—

then we have only created variations of ourselves.

And RD has repeatedly shown:

> Hidden fixed variables dominate explanations.

---

## Observer Construction

### Observer A: Reductionist Physicist

**Assumptions:**
- Lower levels dominate
- Conservation laws primary
- Emergence secondary

**Focus:** Particles, forces, energy, entropy.

**Vocabulary:** Reduction, mechanism, causation, law.

---

### Observer B: Systems Biologist

**Assumptions:**
- Feedback primary
- Homeostasis important
- Networks matter

**Focus:** Networks, feedback loops, adaptation, regulation.

**Vocabulary:** Function, organization, adaptation, system.

---

### Observer C: Information Theorist

**Assumptions:**
- Information flow primary
- Compression important
- Coding matters

**Focus:** Entropy, mutual information, channel capacity, coding.

**Vocabulary:** Information, entropy, encoding, channel.

---

### Observer D: Process Philosopher

**Assumptions:**
- Processes primary
- Objects secondary
- Becoming over being

**Focus:** Change, becoming, interaction, process.

**Vocabulary:** Process, becoming, interaction, event.

---

### Observer E: Sentience-First Observer

**Assumptions:**
- Experience is primitive
- Interaction is primitive experience
- Persistence occurs within experience

**Focus:** Experience, interaction, persistence, coherence.

**Vocabulary:** Experience, interaction, persistence, coherence.

---

### Observer F: Null Minimalist

**Assumptions:**
- Introduce no new explanatory objects unless absolutely required
- Prefer fewer assumptions
- Reject ontology inflation
- Compress explanations aggressively

**Focus:** Compression, parsimony, elimination.

**Vocabulary:** Minimal, sufficient, necessary, parsimonious.

**Scoring Function:**

Score = ExplanatoryPower − λ(AssumptionCount)

where λ is a complexity penalty.

The Null Minimalist always prefers the lower score.

**Note:** Given RD's history of promoting survivors too early, Observer F may be one of the most valuable observers in the entire system. This scoring function turns skepticism into something measurable.

---

## Critical Warning: Pseudo-Independence

All six observers are still generated by the same LLM.

Therefore:

They are **pseudo-independent**.

Not truly independent.

This must be explicitly recorded.

Observer diversity is not observer independence.

---

## Procedure

1. Prepare identical raw data (granular simulation outputs, RD archive)
2. Send data to all six observers
3. Each observer applies their own framework
4. Each observer identifies survivors
5. Compare survivors across observers

---

## Metrics

- **Observer Invariance:** What survives across all observers?
- **Observer Specificity:** What is unique to each observer?
- **Overlap Strength:** How much agreement is there?

---

## Formal Statement

> Evidence Strength ∝ Observer Invariance
