# T400.3 — Alternative-Life Audit

## Mission

Test whether conclusions about fine-tuning survive under alternative
definitions of "life." Consider silicon chemistry, ammonia solvents,
plasma-based intelligence, and substrate-independent information
processing.

---

## Part 1: Silicon chemistry

### Background

Silicon is the most seriously discussed alternative biochemical
substrate. Silicon can form complex molecules (silanes, siloxanes)
with similar structural complexity to carbon compounds. Bains (2004)
provides a thorough chemical analysis.

### Bounds affected

| Constant | Carbon bound | Silicon bound | Delta |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| α | T3: ±4% (Livio via C/O ratio) | Unknown — no published bound | **Critically wider** |
| αs | T3: ±0.5% (Livio via Hoyle state) | Unknown — Si from oxygen burning, not triple-alpha | **Hoyle state irrelevant** |
| μ | T1/T2 only (same) | T1/T2 only (same) | No change |
| αG | T2 only (same) | T2 only (same) | No change |
| v/M_Pl | T2 SM-specific | T2 SM-specific (same) | No change |
| Λ | T2 bound (structure formation) | T2 bound (same) | No change |

### Hoyle state analysis

The Hoyle state (7.656 MeV in ¹²C) is specific to the triple-alpha
reaction (3 ⁴He → ¹²C). This reaction is the bottleneck for carbon
production in red giant stars. Silicon is produced primarily in
oxygen burning and silicon burning phases of massive stars —
*neither requires the triple-alpha resonance*.

**Therefore:** The entire T3 carbon-production bound (α ±4%, αs ±0.5%)
is irrelevant for silicon life.

### Silicon stellar production

Silicon production requires:
1. Oxygen fusion (24O + γ → ⁴⁸Cr, etc.)
2. Silicon burning (photodisintegration rearrangement at ~3×10⁹ K)

Neither step has a resonance as narrow as the Hoyle state. The physics
is governed by statistical equilibrium, not a single energy level.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Literature support | Bains (2004) — chemical feasibility; no fine-tuning analysis |
| Physics robustness | High — silicon nucleosynthesis is well-understood |
| Effect on α bound | **Destroys** the narrow T3 bound |
| Effect on αs bound | **Destroys** the narrow T3 bound |
| Effect on overall case | **Critically weakens** — the narrowest bounds disappear |

## Part 2: Ammonia solvents

### Background

Ammonia (NH₃) is the most discussed alternative to water as a
biochemical solvent. It remains liquid at lower temperatures
(~-78°C to -33°C at 1 atm) and has similar solvation properties
to water. Liquid methane (CH₄) is also considered for Titan-like
environments.

### Bounds affected

Ammonia and methane solvents affect the **temperature range** for
life, which in turn affects:

| Constant | How affected |
|----------|-------------|
| α | Stellar surface temperature needed for liquid ammonia differs; the habitable zone moves inward/outward. No new fundamental bound — changes the *application* of existing α bounds |
| μ | μ affects molecular binding energies; ammonia hydrogen bonds are weaker than water's — changes the μ bound for stable molecular structures. **May narrow the effective μ range** |
| Λ | No change — Λ affects structure formation, not planetary temperatures |

### Key insight

Ammonia solvents change the *boundary conditions* for life, not the
*fundamental constants*. The bounds on α, αs, etc. are still
approximately the same — they just need to be applied at different
stellar distances.

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Significance | Moderate — useful to widen "habitable zone" but doesn't change fundamental bounds |
| Effect on fine-tuning | **Modest** — doesn't widen α or αs bounds |

## Part 3: Plasma-based / non-chemical intelligence

### Background

Some theorists have speculated about non-chemical information
processing:
- **Plasma life** (e.g., in stellar atmospheres): self-organizing
  electromagnetic structures
- **Magnetic monopole life**: hypothetical structures in magnetic
  fields
- **Dark matter life**: self-interacting dark matter could in
  principle form structures analogous to chemistry

### Literature assessment

| Proposal | Scientific status |
|----------|------------------|
| Plasma life | Speculative — no known mechanism for Darwinian evolution in plasma |
| Magnetic monopole life | Hypothetical — depends on monopole existence |
| Dark matter life | Highly speculative — no known interaction complexity sufficient for evolution |

### Why plasma life doesn't escape fine-tuning

Even if information processing could occur in plasma:
- Plasma requires electromagnetic forces — still depends on α
- Plasma requires ionization — depends on atomic binding energies
- Plasma structures require low-entropy environments — bound by
  cosmological initial conditions

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Scientific basis | Extremely weak — no known mechanism |
| Literature support | None in mainstream physics |
| Effect on fine-tuning | **None** — doesn't escape constant constraints |

## Part 4: Substrate-independent information processing

### Background

The most radical alternative: information processing doesn't require
matter at all, or could occur in any substrate. This is essentially
the position that "life = information processing."

### Arguments

**For:** If information processing can occur in any physical system
with sufficient complexity, then the constraints on chemistry may
not apply. A universe with different constants might still permit
computation in some other form.

**Against:**
- All known computation requires physical degrees of freedom
- Information processing requires energy dissipation (Landauer's
  principle) — constrains the thermodynamic environment
- Computation requires distinguishable states — requires some
  substrate with discrete degrees of freedom
- Without physical constraints, the claim is untestable

### Literature assessment

| Source | Position |
|--------|----------|
| No mainstream physics source | Substrate-independent life is not addressed in the fine-tuning literature |
| Tegmark (2014) "Our Mathematical Universe" | Consciousness may be substrate-independent, but requires computation |
| Penrose (1989) | Argues computation is physical — not substrate-independent |

### Verdict

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Scientific basis | Philosophical, not empirical |
| Literature support | None in the fine-tuning corpus |
| Effect on fine-tuning | **Speculative** — cannot be used to support or refute any claim |

## Part 5: The weakless universe (Harnik et al. 2006)

### Already documented in T300.6A

Harnik et al. shows a universe without weak interactions is habitable.
This is the only "alternative physics" with a published, peer-reviewed
calculation.

### Effect on fine-tuning

| Constant | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| v/M_Pl | **Destroys** the Agrawal v bound — weak interaction not necessary |
| α | Indirect effect — weak interactions involved in pp-chain; alternative fusion pathways exist |
| μ | No direct effect |

### Verdict

Already documented. The weakless universe is a published counterexample
showing the v/M_Pl bound is SM-specific.

## Part 7: Final verdict

1. **Silicon life is the only serious alternative** with a
   published chemical analysis, and it **destroys both T3 bounds
   (α, αs)** because the Hoyle state is irrelevant for silicon
   nucleosynthesis.

2. **Ammonia/methane solvents** don't change the fundamental bounds
   — only the habitable zone.

3. **Plasma life, dark matter life, and substrate-independent
   computation** are too speculative to be used as evidence either
   way.

4. **The weakless universe** (Harnik 2006) is a published
   counterexample that shows the v/M_Pl bound is SM-specific.

**Defensible conclusion:** If silicon-based complex life is possible,
the narrowest anthropic bounds (α ±4%, αs ±0.5%) are irrelevant.
The fine-tuning case then depends entirely on T1/T2 bounds, which
are much wider and do not signal fine-tuning.
