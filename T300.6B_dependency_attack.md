# T300.6B — Adversarial Dependency Attack

## Mission

Attack the T200.2 / T300.3 dependency graph. Show that it is
substantially wrong — either overestimating independence or missing
crucial couplings. If the graph is wrong, the effective DoF count
is wrong, and the probability products are wrong.

---

## Attack 1: The 4-DoF result may overstate independence

### The claim

T200.2 and T300.3 conclude that the 6-constant set decomposes into
**4 effective degrees of freedom**:

| Subsystem | Constants | Effective DoF |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| α | α | 1 (isolated) |
| αs–μ–αG triangle | αs, μ, αG | 1–2 |
| v/M_Pl | v/M_Pl | 1 |
| Λ | Λ | 1 |

### Attack 1A: The αs–μ–αG triangle is NOT 1 DoF — it's a leaky abstraction

| Parameter | True physical origin | Could it vary independently? |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------------|
| αs | g_s (QCD coupling) at high scale, run to Λ_QCD | Independent DoF |
| m_p | ~Λ_QCD from αs, with quark mass contributions | Mostly derived from αs |
| m_n - m_p | ~(md - mu) × αs-dependence + EM | **NOT purely αs** — depends on quark mass difference |
| μ = m_e/m_p | m_e from Higgs Yukawa ÷ m_p from αs | **Requires TWO inputs** |

**Attack:** The μ ratio is m_e/m_p. But m_e depends on the electron Yukawa
coupling y_e, not on αs. And m_p depends on both αs (via Λ_QCD) and the
light quark masses (m_u, m_d). The statement "μ is derived from αs" is
correct only if *all quark and lepton Yukawas are held fixed*. If Yukawas
vary (as they would in any landscape), μ becomes independent of αs.

**Evidence:** In the Standard Model, there are exactly 6 quark masses and
3 charged lepton masses. Each has its own Yukawa coupling. There is no
known physics that ties y_e to g_s.

**Severity:** CRITICAL

**Does the 4-DoF result survive?** Only if Yukawas are fixed. In a
landscape where Yukawas vary (string theory predicts they do), μ is NOT
derived from αs. The 4-DoF → 6-DoF. All probability products that
assume 4 DoF are too small by a factor of ~P(μ)^2 — which may be large
or small depending on μ's own bound (which doesn't exist — see T300.6A §6).

---

### Attack 1B: The αs–αG link via m_p² is correct — but irrelevant if G varies independently

| Link | Physical basis | Vulnerability |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| αs → m_p → αG | αG = G m_p² / (ħc) | Only *if* G is held fixed |
| If G varies independently | αG = G × m_p² | αs and αG are NOT linked — both depend on m_p |
| | | but m_p appears in αG only because αG is defined with m_p |

**Attack:** The "strong" link between αs and αG is a definitional artifact.
αG contains m_p². If you define a gravitational coupling that uses the
Planck mass instead of the proton mass (which is the more natural choice),
the αs–αG link weakens substantially.

The anthropically relevant question is: "Can the strength of gravity
relative to nuclear forces vary?" The answer is yes — by varying G
directly while keeping αs fixed. The T200.2 graph captures this as a
"strong" link because of the m_p² factor, but the *real* freedom is
whether G is a free parameter.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the existing analysis correctly notes this in
the T200.2 matrix (marking it as "S" for strong physical dependence).
But the 4-DoF count should note that αG has 1 DoF from G itself if G is
free.

---

### Attack 1C: The α–αs link misses GUT-scale correlations

| Scenario | Effective DoF | Reasoning |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| No GUT | 4 | α and αs independent |
| GUT with unified gauge coupling | 3 | α and αs from a single coupling at MGUT; running to low energy depends on GUT-scale thresholds |

**Attack:** The existing analysis mentions 3 DoF as a sensitivity case.
But a correct treatment must consider:
- If α and αs unify at GUT scale, they are NOT independent — changing
  the unified coupling changes BOTH at low energy through the same
  renormalization group running
- The relationship is not 1-to-1 (threshold corrections split them)
  but they are strongly correlated
- This means the α ±4% bound and αs ±0.5% bound are not independent
  constraints — the effective allowed region in (α, αs) space is
  an ellipse, not a rectangle

**Severity:** HIGH — the Damour & Donoghue (2008) result shows this
changes the effective bound. But only 1 source models this.

## Attack 2: Hidden couplings the graph misses

### Attack 2A: Λ × Q (primordial fluctuation amplitude)

The T200.2 matrix does not include Q (density fluctuation amplitude)
as a constant. But Q is anthropically constrained (Tegmark et al. 2006)
and is known to correlate with Λ for galaxy formation.

**If Q can vary while Λ is held fixed, the Λ anthropic bound changes.**
Piran & Jimenez (2023) show that higher Q permits earlier galaxy
formation, raising the allowable Λ.

**Impact:** The Λ bound may be systematically too tight because the
engine only considers Λ variation with other parameters fixed.
Simulations that vary Λ + Q jointly find Λ can be ~100× larger.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the engine's Λ bound may be tight by ~10².

---

### Attack 2B: The QCD scale (Λ_QCD) dependence on v

Agrawal et al. (1998) model Λ_QCD ∝ v^β (β ≈ 0.5 for certain
scenarios). This means:
- Changing v changes all hadron masses (m_p, m_n, m_π, etc.)
- This changes αs (through the running coupling at hadronic scale)
- This changes μ (through m_p)
- This changes αG (through m_p²)

**Impact:** The v/M_Pl bound is NOT isolated — changing v propagates
to at least 3 other constants through QCD threshold effects. The
T200.2 graph marks this as "W" (weak), but Agrawal's β coefficient
suggests it could be stronger.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the "weak" designation is probably correct
for v/M_Pl → αs propagation, but the existing engine treats v as
fully independent, which is wrong.

---

### Attack 2C: The μ–v link is stronger than marked

The T200.2 matrix labels μ ↔ v/M_Pl as "S†" (conditional strong).
The link is:
- m_e = y_e × v (electron mass from Higgs vev × Yukawa)
- μ = m_e / m_p = (y_e × v) / m_p

**If v changes and y_e is held fixed, m_e changes proportionally.**
This changes atomic physics (Bohr radius, binding energies) in ways
that propagate to chemistry.

**If y_e can also vary** (which is generic in string theory), then
μ becomes a function of TWO free parameters (y_e and v), and the
"conditional strong" label underestimates the independence of μ.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the existing S† label is acceptable if
Yukawas are fixed, but the graph should note this assumption.

## Attack 3: Overestimated independence for α

### The claim: α is genuinely isolated

T300.3 labels α as "genuinely independent" with no known dependencies
on other constants.

### Attack: α defines the electromagnetic coupling — EVERYTHING involving charged particles depends on it

While α may not be "derived from" other constants in the T200.2 sense,
it couples to:
- αs through loop corrections (EM renormalization of QCD — weak but
  non-zero)
- v/M_Pl through loop corrections to the Higgs potential (EM radiative
  corrections to v)
- Λ through vacuum polarization contributions to the running of α

**Severity:** LOW — these are genuine weak effects, but the "isolated"
designation is not 100% correct. The errors are <1% effects, too small
to affect anthropic conclusions.

## Attack 4: The DoF reduction argument is circular

### The logic flow

1. T200.2 computes DoF from known Standard Model relations
2. T300.3 uses 4 DoF to argue probability products are inflated
3. T300.5 multiplies probabilities across 4 groups

### The circularity

**If the constants are not, in fact, physically linked in a particular
theory (landscape), then the 4-DoF count is wrong.** The dependency
graph is based on SM relations — but the SM does not explain *why*
the constants have their values. If we're asking about variation of
constants in a landscape, the relevant question is what can vary
independently in that landscape, not what is related in the SM.

| Scenario | DoF | What varies |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| SM with fixed Yukawas | 4 | αs, α, v, Λ (μ and αG derived) |
| Landscape with all couplings free | 6+ | All 6 + Yukawas + CKM matrix + ... |
| String theory with flux compactifications | Hundreds | Any modulus can shift any coupling |

**Attack:** The 4-DoF count is a *Standard Model* result, not a
*theory of everything* result. If we adopt an agnostic stance about
what can vary, we must use 6 DoF (or more). The 4-DoF count
systematically amplifies fine-tuning probabilities by a factor of
~P(μ) × P(αG) — which may be large or small depending on bounds.

**Severity:** CRITICAL — this is a foundational issue. The 4-DoF
count is correct *within the Standard Model* but may not be correct
for the landscape question the analysis is trying to answer.

## Attack 5: The Dependency Matrix CSV has an arithmetic error

### The effective DoF column

T300.3_dependency_matrix.csv assigns each source an `independent_count`
and `effective_count`. Several sources are assigned `effective_count = 3`
when they analyze all 6 constants OaA (Barrow & Tipler, Rees, Hogan,
Lewis & Barnes).

**Attack:** The reduction from 6 to 3 for OaA sources is based on the
4-DoF model (6 → 4 → rounded to 3 for the triangle). But this assigns
αs, μ, and αG as 1 DoF when the source itself treated them as 3.
This is a correct *physics correction* but incorrect *evidence
weighting* — the source's calculation assumed independence, and
correcting that changes the result.

The engine should include BOTH the source's raw probability (6 DoF)
AND the corrected probability (4 DoF) to show the difference.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the engine already supports both models.

### Final Verdict

The **4-DoF reduction is defensible** if and only if we accept the
Standard Model assumption that Yukawa couplings and G are fixed. In a
generic landscape, the effective DoF is ≥6, and the 4-DoF probability
product systematically overstates fine-tuning by a factor that depends
on the μ and αG bounds.

The T300.5 engine correctly supports both models. The primary result
should use 6 DoF (Model A) as the conservative (hardest-to-destroy)
baseline, with 4 DoF (Model B) as a physics-informed sensitivity case.
