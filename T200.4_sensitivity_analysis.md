# T200.4 — Sensitivity Analysis

**Program:** T200 — Independent Fine-Tuning Analysis
**Phase:** 4 (sensitivity analysis — test every assumption class)
**Cross-reference:** T200.0 (definitions), T200.1 (bounds), T200.2 (dependencies), T200.3 (probabilities)
**Key question:** *"Which assumptions dominate the probability estimates, and how stable are the results under alternative published positions?"*

---

## Rules for This Phase

1. No new bounds or literature. All inputs come from T200.1. All dependency models come from T200.2. All baseline probabilities come from T200.3.
2. Each sensitivity class is analyzed independently (one-at-a-time variation of assumption classes), then the joint effect of multiple simultaneous changes is reported in the synthesis.
3. Results are reported as multiplicative factors relative to the T200.3 baseline (Model B, log measure, Tier 3).
4. Sensitivity is classified as:
   - **Negligible:** Factor < 2× from baseline
   - **Moderate:** Factor 2–100×
   - **Large:** Factor 100–10¹⁰×
   - **Dominant:** Factor > 10¹⁰×
   - **Catastrophic:** Result goes to 0 or ∞ (model collapses)
5. Constants with "?" or "∞" in T200.3 carry exactly that uncertainty into every sensitivity scenario.

## Sensitivity Class B — Dependency Sensitivity

**Question:** *How much does the result change depending on how many effective degrees of freedom exist?*

### B.1 Recasting T200.3 in Sensitivity Terms

T200.3 considered three dependency models:
- **Model A (T200.3 §5.1):** 6 independent parameters — multiply all per-constant probabilities
- **Model B (T200.3 §5.2):** 4 effective DoF — group the αs–μ–α_G subsystem, use most restrictive bound
- **Model C (T200.3 §5.3):** Propagated through coupled subsystem — converges to Model B

The difference between Model A and Model B is:
- Under Model A: αs AND μ AND α_G each contribute separate improbability → product is αs × μ × α_G
- Under Model B/C: αs drives the subsystem → 1 contribution (the most restrictive bound dominates)

### B.2 Quantitative Comparison (Tier 3, Log Measure)

| Dependency scenario | DoF count | Formula | P | Factor vs. Model A |
|--------------------|-----------|---------|---|-------------------|
| **6 independent (Model A, excl. μ)** | 5 | α(T3) × αs(T3) × α_G(T3) × v(T3) × Λ(T2) | 2.9×10⁻⁴ × 3.7×10⁻⁵ × 0.033 × 0.0037 × 0.048 = **6.3×10⁻¹⁴** | 1× (baseline for A) |
| **4 effective DoF (Model B)** | 4 | α(T3) × [αs(T3)] × v(T3) × Λ(T2) | 2.9×10⁻⁴ × 3.7×10⁻⁵ × 0.0037 × 0.048 = **1.9×10⁻¹²** | **30× larger** |
| **Coupled (Model C)** | 4 | same as B | ~1.9×10⁻¹² | ~1× |
| **GUT-linked (α + αs)** | 3 | [α minimum] × v(T3) × Λ(T2) | 2.9×10⁻⁴ × 0.0037 × 0.048 = **5.1×10⁻⁸** | **810,000× larger** |
| **Maximal coupling (all linked)** | 2 | [most restrictive] × Λ(T2) | 3.7×10⁻⁵ × 0.048 = **1.8×10⁻⁶** | **28 million × larger** |

**Critical finding:** The dependency assumption changes the result by **31 million ×** — from 6.3×10⁻¹⁴ (Model A, 6 DoF) to 1.8×10⁻⁶ (maximal coupling, 2 DoF). This is a larger swing than any bound-disagreement sensitivity.

### B.3 Why This Matters

The dependency structure determines which probabilities are multiplied. Every multiplication compounds the improbability. The difference between:

- "5 independent constants, each tight" → ~10⁻¹³
- "2 independent constants, each loose" → ~10⁻⁶

is not a refinement — it is a **different answer**.

The truth is almost certainly closer to Model B (4 DoF) than Model A (6 independent). The αs–μ–α_G coupling is well-established QCD. But the GUT-linked and maximal-coupling scenarios are physically plausible if the unification scale is accessible.

### B.4 Sensitivity Matrix

| DoF count | Physical scenario | Min P | Max P | Swing |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|-------|-------|
| 6 (Model A) | No dependencies at all | 6×10⁻¹⁴ | ∞ (if μ unbound) | ∞ |
| 5 (Model A, excl. μ) | Only μ independent | 6×10⁻¹⁴ | 6×10⁻¹⁴ (only if μ excluded) | 1× |
| 4 (Model B/C) | QCD subsystem coupled | 1.9×10⁻¹² | 1.9×10⁻¹² | 1× |
| 3 (GUT-linked) | α unifies with αs | 5.1×10⁻⁸ | 5.1×10⁻⁸ | 1× |
| 2 (maximal coupling) | Everything linked to one parameter | 1.8×10⁻⁶ | 1.8×10⁻⁶ | 1× |

**Verdict:** Dependency sensitivity is **large** (factor ~10⁷ from worst case to best case within plausible scenarios). This is the second-largest uncapped uncertainty after observer selection.

## Sensitivity Class C — Measure Sensitivity

**Question:** *How much does the probability change depending on which probability measure is chosen?*

### C.1 The Four Measures from T200.0

| Measure | Formula | Primary issue | When it dominates |
|---------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| **A — Linear** | P(x) = 1/(x_max − x_min) | Pathological for constants << 1 | Never, for small-valued constants |
| **B — Log** | P(log x) = 1/(log x_max − log x_min) | Sensitive to lower bound choice | Preferred range |
| **C — Observer-weighted** | P(x) ∝ N_obs(x) | N_obs(x) not computable | Cannot use |
| **D — Agnostic** | "No probability assigned" | Does not answer the question | Always honest |

### C.2 Measure Comparison (Tier 3, Model B, Narrow Bounds)

| Measure | α P | αs P | α_G P | v/M_Pl P | Λ P |
|---------|-----|------|-------|---------|-----|
| **Log (B)** | 2.9×10⁻⁴ | 3.7×10⁻⁵ | 0.033 | 0.0037 | 0.048 |
| **Linear (A)** | 5.9×10⁻⁴ | 0.0012 | ~10⁻³⁷† | ~10⁻¹⁷† | ~10⁻¹¹⁸† |
| **Ratio (Linear/Log)** | 2.0× | 32× | **3×10³⁶×** | **4.5×10¹⁵×** | **2.5×10¹¹⁷×** |

† = Prior-range-dominated artifact (see T200.3 §4 — Log-Linear Pathology)

### C.3 Joint Probability Comparison (Tier 3, Model B)

| Measure | Narrow (Agrawal v, Livio αs) | Wide (Harnik v) |
|---------|-----------------------------|-----------------|
| **Log (B)** | 2.9×10⁻⁴ × 3.7×10⁻⁵ × 0.0037 × 0.048 = **1.9×10⁻¹²** | **?** (∞ from Harnik) |
| **Linear (A)** | 5.9×10⁻⁴ × 0.0012 × 10⁻¹⁷ × 10⁻¹¹⁸ = **~10⁻¹⁷²†** | **?** |
| **Ratio (Linear/Log)** | **~10¹⁶⁰** | — |
| **Agnostic (D)** | "Cannot assign probability" | "Cannot assign probability" |

### C.4 What Drives the Linear Measure Sensitivity

The linear measure produces a probability of ~10⁻¹⁷² for Tier 3. But **99.999...% of this improbability comes from two constants**: α_G and Λ, whose small observed values (~10⁻³⁹ and ~10⁻¹²²) force the linear measure to report them as "extremely unlikely" even though their anthropic bounds are orders of magnitude wider than their observed values.

The linear measure is not measuring fine-tuning — it is measuring the distance from the Planck scale.

### C.5 Measure Sensitivity Summary

| Measure comparison | Factor | Meaning |
|--------------------|--------|---------|
| Log vs. Linear (α_G or Λ) | **10³⁶–10¹¹⁷×** | Measure choice dominates for small-valued constants |
| Log vs. Linear (α or αs) | **2–32×** | Measure choice is mild for O(0.01–0.1) constants |
| Linear vs. Agnostic | **∞** (no P assigned) | Agnostic rejects the question entirely |
| Observer-weighted vs. any | **Unknown, unbounded** | Can go to 0 or ∞ until N_obs(x) is computable |

**Verdict:** Measure sensitivity is **dominant** for the three small-valued constants (α_G, v/M_Pl, Λ) and **negligible-to-moderate** for the O(0.01–0.1) constants (α, αs). The overall result is measure-dominated.

   ┃                    ┃

Each tier adds **different** constants (Tier 2 adds α_G and Λ; Tier 3 tightens α and αs dramatically). The ~10⁶ drop from Tier 2 to Tier 3 comes entirely from tightening α (4% → 0.5 decades) and αs (0.5% → 0.0045 decades).

### D.4 Sensitivity to What "Life" Means

| Life definition | P (Model B, Log) | Factor from Tier 3 | What changed |
|----------------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------|

| Non-carbon life | **?** | Potentially **∞** | No bound exists for non-carbon biochemistry |

**Critical finding:** The life-definition sensitivity is **dominant** (factor ~10⁸ between Tier 1 and Tier 3). This is driven by the Livio α and αs carbon bounds. If the question is "what's the probability that our universe supports *some* form of life" (Tier 1), the answer is ~10⁻⁶. If it's "carbon-based life" (Tier 3), it's ~10⁻¹².

### D.5 The Tier-4 Gap

No independent bounds exist for Tier 4 (information processing). All constraints are inherited from Tier 3. This means the fine-tuning argument itself cannot distinguish between "carbon chemistry is necessary for complex information processing" and "carbon chemistry is sufficient but not necessary." If non-carbon information processors are possible, Tier 4 reverts to Tier 1 or Tier 2 bounds — widening the result by 10⁶–10⁸×.

## Sensitivity Class E — Literature-Disagreement Sensitivity

**Question:** *Which conclusions are robust across papers and which collapse under alternative published assumptions?*

### E.1 The Four Disagreements

The T200.1 literature survey identified exactly four fundamental disagreements among published sources:

| Disagreement | Authors disagreeing | What's at stake | Resolution status |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **E1:** αs diproton bound | Davies (1993) 2% vs. MacDonald & Mullan (2009) 50% | Does αs need to be within 2% or 50% of observed? | **Relevance-limited** — Tier 3 carbon bound is stricter than either |
| **E2:** v/M_Pl constraint | Agrawal et al. (1998) narrow vs. Harnik et al. (2006) none | Is the weak scale anthropically constrained? | **Unresolved** — contradictory findings from equally credible methods |
| **E3:** μ existence of any bound | Every source vs. no source | Does any quantitative bound exist for μ? | **Genuine gap** — no paper claims one; popular books assert without citation |
| **E4:** Λ bound precision | Weinberg (1987) vs. Barnes (2012) vs. Piran & Jimenez (2023) | Does Λ need to be small? | **Quantitatively resolved** — agreement within factor ~4 |

### E.2 Scenario Analysis — Robust vs. Fragile Results

#### Result: "α is fine-tuned to ~4% for carbon" — ROBUST

| Challenge | Effect on result |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Alternative stellar models (Adams) | Widens to ~factor 10 — still fine-tuned |
| Different carbon threshold | Some sensitivity but ~4% is stable |
| Non-carbon life | Irrelevant — bound applies only to carbon |

**Verdict:** The 4% α bound for carbon production (Livio 1989) is **well-established** and not seriously contested. It survives all literature disagreements.

#### Result: "αs is fine-tuned to ~0.5% for carbon" — ROBUST

| Challenge | Effect on result |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Diproton controversy (E1) | **No effect** — carbon bound is independent |
| MacDonald (50%) | No effect on Tier 3 |
| Non-carbon life | Irrelevant — bound applies only to carbon |

**Verdict:** Despite the diproton controversy, the αs carbon bound is **uncontested** and survives.

#### Result: "The fine-tuning probability is ~10⁻¹²" — FRAGILE

| Challenge | Effect on result | Classification |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| μ excluded (baseline) | 1.9×10⁻¹² | Baseline |
| Agrawal → Harnik (E2) | **∞ (model collapses)** | Catastrophic |
| μ bound exists (E3) | Unknown — depends on bound | Potentially catastrophic |
| μ bound never exists | P = 1 for μ | Model changes |
| GUT linking (E4 resolution) | 5.1×10⁻⁸ (810,000× larger) | Large |
| Maximal dependency | 1.8×10⁻⁶ (28M× larger) | Large |
| Observer weighting | Unknown, unbounded | Potentially catastrophic |

#### Result: "The probability is dominated by the carbon bounds" — ROBUST

Across all scenarios, when the result is finite, the α and αs Tier 3 bounds contribute >99% of the total improbability. This holds for every measure, every dependency model, and every tier where carbon is relevant.

#### Result: "Λ is fine-tuned" — MISLEADING AS STATED

| Claim | Reality |
|-------|---------|
| "Λ is fine-tuned to 1 part in 10¹²⁰" | The anthropic bound (Weinberg) is ~10¹¹⁸, giving P_Λ ~ 0.048 under log measure — not fine-tuned at all |
| "The 10¹²⁰ is the QFT naturalness problem" | **Correct.** The 10¹²⁰ claim conflates anthropic bounds with QFT naturalness |
| "Λ is the most fine-tuned constant" | **False** under any measure that avoids the log-linear pathology. αs at 0.5% is ~10⁴× more restrictive |

### E.4 Results That Survive All Literature Disagreements

1. **α and αs are the dominant contributors** to whatever fine-tuning signal exists in the carbon tier. Every finite scenario has this structure.

2. **μ has no quantitative anthropic bound.** This is not a disputed claim — it is a gap in the literature that every surveyed source implicitly acknowledges by not providing one.

3. **The diproton controversy (2% vs. 50%) does not affect the strongest fine-tuning signal** because the carbon bound (0.5%) is already stricter than either.

4. **Λ contributes negligibly** to the joint probability under any defensible measure (P_Λ ~ 0.03–0.05).

5. **The linear measure produces unusable results** for any constant whose value is more than ~2 orders of magnitude below its prior upper bound.

### E.5 Results That Collapse Under Literature Disagreement

1. **Any joint probability that includes v/M_Pl** is fragile — switching from Agrawal to Harnik sends the result to ∞.

2. **Any single-number probability** (e.g., "1 in 10¹²") collapses under measure and dependency sensitivity, which together span a range of >10¹⁶⁰.

3. **Any claim about μ being fine-tuned** has zero empirical support. No quantitative bound exists anywhere in the surveyed literature.

4. **The "0.1% change destroys the universe" claim** has no derivation in any paper surveyed by T200.1. The Livio bounds (4% for α, 0.5% for αs) are the tightest quantitative anthropic bounds found — and even those are orders of magnitude wider than 0.1%.
