## 3. Baseline Vulnerability Summary

A prediction's ability to be tested independently of SFH metaphysics depends on the status of its materialist baseline.

| Baseline Status | Count | Predictions Affected |
|-----------------|-------|---------------------|
| **Established** (standard physics value, independently verifiable) | 3 | N35 (Bell S≤2), N36 (GP equation), N46 (QCD lifetime) |
| **Asserted** (no citation, no derivation) | 13 | N33, N34, N37, N38, N39, N40, N41, N42, N43, N44, N47, N48, N57-N59 |
| **Analogy** (not a quantitative claim) | 2 | N51 (murmuration), N61-N62 (consciousness) |

**Key finding:** 13 of 18 predictions depend on materialist baselines that are asserted, not derived. The 3 predictions with established baselines (N35, N36, N46) test standard physics, not SFH — any measured deviation is already being investigated by standard science. SFH's only addition is the interpretive label "this means the SF is optimizing."

## 4. Structural Observation

All 18 predictions share a single logical form:

> **SFH predicts that MeasuredValue > BaselineValue, where the excess is attributed to sentient field optimization.**

The prediction is never:
- A specific numeric value derived from SFH axioms
- A relationship between two constants derived from fiber bundle parameters
- A cross-system correlation predicted by a unified equation
- A new phenomenon that standard theories do not anticipate

Every prediction is a **threshold-excess claim** over a materialist baseline that SFH does not independently derive.

This is the central vulnerability across all 18 predictions: without an independent SFH derivation of the baseline, deviations are unfalsifiable — one can always adjust the baseline to match observation.
