Q1: Do competing propagation structures destabilize amplification?
A1: YES - Competition reduces amplification efficiency.
    Dual topology shows larger magnitude loss than single topology.
    Mixed system shows intermediate behavior.

Q2: Do competing topologies alter localization boundaries?
A2: YES - Localization depends on competition level (alpha).
    Alpha < 0.75: localization preserved
    Alpha = 1.0 (pure ER): localization lost

Q3: Do propagation manifolds coexist stably?
A3: YES - Dual topology creates distinct manifold.
    Variance across k: > 0.005
    Neither pure A nor pure B
    Zero variance across seeds

Q4: Do dominant propagation regimes emerge?
A4: YES - All-to-all DOMINATES under competition.
    Mixed geometry closer to AA (-0.010) than ER (-0.033)
    High-efficiency topology suppresses low-efficiency

Q5: Do saturation boundaries survive competition?
A5: YES (shifted) - Saturation boundary survives but shifts earlier.
    Single: k_s ≈ 0.6
    Dual: k_s ≈ 0.4
    Shift of 0.2, but boundary still present
