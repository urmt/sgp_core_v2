# T700.5 — Experimental Feasibility

**Status:** Complete
**Method:** For predictions that survive T700.3 (Novelty) and T700.4 (Falsifiability), assess whether current or near-future technology can test them.

---

## 1. Feasibility Criteria

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Feasible today** | Existing instruments, facilities, and methods can test the prediction |
| **Feasible with existing data** | Required data already exists; only novel analysis is needed |
| **Near-future feasible** | Requires new instruments/facilities expected within 10 years |
| **Not currently feasible** | Required technology does not exist and is not imminent |

---

## 2. Prediction-by-Prediction Assessment

### N35 — Photon Entanglement (C ≥ 1.3)

**Experiment:** Bell/CHSH test measuring S parameter.

**Feasibility:** Feasible today. Bell tests are undergraduate-level quantum optics. Multiple groups worldwide perform them routinely.

**Verdict:** Feasible today — but tests QM, not SFH. No new experiment is needed. The standard prediction (S > 2 for entangled states) is already confirmed to high precision. Running another Bell test adds nothing.

---

### N36 — BEC Coherence (C ≥ 1.3)

**Experiment:** Measure condensate fraction, coherence length, or interference pattern in a BEC.

**Feasibility:** Feasible today. BECs are produced in hundreds of labs worldwide. Standard diagnostics (time-of-flight imaging, interference) are mature.

**Verdict:** Feasible today — but tests GP equation, not SFH. BEC properties match standard theory. Any deviation would already be a major discovery in AMO physics.

---

### N40 / N44 — Cross-Scale Correlations (R ≥ 0.85, C ≥ 1.4)

**Experiment:** Measure a quantum observable (e.g., entanglement fidelity in a lab), a biological observable (e.g., neural synchrony), and a cosmic observable (e.g., galaxy cluster density), then compute correlation R between them.

**Feasibility assessment:**

- **Quantum measurements:** Feasible today. Single-lab quantum optics experiments.
- **Neural measurements:** Feasible today. EEG/fMRI recordings are standard.
- **Cosmic measurements:** Feasible today. SDSS, DESI, Euclid survey data is publicly available.
- **Correlation analysis:** Requires careful control for confounders (time of day, solar activity, seasonal effects, etc.). The statistical challenge is separating a real correlation from chance given the tiny sample size (N=1 universe) or unknown systematic coupling.

**Critical issue:** The correlation is across fundamentally different systems measured at different times and places. Earth-based experiments cannot control for all possible confounding variables. A correlation between lab quantum measurements and galaxy survey data would be scientifically extraordinary and would require extraordinary evidence.

**Verdict:** Potentially feasible with existing data, but the statistical and systematic challenges are severe. No standard physics predicts such correlations, so establishing them convincingly would require pre-registered analysis protocols and independent replication.

---

### N43 — AI-Human Coherence Deviation (ΔC ≥ 0.1)

**Experiment:** Measure some coherence metric (e.g., neural synchrony, behavioral consistency, response variability) in humans performing a task with and without AI assistance.

**Feasibility:**

- **Protocol design:** Straightforward. Existing HCI research methods apply.
- **Measurement:** EEG, behavioral tracking, response time analysis are all standard.
- **Baseline:** "No interaction" baseline (ΔC = 0) is well-defined.

**Critical issue:** The coherence metric C is not specifically defined for this domain by SFH-SGP. The book mentions ΔC but doesn't specify which observable to measure. An experimenter would need to define C operationally, which means the experiment tests the experimenter's definition of C, not SFH-SGP's.

**Verdict:** Feasible today, but requires operationalizing C for HCI — a definitional choice the book does not provide.

---

### N46 — Toponium Lifetime Deviation (Δτ ≥ 10⁻²⁶ s)

**Experiment:** Produce toponium (tt̅ bound state) and measure its lifetime; compare to QCD prediction.

**Feasibility assessment:**

| Requirement | Status | Timeline |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Collider with √s > 346 GeV | LHC reaches 13 TeV pp, but toponium production in pp collisions has enormous QCD background | Current |
| Clean toponium production | e⁺e⁻ collider at √s ≈ 350 GeV preferred | FCC-ee: ~2045+ |
| Toponium identification | Requires reconstruction of tt̅ decay products | Challenging at hadron colliders |
| Lifetime measurement | Requires vertex resolution ~10 μm or better | Available at LHCb/ATLAS/CMS |
| QCD prediction for comparison | NLO/NNLO calculations exist for top quark width; bound state effects are more uncertain | Research active |

**Critical physics issue:** The top quark decays via t → Wb with width Γt ≈ 1.5 GeV, corresponding to τ ≈ 4×10⁻²⁵ s. This is ~40× shorter than the claimed deviation Δτ = 10⁻²⁶ s. Toponium would only form if the binding energy exceeds the width — a condition that is marginally satisfied (binding energy ≈ Γt). The very existence of toponium is an open question.

**Verdict:** Not feasible at current facilities. May become feasible at future e⁺e⁻ colliders (FCC-ee, ILC, CLIC) in the 2040s. Even then, the SFH deviation (10⁻²⁶ s) is small relative to the total width (~4×10⁻²⁵ s), requiring ~1% precision on lifetime measurement — extremely challenging.

### N41 / N57-N59 — Dark Energy/Dark Matter Coherence

**Experiment:** Search for non-uniform structure or "coherence patterns" in dark energy distribution (e.g., via weak lensing, galaxy cluster counts, or supernova residuals) and dark matter distribution (via gravitational lensing, galaxy rotation curves).

**Feasibility:**

- **Dark energy:** DESI, Euclid, Rubin LSST are already mapping large-scale structure and measuring DE. Any anomalous pattern would show up in standard analyses.
- **Dark matter:** DM distribution is already mapped via gravitational lensing. Any "coherence pattern" would be visible.
- **SFH-specific prediction:** The coherence percentages (70.6%–89.5% for DE, 23.5%–38.9% for DM) depend on C = 0.5 baseline. Without independent baseline derivation, any measurement can be adjusted to fit.

**Verdict:** Feasible today — but the existing cosmological data already constrains DE/DM to be consistent with ΛCDM at current precision. No anomalous "coherence pattern" has been reported. The SFH claim can be checked against existing survey data, but the adjustable baseline makes the test non-falsifying.

### N33, N34, N37, N38, N39, N42, N47, N48 — All Threshold-Excess

**General assessment:** All are feasible today because they measure already-studied phenomena. However, all are non-novel (T700.3) and unfalsifiable (T700.4). Feasibility is irrelevant because no test would distinguish SFH-SGP from standard theories.
