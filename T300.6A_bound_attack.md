# T300.6A — Adversarial Bound Attack

## Mission

Attack every bound in the database. For each, determine whether it survives
when subjected to: source credibility checks, independent replication status,
contradictory literature, methodology limitations, and consensus assessment.

---

## Section 1 — The Strongest Bound: α (Livio et al. 1989, T3)

### Attack 1A: The Livio α bound depends on a single un-replicated stellar code

| Property | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Original source | Livio et al. (1989), *Nature* 340, 281–284 |
| Methodology | Stellar reaction network for helium burning |
| Code used | Not publicly available (1988-era Fortran) |
| Independent replication? | **None.** Oberhummer et al. (2000) performed a *different* nuclear calculation that broadly agrees, but no independent group has reproduced the Livio ±4% threshold exactly |
| Simulation status | Analytic + one code; 0 independent replications |

**Attack:** A ±4% bound on α rests on a single 1989 paper whose code is not
available, whose nuclear reaction network is not independently validated,
and whose result no one has ever independently reproduced. This is a
****weak evidence foundation**** for the most widely cited anthropic bound
in the literature.

**Severity:** HIGH

**Does the conclusion survive?** The existence of some α sensitivity at
~10% level is multiply confirmed (Barrow & Tipler T1 gives 0.001–0.1;
Oberhummer gives ~0.06 MeV on nuclear energy). The *specific* ±4% bound
is poorly replicated. The weaker factor-100 bound survives; the
headline ±4% has weaker support than commonly claimed.

---

### Attack 1B: The Livio α bound assumes the Hoyle state is the only pathway to carbon

| Property | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Claim | C/O > 0.1 requires α within ±4% |
| Hidden assumption | The triple-alpha process via the Hoyle state is the *only* way to produce significant carbon |
| Contradiction | Alternative fusion pathways exist in non-standard parameter regimes. If α changes, the Gamow peak moves and *different* resonances can contribute |

**Attack:** The Livio calculation varies α while keeping all other nuclear
properties fixed. In reality, changing α changes the Coulomb barrier, which
changes stellar temperatures at helium burning, which changes which
resonances dominate. A fixed C/O ratio requirement does not imply a fixed
Hoyle state energy requirement — it implies a more complex constraint
that the Livio code may not capture.

**Severity:** MODERATE

**Does the conclusion survive?** The bound is weakened but not destroyed.
The physics is sound: changing α shifts Coulomb barriers and resonance
energies. The *numerical value* of ±4% is less certain than typically
claimed.

---

### Attack 1C: Symmetry assumption is untested

| Property | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| Livio α bound | ±4% (symmetric) |
| Tested asymmetry? | **No.** The paper computed ±4% bounds. Did not compute asymmetric bounds. |
| Physical reason for asymmetry | α increases binding for both ¹²C and ¹⁶O differently; C/O ratio could degrade asymmetrically |

**Attack:** There is no physical reason to expect the α bound to be
symmetric. The difference in Coulomb repulsion between triple-α (3×2e)
and ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O (6e×2e) suggests an asymmetric window — but no one has
computed it. The ±4% window is a simple computational output, not a
physical law.

**Severity:** MODERATE

## Section 2 — The Carbon Bound: αs (Livio et al. 1989, MacDonald & Mullan 2009, Oberhummer et al. 2000)

### Attack 2A: Two contradictory bounds for αs — which is correct?

| Bound | Source | Range | Confidence |
|-------|--------|-------|------------|
| αs (carbon, T3) | Livio et al. 1989 | ±0.5% | Moderate |
| αs (BBN, T2) | MacDonald & Mullan 2009 | Up to ~+50% | Firm |
| αs (deuterium, T1) | Barrow & Tipler 1986 | ~±20% | Moderate |

**Attack:** The literature contains three incompatible αs bounds:
- A *narrow* ±0.5% bound (Livio, carbon production)
- A *wide* +50% bound (MacDonald & Mullan, BBN hydrogen survival)
- A *moderate* ±20% bound (Barrow & Tipler, deuterium binding)

These are not independent constraints — they apply to different criteria
(T1 vs T3). The engine handles this correctly by tiering. However, the
Livio ±0.5% bound has *never been independently replicated* and the
MacDonald & Mullan simulation suggests it may be too strict.

**Severity:** HIGH — the αs Tier 3 bound is the narrowest in the corpus
and has the weakest replication support.

**Attack:** MacDonald & Mullan (2009) used a modern BBN network to show
that the strong force can increase by up to ~50% before hydrogen is
eliminated. Their simulation is the *only* modern replication attempt of
the earlier αs BBN claims. If a 50% αs increase still permits hydrogen,
what does this imply for the 0.5% carbon bound? The answer is unclear —
they are different physical processes — but the discrepancy raises
questions about the reliability of single-code results.

**Severity:** HIGH

---

### Attack 2B: The diproton catastrophe was a myth

| Claim | Source | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| "2% stronger strong force → diproton → no hydrogen → no life" | Davies (1993), widely repeated | **REFUTED** |
| Actual bound | MacDonald & Mullan (2009) | ~50% increase needed |
| Correct | Journalists, popularizers | This claim is still circulating |

**Attack:** The most famous "fine-tuning" example in popular culture is
flatly wrong. The 2% figure is not physically grounded. This damages
credibility of all similar claims, even those with better evidence.

**Does the conclusion survive?** The Tier 1 (BBN deuterium) bound survives
independently at ~±20%. The Tier 1 bound does not depend on the
diproton claim. But the *popular* conclusion is substantially weakened.

**Severity:** MODERATE (for Tier 1); NONE (for the refutation is already
documented in T300.2)

## Section 3 — The αG Bound: Order-of-Magnitude Only

### Attack 3A: The αG bound is an artifact of analytic approximations

| Bound | Source | Range | Span |
|-------|--------|-------|------|
| Carr & Rees (1979) | Analytic scaling | ~10⁻⁴⁵ to 10⁻³⁶ | ~10⁹ |
| Adams (2019) | Stellar models | ~5.9×10⁻⁴⁰ to 5.9×10⁻³⁸ | ~10² |
| Stenger (2011) | MC with permissive criterion | ~0 to 1 | ~10³⁹ |

**Attack:** The αG bound is the *weakest* anthropic constraint by far.
Carr & Rees span ~10⁹, Adams narrows to ~10² for stellar lifetime.
The "fine-tuning" of gravity is an artifact of:
1. Using linear measure (pathological — see T300.6C)
2. Setting the prior upper bound at 1 (Planck scale) — which guarantees
   a tiny probability because αG is so small
3. Ignoring that the Carr & Rees bounds are derived from analytic scaling
   relations, not from any simulation of actual star/galaxy formation

**Severity:** MODERATE (weak bound but correct physics)

**Does the conclusion survive?** The αG bound is correctly documented as
wide. The engine properly flags linear measure as pathological for αG.
No destruction needed — the bound is self-limiting.

---

### Attack 3B: The Adams αG stellar lifetime bound uses one stellar model

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Adams (2019) bound | αG within factor ~10 of observed for >1 Gyr stellar lifetime |
| Code used | MESA (Modules for Experiments in Stellar Astrophysics) — one code |
| Variations tested | Stars of one initial mass (solar-type)? |
| Metallicity dependence | Not explored |
| Rotation, magnetic fields | Not included |

**Attack:** The Adams αG bound is the best available but still rests on
a single stellar evolution code run with limited parameter exploration.
The factor ~10 is an estimate, not a hard threshold.

**Severity:** LOW — the bound is already labeled as "Moderate" confidence
with wide range in the database.

## Section 4 — The v/M_Pl Bound: The Weakless Universe Counterexample

### Attack: Harnik et al. (2006) shows the weak interaction is not required

| Source | Claim |
|--------|-------|
| Agrawal et al. (1998) | v/M_Pl must be within 10⁻¹⁸ to 5×10⁻¹⁷ for life (BBN, pp-chain) |
| Harnik et al. (2006) | A universe *with no weak interaction* is still habitable |
| Resolution | The Agrawal bound is *not* an anthropic necessity — it's a *sufficient condition* for life-as-we-know-it |

**Attack:** The entire v/M_Pl bound is contingency-based:
- The lower bound (weak interactions too strong) assumes the Standard
  Model particle content with fixed Yukawa couplings
- The upper bound (weak interactions too weak) assumes the pp-chain
  is needed for stellar fusion
- If the weak interaction is absent, deuterium and pp-chain are irrelevant
  — stars can fuse through other pathways (Harnik shows gravitational
  contraction + alternative fusion suffices)

**Severity:** CRITICAL for the "v/M_Pl is fine-tuned" claim

**Does the conclusion survive?** The Agrawal bound is a valid bound
*for universes with the Standard Model particle content*. It is not
an anthropic bound on v/M_Pl *simpliciter*. The Harnik counterexample
demonstrates that the bound is model-dependent, not fundamental.

## Section 5 — The Λ Bound: The Most Tested, But Still Uncertain

### Attack 5A: The Weinberg bound was analytic — N-body confirmation is mixed

| Source | Method | Outcome |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Weinberg (1987) | Linear perturbation theory | Λ < 10⁻¹¹⁸ |
| Tegmark et al. (2006) | N-body simulation | Λ < ~10⁻¹²⁰ (for Milky-Way-like) |
| Barnes (2012) | Analytic | Λ < 10⁻¹¹⁶ (for any structure) |
| Piran & Jimenez (2023) | N-body simulation | Λ can vary by ~100× and still form galaxies |

**Attack:** The best-simulated bound (Tegmark) is ~100× tighter than the
most permissive bound (Barnes). Piran & Jimenez find that the Λ
constraint is weaker than Weinberg thought because galaxies can form
even with earlier Λ-domination if density fluctuations are higher.
The Λ bound is real but its *numerical value* varies by ~10⁴ between
the tightest and most permissive estimates.

**Severity:** MODERATE — the existence of a Λ bound is robust; the
numerical value is not.

---

### Attack 5B: No lower bound exists for Λ

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| All Λ bounds | Upper bounds only |
| Lower bound | None — Λ could be zero or negative |
| Implication | For log-uniform measure, P(Λ life-permitting) depends entirely on where you set the prior lower bound |

**Attack:** With no lower bound, the probability calculus for Λ is
indeterminate. If the total range is 124 decades (10⁻¹²⁴ to 1), the
life-permitting fraction is ~4/124 ≈ 3%. If the range is 200 decades,
it is ~4/200 ≈ 2%. But if Λ can be *negative* (AdS vacua), the range
is truly unbounded below, and the probability → 0.

**Severity:** MODERATE — acknowledged in T300.4 but not resolvable

## Section 6 — The μ Bound: The Gap

### Attack: No published μ bound exists for carbon-based life

| Bound | Citation | Detail |
|-------|----------|--------|
| μ (Tier 2) | Adams (2019) | Qualitative: wide factor ~100 |
| μ (Tier 3) | **NONE** | **Genuine gap in the literature** |
| Stenger (2011) | MC | Puts no meaningful bound (~1–10) |
| Popular claims | Various | Often conflate μ with α or αs |

**Attack:** The fine-tuning literature routinely cites μ as a "finely
tuned" constant. There is no quantitative Tier-3 bound for μ in the
peer-reviewed literature. The engine correctly returns "UNBOUNDED"
but this means any claim about μ fine-tuning at the carbon-chemistry
level is unsupported.

**Severity:** HIGH — a major gap in the fine-tuning case. The engine
handles this correctly (UNBOUNDED), but the gap damages the overall
case for fine-tuning.

## Section 7 — Inter-Constant Bound Consistency

### Attack: Bounds from different sources for the same constant are inconsistent

| Constant | Tightest bound | Most permissive | Ratio | Problem? |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-------|----------|
| α | ±4% (Livio T3) | factor ~100 (Barrow T1) | ×~2 | Different tiers — defensible |
| αs | ±0.5% (Livio T3) | +50% (MacDonald T2) | ×~100 | **Different tiers — but T3 bound unreplicated** |
| αG | factor ~10 (Adams T2) | factor ~10⁹ (Carr T2) | ×~10⁸ | Real disagreement at same tier — who is right? |
| Λ | 10⁻¹²⁰ (Tegmark T2) | 10⁻¹¹⁶ (Barnes T2) | ×~10⁴ | Real disagreement at same tier |
| μ | factor ~100 (Adams T2) | factor ~10²⁰ (Stenger MC) | ×~10¹⁸ | Not a meaningful bound |
| v/M_Pl | factor ~50 (Agrawal T1→T2) | no constraint (Harnik) | ∞ | Not a bound on v alone |

**Attack:** For αG and Λ, different sources disagree by 10⁴–10⁸ at the
same tier. The engine picks the *narrowest* bound, which produces
the *strongest* fine-tuning signal. This is maximally generous to
fine-tuning claims.

**Severity:** HIGH — the engine's design choice (pick minimum probability)
systematically favors fine-tuning conclusions. An adversarial engine
would pick the *widest* bound or marginalize over the uncertainty,
which would produce weaker conclusions.
