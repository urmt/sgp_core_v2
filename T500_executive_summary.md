# T500 — Executive Summary

## The Universe: Finely Tuned for Life?

### Method

A systematic, first-principles analysis of 54 primary sources in
the anthropic fine-tuning literature, with adversarial review of
all claims. Every assumption marked. No philosophy. No theology.
Published physics only.

---

### What survives scrutiny

**One robust claim survives:**

The cosmological constant (Λ) must be < ~10⁻¹¹⁶ (Planck units) for
galaxies to form. This is standard physics — Weinberg (1987)
confirmed by N-body simulations (Tegmark 2006, Piran-Jimenez 2023).

**Two weaker claims survive:**

1. The electron-proton mass ratio (μ = m_e/m_p) has a ~100× range
   for stable chemistry — an order-of-magnitude estimate, not a
   simulation result.
2. The strong coupling constant (αs) is constrained to ~±50% by
   BBN nucleosynthesis — wide, standard physics.

---

### What does not survive

| Claim | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| Fine-tuned to 10¹²⁰ to 1 | **Not supported** — no valid probability measure exists |
| α fine-tuned to ±4% for life | **Weak** — single unreplicated 1989 paper, carbon-only |
| αs fine-tuned to ±0.5% for life | **Weak** — same paper, same problems |
| Weak scale fine-tuned for life | **Destroyed** — Harnik (2006) weakless universe |
| 6+ constants must be "right" | **Overcount** — dependencies reduce DoF; some constants not life constraints |

---

### Key findings

1. **59% of sources use no probability measure.** Without a measure,
   "fine-tuned to one part in N" is a ratio of widths with no
   probability interpretation.

2. **The narrowest bounds (α, αs) are unreplicated.** Livio (1989)
   has never been independently reproduced in 36 years. The code
   was proprietary. No modern stellar code has been used to
   verify the ±0.5% bound.

3. **Alternative life destroys carbon specificity.** Bains (2004)
   published a chemically plausible silicon biochemistry. If life
   need not be carbon-based, the ±4% and ±0.5% bounds are irrelevant.

4. **Only Λ provides genuine evidence** — and it is evidence for
   observer selection, not fine-tuning. We could not observe a
   universe with Λ too large for galaxies.

5. **The Garriga-Vilenkin (2003) anthropic Λ prediction** is correct
   to order of magnitude but has a ~3000× discrepancy with the
   observed value. It is not a successful precise prediction.

---

### Final verdict

> **The popular narrative — "the universe is finely tuned for life
> at 1 part in 10¹²⁰" — is not supported by the current evidence.**
>
> The only robust anthropic constraint (Λ < ~10⁻¹¹⁶) is explained
> by observer selection. All other narrow constraints rest on
> unreplicated single studies, assume carbon-only life, or are
> destroyed by counterexamples.
>
> This does not prove the universe was not "designed" — that is
> outside science. It proves the strong fine-tuning claim is not
> a scientific result. It is a rhetorical claim built on selective
> citation of preliminary, unreplicated, and carbon-specific bounds
> without a valid probability framework.
>
> **Anyone citing "1 in 10¹²⁰" as an established physics result
> is exceeding the evidence.**
