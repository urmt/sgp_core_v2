================================================================================
SECTION A: PERSISTENCE PROPAGATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Persistence propagation is the change in future coordinated persistence
caused by interaction with another partially organized structure.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:

Let P(S) be the persistence metric of structure S.
Let I(S_A, S_B, k) be the interaction between structures A and B at coupling k.

Propagation ΔP:
  ΔP(k) = P(I(S_A, S_B, k)) - max(P(S_A), P(S_B))

ΔP > 0 indicates positive propagation (persistence transfer).
ΔP < 0 indicates negative propagation (persistence loss).
ΔP ≈ 0 indicates neutral propagation.

OBSERVED: ΔP(k) is negative for all k in multi-node systems, but
approaches zero as coupling increases. This indicates reduced
persistence loss at higher coupling.

================================================================================
SECTION B: AMPLIFICATION REGIME DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
An amplification regime exists where d(transfer)/dk > 0 consistently
across the coupling range, meaning higher coupling increases persistence transfer.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- d(ΔP)/dk > 0 for k > threshold
- Transfer becomes less negative as coupling increases
- Amplification regime begins around k = 0.4-0.6

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:
  Amplification regime: ∀ k ∈ [k_min, k_max]: d(ΔP)/dk > 0

================================================================================
SECTION C: SATURATION REGIME DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A saturation regime exists where transfer plateaus despite increasing
coupling, indicating no further change in propagation geometry.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Variance of ΔP in high-k region < threshold
- Transfer values cluster near a constant value
- Saturation confirmed at both topologies tested

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:
  Saturation regime: Var(ΔP[k_high]) < ε for ε = 0.0005

================================================================================
SECTION D: COLLAPSE REGIME DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A collapse regime exists where increasing coupling reduces persistence
organization, causing ΔP to become more negative.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- No collapse detected in current experiment
- ΔP at k=1.0 > ΔP at k=0.0 (less negative)
- Collapse threshold not reached

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:
  Collapse regime: ΔP(k=1.0) < ΔP(k=0.0) AND ΔP(k) < 0

STATUS: No collapse regime observed.

================================================================================
SECTION E: RECURSIVE AMPLIFICATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
Recursive amplification exists when transfer-enhanced organization
increases future transfer capacity, meaning transfer at stage n+1
exceeds transfer at stage n.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR:
- Recursive amplification NOT confirmed
- Transfer values remain constant across stages
- No evidence of self-reinforcing propagation

MATHEMATICAL CHARACTERIZATION:
  Recursive amplification: ∃ n: ΔP[n+1] > ΔP[n]

STATUS: No recursive amplification observed.
