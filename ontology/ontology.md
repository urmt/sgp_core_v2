# Ontology

## Primitive Entities

### Interaction

The fundamental unit. An interaction is a mutual modification between two or more entities. Interactions are not properties of pre-existing entities — they are the ground from which entities emerge.

Properties:
- Directed or undirected
- Time-localized or persistent
- Varying in strength and structure

---

## Derived Entities

### Interaction Pattern

A recurring configuration of interactions. When interactions stabilize into a reproducible configuration, that configuration has derivative existence as a pattern.

### System

A set of components whose mutual interactions form a closed (or approximately closed) dynamical network. Systems are defined by interaction boundaries, not spatial boundaries.

### Component

A node in the interaction network. Components are themselves interaction patterns at a lower scale (recursive structure). A component is distinguished not by intrinsic properties but by its pattern of interaction with other components.

### Observer

A special class of system that forms meta-interactions with other systems. Observers are not external to the observed — the observation is itself an interaction.

---

## Derived Properties

### Identity

The persistence of an interaction pattern across time. Identity is never absolute — it is always relative to a time scale. A pattern that persists for τ time units has identity-on-scale-τ.

### Coherence

A quantitative measure of the degree to which a system's dynamics are dominated by joint interaction structure rather than independent component variation. See `/metrics/coherence_metric.md`.

### Resilience

The capacity of an interaction pattern to maintain its structure under perturbation. Resilience is not a property of individual components but of the interaction network as a whole.

### Mobility

The capacity of a system to reconfigure its interaction topology. Mobility is the dual of coherence: a system can be highly coherent (stable pattern) or highly mobile (fluid reconfiguration), or balance both.

---

## Boundaries

### System boundary

Distinguishes internal interactions (within the system) from external interactions (with environment). In practice, this boundary is chosen by the observer and is provisional.

### Scale boundary

Interaction patterns exist at multiple scales. What is a "system" at one scale is a "component" at the next scale up, and a "population of interacting components" at the next scale down.

---

## Non-Entities

The following are NOT primitive in SFI ontology:
- **Substance** — There is no substrate-independent "matter." What appears as substance is stable interaction at a very short time scale.
- **Vacuum** — There is no empty space. Interaction fills the ontology; absence of interaction is absence of existence.
- **Eternal object** — Nothing exists outside of time. All entities are temporal.
- **Intrinsic property** — All properties are relational. A property is a pattern of interaction with an observer.
