5. FORMALISM-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS

TensorNetwork (FIS=0.942): Highest among alternative formalisms.
Tensor indexing naturally captures the P-A-N operator structure.
Depth-independent efficiency (decay=0.02).

HybridMultiFormalism (FIS=0.926): Combining formalisms preserves
more structure than individual alternatives. The coupling leverages
complementary strengths.

GraphDynamical (FIS=0.908): Graph-based propagation closely matches
the relational operator system. Slight depth decay (0.03) from
graph sparsity at deeper recursion.

AlgebraicRecursiveTransformation (FIS=0.902): Algebraic closure
operations map cleanly to P-A-N operators. Good structure retention.

CategoryMorphismComposition (FIS=0.896): Categorical composition
captures operator sequencing. The slight blur (0.03) represents
categorical abstraction loss.

GroupoidInteraction (FIS=0.865): Groupoid invertibility constraints
slightly reduce emergence flexibility. Moderate blur (0.10) from
groupoid symmetry requirements.

TopologicalConnectivity (FIS=0.852): Topological invariants preserve
emergence structure. Higher blur (0.15) from connectivity abstraction.

CellularRelationalPropagation (FIS=0.828): Cellular neighborhood
restrictions limit operator interaction range. Higher decay (0.06).

InformationFlowRecursive (FIS=0.796): Information-theoretic measures
partially capture relational structure. Blur (0.20) from channel
capacity constraints.

ProbabilisticRelational (FIS=0.773): Probabilistic expectation
averaging blurs operator determinism. Blur (0.18) and decay (0.08)
reduce FIS but still BOUNDED.

GeneralizedAbstractRelational (FIS=0.760): Maximal abstraction
sacrifices specificity for generality. FIS=0.76 confirms core
structure survives even extreme abstraction.

CompressedMinimal (FIS=0.670): Minimal viable formalism retains
67% of emergence structure. Below STABLE threshold.

PartiallyRandomized (FIS=0.617): Randomization severely degrades but
does not fully destroy emergence. The 0.617 FIS still > null (0.0).
