1. TRANSFER RETENTION IN PERSISTENCE SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Phase 352 tests whether recursive persistence can be coherently transferred
between stabilized domains. This extends Phase 351's persistence domains by
adding organizational transfer dynamics.

Key finding: STABLE TRANSFER EXISTS for strong networks.

- P-A-N composite achieves TRANSFER-STABLE at depths 1-8
- P-A pair achieves TRANSFER-STABLE at depths 1-6
- Transfer retention for P-A-N: 0.9578 at depth 1, declining to 0.6412 at depth 20
- This represents bounded degradation during transfer

Transfer retention > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (depths 1-12)
- P-A (depths 1-10)
- Projection (depths 1-8)
- Antisymmetry (depths 1-4)

10. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (TRANSFER RETENTION > 0.75): PASS
- P-A-N, P-A, Projection achieve stable transfer

H2 (PROPAGATION STABILITY > 0.70): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 16
- A-N collapses before depth 8

H3 (DEGRADATION LOSS < 0.30): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains < 0.30 through depth 16
- Neutral already exceeds at depth 1

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- Only P-A-N at depth 1 achieves > 0.90
- Scale invariance limited to strong networks at early depths

H5 (TRANSFER HIERARCHY): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across depths

OVERALL VERDICT: WEAKLY_TRANSFERRED
- Stable transfer exists in strong networks (P-A-N, P-A)
- Propagation remains stable for P-A-N through depth 16
- Degradation loss bounded for P-A-N
- Weak networks collapse during transfer
- Cooperative transfer gains present
- Scale invariance limited
