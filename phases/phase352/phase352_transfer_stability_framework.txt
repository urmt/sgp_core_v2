THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK: TRANSFER STABILITY
================================================================================

Recursive transfer stability:
- Organizational persistence transmitted between domains
- Coherent structure retained during transfer
- Bounded degradation during propagation
- Recursive persistence preserved across transfer events

Key distinction from prior phases:
- PERSISTENCE DOMAINS (Phase 351): Bounded coherent regions
- TRANSFER STABILITY (Phase 352): Propagation between domains

Transfer measures organizational propagation.
Domains measure spatial localization of persistence.
Selection measures pathway discrimination.

================================================================================
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
================================================================================

1. Initialize stabilized persistence domains
   - From prior phase domains
   - Bounded stabilization regions
   - Internal coherence parameters

2. Allow transfer processes
   - Bounded organizational exchange
   - Cooperative reinforcement
   - Recursive transfer attempts
   - Selective stabilization filtering

3. Introduce perturbations
   - Transfer perturbations
   - Recursive degradation events
   - Partial domain disruption

4. Measurement framework
   - Transfer retention: retained organization after transfer
   - Propagation stability: persistence during transfer
   - Degradation loss: organization lost during transfer
   - Cooperative gain: amplification from coordinated transfer

================================================================================
METRIC DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

TRANSFER_RETENTION:
- Retained organization after transfer process
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate stable transfer
- Values > 0.75 indicate transfer-stable domains

PROPAGATION_STABILITY:
- Persistence during recursive transfer
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate stable propagation
- Values > 0.70 indicate stable propagation

DEGRADATION_LOSS:
- Organization lost during transfer
- Range: [0, 1]
- Low values indicate bounded loss
- Values < 0.30 indicate bounded degradation

COOPERATIVE_TRANSFER_GAIN:
- Amplification from coordinated transfer
- Range: [0, 1]
- Positive values indicate cooperative benefit
- Values > 0.10 indicate cooperative amplification

RECURSIVE_TRANSFER_PERSISTENCE:
- Cross-depth transfer structure retention
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate recursive persistence
- Values > 0.70 indicate stable recursive transfer

RG_SIMILARITY:
- Fine-grained vs coarse-grained transfer preservation
- > 0.90 indicates scale-invariant transfer

================================================================================
NETWORK-SPECIFIC PREDICTIONS
================================================================================

P-A-N COMPOSITE:
- Strongest transfer retention
- Best propagation stability
- Bounded degradation loss
- Cooperative amplification present
- Forms strongest transfer-stable regime

P-A PAIR:
- Strong transfer retention
- Good propagation stability
- Moderate degradation loss
- Some cooperative gain
- Stable pair transfer

P-N PAIR:
- Moderate transfer retention
- Some propagation degradation
- Partial degradation loss
- Weakening transfer
- Weaker pair transfer

A-N PAIR:
- Weak transfer retention
- Low propagation stability
- High degradation loss
- Minimal cooperative gain
- Collapsing transfer

CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA

- transfer_retention > 0.65

COOPERATIVELY_PROPAGATING:
- cooperative_transfer_gain > 0.15
- transfer_retention > 0.70

COLLAPSING:
- transfer_retention < 0.65
- OR degradation_loss > 0.45
- OR propagation_stability < 0.50
