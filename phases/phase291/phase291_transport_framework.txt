SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. RELATIONAL TRANSPORT
-----------------------
Definition: Directional propagation of recursive relational
organization across depth.

Operationalization:
- Compute net displacement in configuration space per depth
- Track directional consistency across trajectories
- transport_strength = mean_net_displacement / total_path_length

B. TRANSPORT CHANNEL
--------------------
Definition: A statistically preferred recursive pathway through
configuration geometry.

Operationalization:
- Identify trajectory clustering in embedding space
- Compute path preference probability
- Channel present if clustering > random baseline

C. TRANSPORT STABILITY
----------------------
Definition: Persistence of directional propagation structure under
recursive evolution.

Operationalization:
- Track transport_strength across depths
- Stable if variance < threshold
- Unstable if variance increases with depth

D. DIFFUSE TRANSPORT
--------------------
Definition: Propagation without localized fixed-region retention.

Operationalization:
- High dispersion with nonzero transport
- Diffuse if dispersion > threshold AND transport > 0
- Channeled if dispersion < threshold AND transport > 0

E. RG-STABLE TRANSPORT
----------------------
Definition: Transport organization preserved under coarse-graining.

Operationalization:
- Compute fine-scale transport at multiple depths
- Compare coarse-scale (RG block) transport
- Correlation > 0.90 = RG_STABLE
