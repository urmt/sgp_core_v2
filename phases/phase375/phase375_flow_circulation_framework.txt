# Phase 375 Framework: Topological Flow and Attractor Circulation

## Purpose
Determine whether connected recursive attractor topologies support stable large-scale organizational flow dynamics.

## Structure

### 1. Topological Flow Mapping
- Map flow pathways across attractor topology
- Identify flow directionality and magnitude
- Measure circulation stability (CS)

### 2. Circulation Loop Detection
- Identify closed circulation loops in topology
- Measure loop frequency and stability
- Map loop hierarchy

### 3. Flow Bottleneck Analysis
- Identify flow bottlenecks in topology
- Measure bottleneck resilience (BR)
- Map bottleneck impact on stability

### 4. Attractor Source/Sink Characterization
- Classify attractors as sources or sinks
- Measure source/sink balance (SSB)
- Identify flow-generating vs. flow-absorbing basins

### 5. Recursive Transport Stability
- Track transport trajectories across topology
- Measure recursive transport coherence (RTC)
- Identify transport degradation patterns

### 6. Perturbation Flow Recovery
- Apply perturbations to disrupt flow
- Measure flow recovery trajectories
- Identify flow resilience

### 7. Null Circulation Controls
- Randomized circulation baselines
- Confirm signal is not artifact
- Establish significance thresholds

## Metrics
- circulation_stability (CS)
- flow_persistence (FP)
- bottleneck_resilience (BR)
- source_sink_balance (SSB)
- recursive_transport_coherence (RTC)
- rg_similarity (RGS)
- circulation_hierarchy_index (CHI)

## Classification Thresholds
- FLOW-STABLE: CS > 0.75, FP > 0.75, RTC > 0.70
- FLOW-BOUNDED: CS > 0.65, FP > 0.65, RTC > 0.60
- FLOW-PARTIAL: CS > 0.55, FP > 0.55, RTC > 0.50
- FLOW-DEGRADING: CS > 0.40
- FLOW-FAILED: CS <= 0.40
