================================================================================
A. INVARIANT REGIME
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
A recursively persistent organizational structure remaining stable across
recursive depth and scale variation.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which organizational patterns maintain consistent regime
identity regardless of recursive depth, sector composition, or phase focus.

QUANTIFICATION:
Regime strength measures the coherence and persistence of invariant
organizational structure.

================================================================================
B. REGIME STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Retention of organizational identity under recursive propagation.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which regime structure remains identifiable and consistent
through recursive transformation.

QUANTIFICATION:
Regime stability measures the preservation of regime identity.

================================================================================
C. REGIME RETENTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistence of identifiable regime structure across recursive evolution.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which invariant regimes maintain their defining properties
as recursion increases.

QUANTIFICATION:
Recursive retention measures the persistence of regime structure.

================================================================================
D. REGIME TRANSITION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Bounded organizational transition between persistent regimes.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which transitions between organizational regimes remain
bounded and controlled rather than abrupt or catastrophic.

QUANTIFICATION:
Transition control measures the boundedness of regime changes.

================================================================================
E. REGIME BOUNDEDNESS
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Constraint on unbounded organizational divergence across recursive evolution.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which invariant regimes maintain bounded structure without
uncontrolled expansion or collapse.

QUANTIFICATION:
Boundedness measures the containment of regime organization.
Dispersion measures the degree of unbounded spread.

================================================================================
F. REGIME RG STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Scale-stable preservation of regime structure.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which invariant regimes survive coarse-graining operations
at different scales.

QUANTIFICATION:
RG stability measures the correlation between fine and coarse regime metrics.

================================================================================
REGIME STRUCTURE FORMALIZATION
================================================================================

REGIME = (R_str, R_stab, R_ret, Bound, T_ctrl, Disp, RG_stab)
