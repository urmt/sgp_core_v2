================================================================================
SYMMETRY BREAKING FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

This framework establishes operational definitions for analyzing whether
recursive organizational structure can spontaneously differentiate, amplify
initially small asymmetries, stabilize divergent organizational regimes,
or develop irreversible sector specialization.

================================================================================
CORE CONCEPT
================================================================================

Symmetry breaking exists if:

initially similar recursive structures evolve into persistently
differentiated organizational states.

Key phenomena tested:
- TINY INITIAL ASYMMETRIES AMPLIFY
  Small differences grow into large divergences
- SECTORS DIVERGE OVER DEPTH
  Initially equivalent configurations become distinct
- DIFFERENTIATION STABILIZES
  Divergent states persist rather than reverting
- ORGANIZATIONAL ROLES BECOME PERSISTENT
  Sectors acquire stable functional identities
- RECURSIVE TRAJECTORIES CEASE REMAINING EQUIVALENT
  Future paths diverge and remain separate

================================================================================
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
================================================================================

For each sector, symmetry evolution is tracked:

1. INITIALIZE NEAR-SYMMETRIC CONDITIONS
   - Start with only tiny perturbations from symmetry
   - Measure initial asymmetry amplitude

2. EVOLVE RECURSIVELY ACROSS DEPTH
   - Progress through depths: 1→2→4→6→8→12→16→20
   - Track asymmetry amplification at each depth

3. TRACK DIVERGENCE GROWTH
   - Measure asymmetry amplitude increase
   - Monitor trajectory divergence
   - Track role specialization emergence

4. COMPARE EARLY vs LATE DEPTH
   - Early depth (1-4): Near-symmetric behavior
   - Late depth (16-20): Fully differentiated behavior

================================================================================
KEY HYPOTHESES
================================================================================

H1: SYMMETRY AMPLIFICATION EXISTS
   Asymmetries grow with depth.
   PASS if asymmetry_amplitude increases with depth for at least one sector.

H2: PERSISTENT DIFFERENTIATION EXISTS
   Trajectories remain divergent through late recursion.
   PASS if trajectory_divergence remains nonzero through depth 20.

H3: STABLE ROLE FORMATION EXISTS
   Specialization stabilizes after depth 8.
   PASS if recursive_specialization stabilizes.

H4: RG STABILITY
   Symmetry-breaking structure preserved under coarse-graining.
   PASS if rg_similarity > 0.90.

H5: HIERARCHICAL DIFFERENTIATION PERSISTS
   Sector ordering Projection > Antisymmetry > Neutral maintained.
   PASS if hierarchy preserved across depths.

================================================================================
OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1. ASYMMETRY AMPLITUDE (a)
   Divergence from symmetric baseline.
   a = deviation_from_symmetry
   Range: [0, 1]
   Increases indicate symmetry breaking.

2. DIFFERENTIATION GROWTH (d)
   Amplification across depth.
   d = da/d(depth)
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate faster differentiation.

3. ROLE PERSISTENCE (p)
   Stability of differentiated role.
   p = persistence_probability
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate stable roles.

4. TRAJECTORY DIVERGENCE (t)
   Separation between sector paths.
   t = path_separation
   Range: [0, 1]
   t > 0 indicates differentiation.

5. RECURSIVE SPECIALIZATION (s)
   Depth-dependent role fixation.
   s = specialization_depth
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate stronger specialization.

6. RG SIMILARITY (r)
   Fine/coarse preservation of asymmetric structure.
   Range: [0, 1]
   r > 0.90 indicates stable asymmetry.

7. CLASSIFICATION (cls)
   SYMMETRY-BREAKING: Strong amplification, stable roles
   WEAKLY_DIFFERENTIATED: Moderate amplification
   ROLE-STABLE: Stable roles, bounded growth
   ASYMMETRY-DEGRADING: Decreasing asymmetry
   DIFFUSIVE: No stable differentiation

================================================================================
SECTOR DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

PROJECTION:
- Role: Typically strongest/stable sector
- Expected: ROLE-STABLE or WEAKLY_DIFFERENTIATED
- Specialization should stabilize

ANTISYMMETRY:
- Role: Intermediate sector
- Expected: WEAKLY_DIFFERENTIATED or ASYMMETRY-DEGRADING
- Differentiation moderate

NEUTRAL:
- Role: Typically weakest sector
- Expected: ASYMMETRY-DEGRADING or DIFFUSIVE
- Specialization may fail

================================================================================
DEPTH ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Analyzed depths: [1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20]

Expected pattern:
- Shallow depths (1-4): Near-symmetric, minimal divergence
- Middle depths (6-12): Symmetry breaking onset
- Deep recursion (16-20): Fully differentiated, stable roles
