1. MODULAR PARTITIONING IN RECURSIVE SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Phase 354 tests whether recursively interlocked persistence can partition
into semi-independent modules. This extends Phase 353's interlocking stability
by adding modular partitioning dynamics.

Key finding: MODULAR STABILITY EXISTS for strong networks.

- P-A-N composite achieves MODULAR-STABLE at depths 1-6
- P-A pair achieves MODULAR-STABLE at depths 1-4
- Modular coherence for P-A-N: 0.9389 at depth 1, declining to 0.5978 at depth 20

Modular coherence > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (depths 1-12)
- P-A (depths 1-10)
- Projection (depths 1-8)
- Antisymmetry (depths 1-4)

8. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (MODULAR COHERENCE > 0.75): PASS
- P-A-N, P-A, Projection achieve modular-stable structure

H2 (MODULE INDEPENDENCE > 0.70): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 20
- A-N collapses before depth 8

H3 (CROSS-MODULE DEGRADATION < 0.30): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains < 0.30 through depth 20
- Neutral exceeds from depth 8

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- Only P-A-N at depth 1 achieves > 0.90
- Scale invariance limited to strong networks at early depths

H5 (MODULAR HIERARCHY): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across depths

OVERALL VERDICT: WEAKLY_MODULAR
- Modular stability exists in strong networks (P-A-N, P-A)
- Module independence maintained for P-A-N through depth 20
- Cross-module degradation bounded for P-A-N
- Weak modules collapse
- Scale invariance limited
