1. ADAPTIVE RECONFIGURATION IN RECURSIVE SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Phase 355 tests whether recursively modular persistence can dynamically
reconfigure under perturbation. This extends Phase 354's modular stability
by adding adaptive dynamics.

Key finding: ADAPTIVE STABILITY EXISTS for strong networks.

- P-A-N composite achieves ADAPTIVELY_STABLE at depths 1-6
- P-A pair achieves ADAPTIVELY_STABLE at depths 1-6
- Adaptive retention for P-A-N: 0.9323 at depth 1, declining to 0.6156 at depth 20

Adaptive retention > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (depths 1-12)
- P-A (depths 1-12)
- Projection (depths 1-8)
- Antisymmetry (depths 1-4)

8. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (ADAPTIVE RETENTION > 0.75): PASS
- P-A-N, P-A, Projection achieve adaptive stability

H2 (RECOVERY EFFICIENCY > 0.70): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 20
- A-N collapses before depth 8

H3 (ADAPTIVE DRIFT < 0.30): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains < 0.30 through depth 20
- Neutral exceeds from depth 12

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- Only P-A-N at depth 1 achieves > 0.90
- Scale invariance limited to strong networks at early depths

H5 (ADAPTIVE HIERARCHY): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across depths

OVERALL VERDICT: WEAKLY_ADAPTIVE
- Adaptive stability exists in strong networks (P-A-N, P-A)
- Recovery efficiency maintained for P-A-N through depth 20
- Adaptive drift bounded for P-A-N
- Weak systems collapse
- Scale invariance limited
