# Phase 503: Physical Organizational Correspondence Framework

## Objective
Test whether known physical organizational structures share statistically measurable bounded correspondence with SFH-SGP invariants WITHOUT claiming derivation, causation, or ontology.

## Core Question
Do independently known physical organizational systems exhibit constrained structural correspondence to SFH-SGP universality structures?

NOT: "Does SFH-SGP explain physics?"

## Allowed Target Domains
- renormalization scaling,
- conservation structures,
- wave/interference organization,
- attractor persistence,
- transport dynamics,
- localization behavior,
- equilibrium systems,
- symmetry breaking,
- field coherence structures.

## Forbidden Claims
NO:
- quantum derivation claims,
- spacetime derivation claims,
- consciousness claims,
- unified physics claims,
- ontology claims,
- "theory of everything" language.

## Primary Hypotheses
H1: Some physical organizational structures show bounded correspondence to SFH-SGP invariants.
H2: Correspondence localizes to recursive stability structures, NOT arbitrary physical variables.
H3: Some physical systems show no meaningful correspondence.
H4: Physical correspondence strength depends on recursive compositional structure.
H5: Null-controlled physical randomizations fail correspondence significantly.

## Required Controls
- blind metric locking,
- predefined scoring,
- null physical ensembles,
- randomized baselines,
- exclusion criteria,
- anti-retrofitting protocols.

## Primary Metrics
1. physical_correspondence_score: Overall correspondence strength with SFH-SGP
2. recursive_stability_alignment: Alignment with SFH-SGP recursive stability patterns
3. scale_invariance_overlap: Overlap with SFH-SGP scale invariance properties
4. attractor_correspondence_density: Density of attractor-like correspondence
5. transport_similarity_index: Similarity in transport dynamics organization
6. null_separation_ratio: Separation from null physical ensembles
7. localization_match_score: Match with SFH-SGP localization patterns

## Success Condition
Nontrivial bounded correspondence appears reproducibly in specific physical organizational domains.

## Failure Condition
Correspondence collapses under controls, randomization, or blind evaluation.

## Interpretation Discipline
Even successful correspondence implies ONLY:
shared organizational structure.

NOT:
physical derivation,
fundamental ontology,
or explanatory supremacy.

4. Apply null hypothesis testing with physical system randomization
5. Apply the same blind evaluation protocols used in Phase 501-502
