================================================================================
A. CONSTRAINT PROPAGATION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Recursive transmission of bounded organizational constraints across
recursive depth while preserving universality structure.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which constraint structures maintain bounded organization
as they propagate across recursive depths.

QUANTIFICATION:
Propagation strength measures the coherence of constraint propagation.

================================================================================
B. PROPAGATION CONSISTENCY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Retention of organizational propagation behavior under recursive
evolution.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which propagation structure remains consistent when
constraints are transmitted across recursive depths.

QUANTIFICATION:
Propagation consistency measures the stability of propagation behavior.

================================================================================
C. PROPAGATION RETENTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistence of identifiable propagation structure across recursive
depth.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which propagation organization maintains its defining
properties as recursive depth increases.

QUANTIFICATION:
Recursive retention measures the persistence of propagation structure.

================================================================================
D. PROPAGATION TRANSITION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Bounded transition between propagation-preserving regimes.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which transitions between propagation states remain
bounded and controlled rather than abrupt or catastrophic.

QUANTIFICATION:
Transition control measures the boundedness of propagation changes.

================================================================================
E. PROPAGATION BOUNDEDNESS
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Constraint on unbounded divergence during recursive propagation.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which recursive propagation maintains bounded structure
without uncontrolled expansion or collapse.

QUANTIFICATION:
Boundedness measures the containment of propagation organization.
Dispersion measures the degree of unbounded spread.

================================================================================
F. PROPAGATION RG STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Scale-stable preservation of propagation organization.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which propagation structure survives coarse-graining
operations at different scales.

QUANTIFICATION:
RG stability measures the correlation between fine and coarse
propagation metrics.
