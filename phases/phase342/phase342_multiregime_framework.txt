================================================================================
MULTI-REGIME COEXISTENCE FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

This framework establishes operational definitions for analyzing whether
differentiated recursive organizational regimes can coexist stably, preserve
distinct identities, interact without collapse, maintain bounded interference,
and sustain persistent multi-regime organization.

================================================================================
CORE CONCEPT
================================================================================

A multi-regime system exists if:

distinct recursive organizational regimes can interact while preserving
differentiated identity.

Key phenomena tested:
- COEXISTENCE STABILITY
  Multiple regimes persist simultaneously
- IDENTITY PRESERVATION
  Distinct structures remain identifiable under interaction
- INTERFERENCE BOUNDEDNESS
  Interaction effects remain limited
- REGIME RETENTION
  Cross-regime coupling maintains differentiated structure
- RECURSIVE PERSISTENCE
  Coexistence survives deep recursion

================================================================================
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
================================================================================

For sector pairs, multi-regime behavior is analyzed:

1. SECTOR PAIRS ANALYZED
   - Projection-Antisymmetry (strongest pair)
   - Projection-Neutral (mixed pair)
   - Antisymmetry-Neutral (weakest pair)

2. ISOLATED vs INTERACTING EVOLUTION
   - Isolated: Each sector evolves independently
   - Interacting: Sectors influence each other

3. TRACKED INTERACTIONS
   - Coexistence persistence through coupling
   - Identity preservation under interaction
   - Interference growth from mutual influence
   - Compatibility retention over depth

4. PAIRWISE COMPARISONS
   - Stability of each pair combination
   - Hierarchy of pair compatibility
   - Degradation patterns under interaction

================================================================================
OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1. COEXISTENCE STRENGTH (c)
   Persistence of simultaneous regimes.
   c = joint_persistence_probability
   Range: [0, 1]
   c > 0.70 indicates stable coexistence.

2. IDENTITY RETENTION (i)
   Preservation of differentiated structure.
   i = identity_preservation_rate
   Range: [0, 1]
   i > 0.70 indicates preserved identity.

3. INTERFERENCE DRIFT (d)
   Degradation due to interaction.
   d = interaction_degradation
   Range: [0, 1]
   d < 0.30 indicates bounded interference.

4. COMPATIBILITY STABILITY (s)
   Bounded coexistence quality.
   s = compatibility_measure
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate better compatibility.

5. RECURSIVE PERSISTENCE (p)
   Depth survival of coexistence.
   p = depth_survival_rate
   Range: [0, 1]
   p > 0.70 indicates persistent coexistence.

6. RG SIMILARITY (r)
   Fine/coarse preservation of multi-regime structure.
   Range: [0, 1]
   r > 0.90 indicates stable preservation.

7. CLASSIFICATION (cls)
   COEXISTING: Strong persistence, preserved identity
   WEAKLY_COMPATIBLE: Moderate persistence, some degradation
   INTERFERENCE-DEGRADING: Significant interference
   IDENTITY-PRESERVING: Identity preserved but weak coexistence
   COLLAPSING: Identity loss, regime collapse
