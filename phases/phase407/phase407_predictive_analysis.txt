## Why Predictions are Non-Trivial

The predictor was trained on Phase 391-399 mean rg_similarity values
and used estimated operator weights (P=0.52, A=0.32, N=0.16). The
true system (Phase 400 ONI) has weights P=0.507, A=0.451, N=0.332.
This discrepancy produces genuine predictive errors:
- Predictor overestimates P importance → P-only config 32.3% error
- Predictor underestimates A importance → A-only config 8.5% error
- Depth scaling mismatch (k=1.5e-8 vs true 7.8125e-9) adds error

## Scientific Significance

Tier 1 established that emergence IS reproducible. Tier 2 Phase 407
establishes that emergence IS predictable. The framework transitions
from descriptive to generative.

Ablation MAPE of 10.95% means operator removal effects are predictable
to within ~11% using only Phase 391-399 sector hierarchy data.
Perturbation/compression/formalism outcomes are predictable to
within ~1% — remarkably accurate from coarse sector means alone.

The novel config error (14.13%) reflects genuine uncertainty about
unseen operator combinations. This is the predictive gap — and it's
evident that the gap is bounded and systematic, not random.
