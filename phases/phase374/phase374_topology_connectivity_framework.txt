# Phase 374 Framework: Attractor-Topology and Basin Connectivity

## Purpose
Determine whether recursive relational attractors form a connected higher-order topology with stable basin geometry and transition connectivity.

## Structure

### 1. Basin Connectivity Mapping
- Map connections between attractor basins
- Identify connected components in topology
- Measure basin connectivity (BC)

### 2. Transition Corridor Analysis
- Identify stable transition corridors between basins
- Measure corridor stability (CS)
- Map corridor geometry and capacity

### 3. Topological Hierarchy Extraction
- Extract hierarchical structure from topology
- Measure topological hierarchy index (THI)
- Identify dominant vs. subordinate basins

### 4. Attractor Navigation Stability
- Track navigation trajectories across topology
- Measure navigation survival rate (NSR)
- Identify navigation bottlenecks

### 5. Basin Merger/Splitting Dynamics
- Track basin merger and splitting events
- Measure merger coherence
- Identify topology restructuring patterns

### 6. Perturbation Corridor Recovery
- Apply perturbations to disrupt corridors
- Measure corridor recovery trajectories
- Identify corridor resilience

### 7. Null Topology Controls
- Randomized topology baselines
- Confirm signal is not artifact
- Establish significance thresholds

## Metrics
- basin_connectivity (BC)
- corridor_stability (CS)
- topological_hierarchy_index (THI)
- navigation_survival_rate (NSR)
- attractor_dominance (AD)
- rg_similarity (RGS)
- topology_persistence (TP)

## Classification Thresholds
- TOPOLOGY-CONNECTED: BC > 0.75, CS > 0.75, NSR > 0.70
- TOPOLOGY-BOUNDED: BC > 0.65, CS > 0.65, NSR > 0.60
- TOPOLOGY-PARTIAL: BC > 0.55, CS > 0.55, NSR > 0.50
- TOPOLOGY-DEGRADING: BC > 0.40
- TOPOLOGY-FAILED: BC <= 0.40
