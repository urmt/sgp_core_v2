ATTRACTOR DYNAMICS (T077): IS THE FERTILE CORRIDOR AN ATTRACTOR?
================================================================================

T077 tested whether the fertile corridor acts as an attractor (systems converge
toward it), saddle (easy to leave), transit (no attraction), or external
(constructed) across all 4 domains.

KEY RESULTS:
- 3/4 domains classified as Type II — Saddle (mathematics, recursive, dynamics)
- 1/4 classified as Type III — Transit (physics — no fortress systems exist)
- No Type I (Attractor) or Type IV (External) found
- The fertile corridor is NOT a universal attractor

DRIFT DIRECTION:
- 3 domains collapse-ward (physics, recursive, dynamics) — failure is from
  insufficient stability
- 1 domain fortress-ward (mathematics) — failure is from excessive stability
- Asymmetry is a fundamental property of the corridor

RECURSIVE SUBSTRATE SHOWS STRONGEST ATTRACTOR PROPERTIES:
- Fortress systems closest to fertile centroid (0.650 vs 1.102–1.751 elsewhere)
- Attraction ratio 0.359 (fortress 2.8× closer to fertile than collapse)
- Recovery potential 0.238 (only domain with non-zero recovery potential)
- This is expected — the substrate is DESIGNED for generative recovery

CROSS-DOMAIN PATTERN:
  Physics:        Transit (no fortress possible — all viable systems fertile)
  Mathematics:    Saddle (fortress-ward drift, large S gap blocks recovery)
  Recursive:      Saddle but NEAR-ATTRACTOR (strongest attraction/recovery)
  Dynamics:       Saddle (collapse-ward drift, low recovery potential)

CONCLUSION: The fertile corridor is a saddle-like region, not a basin of
attraction. Systems must be maintained in the corridor — they do not naturally
converge there. The recursive substrate is the exception, showing near-attractor
behavior consistent with its design purpose. This suggests the fertile corridor
is maintained by specific dynamical mechanisms, not by generic attraction.
