PHASE 402 ANALYSIS: COMPRESSION-GENERALIZATION VALIDATION

1. OVERVIEW

Phase 402 tests whether the recursive emergence hierarchy survives
substantial compression, simplification, parameter reduction, and
generalized representation constraints. The core question is whether
the emergence architecture is deeply compressible or depends on
hidden overparameterized complexity.

Parameters: SEED=390, k=0.00000000390625, 46 depths to 67108864,
7 sectors, 15 conditions (14 compression + 1 null), 4620 points.

3. INFORMATION EFFICIENCY (RIE)

Recursive Information Efficiency = CSF / compression_ratio:
  11 of 14 signal conditions have RIE > 1.0, meaning emergence
  survives at a higher rate than the parameter budget retained.

  Best: LowPrecisionRecursion RIE=1.840
  Worst: RecursiveDimensionalReduction RIE=0.889

  RIE > 1.0 for all conditions where compression_ratio >= 0.55.
  Below 0.55 (Minimal Arch=0.5, Dim Reduce=0.55), RIE drops below 1.0.

  This demonstrates superlinear information efficiency — emergence
  retains proportionally more structure than the parameter reduction
  would suggest, confirming the architecture is NOT overparameterized.

4. COMPRESSION TYPE ANALYSIS

Sector differentiation (ReducedSectorCount): CSF=0.997
  Blurring sector distinctions to 60% differentiation has virtually
  no effect. The sectors share most structural information.

Memory compression (CompressedMemoryRepresentation): CSF=0.995
  Reducing recursive memory depth by 35% has minimal impact.
  Emergence is not dependent on full recursive memory.

Operator precision (CompressedOperatorBasis): CSF=0.987
  8-level quantization with 70% precision barely affects emergence.
  Operator values do not need high precision.

Depth coarsening (CoarseGrainedRecursiveDepth): CSF=1.000
  Reducing effective depth resolution to 60% preserves full emergence.
  Fine depth structure is redundant.

Precision reduction (LowPrecisionRecursion): CSF=0.920
  4-level quantization at 50% precision still retains 92% of emergence.
  The architecture is remarkably low-precision tolerant.

Transport/Gradient simplification (SimplifiedTransportDynamics): CSF=0.716
  70% transport fidelity retains 72% emergence. Moderate degradation.

Operator abstraction (GeneralizedOperatorAbstraction): CSF=0.716
  70% operator fidelity retains 72% emergence. Same as transport.

Channel reduction (ReducedInteractionChannels): CSF=0.676
  55% channel retention gives 68% emergence. Below STABLE threshold.

Sparsity (SparseRecursiveConnectivity): CSF=0.622
  60% connectivity retains 62% emergence. Notable that connectivity
  fraction and survival fraction are nearly equal — linear mapping.

Randomized compression (RandomizedParameterCompression): CSF=0.619
  Adding randomization to compression slightly worsens it (vs 0.622
  for pure sparsity). HRR=0 due to random ordering shifts.

Bandwidth constraint (ConstrainedPropagationBandwidth): CSF=0.597
  50% bandwidth retains ~60% emergence. Below STABLE threshold.

Dimensional reduction (RecursiveDimensionalReduction): CSF=0.489
  55% dimension retention gives only 49% emergence. Dimensions are
  more critical than other parameters for emergence.

Minimal architecture (MinimalSufficientArchitecture): CSF=0.466
  50% minimal architecture retains 47% emergence. The minimal viable
  architecture is about half the full structure.

5. HIERARCHY PRESERVATION

HRR = 1.0 for 13/14 signal conditions. Only RandomizedParameterCompression
(HRR=0) scrambles ordering due to random parameter dropout creating
uneven sector effects.

This shows that compression preserves structural hierarchy — ordering
is robust under simplification, unlike additive noise (Phase 401).

6. MINIMAL SUFFICIENT ARCHITECTURE

The MinimalSufficientArchitecture condition (50% of full:
compression_ratio=0.5, factor=0.5^1.2=0.435) gives CSF=0.466, ERF=0.435.
At this compression level, emergence enters COMPRESSION-DEGRADING.

The minimal viable architecture occurs around compression_ratio=0.55-0.60
where CSF crosses above 0.60 (COMPRESSION-BOUNDED boundary).

This quantifies the irreducible complexity: approximately 55-60% of the
full architecture is needed to maintain bounded emergence.

7. NULL CONTROL CONTRAST

NullCompressedRecursionControl: CSF=0.0537, ERF=0.0000 (floor).
All emergence collapsed. 14x-19x below signal floor.

9. VERDICT

COMPRESSION-BOUNDED

10. KEY FINDINGS

(1) Emergence compresses efficiently: RIE > 1.0 for 11/14 conditions.
(2) Moderate compression (>=55% architecture) preserves emergence.
(3) Aggressive compression (<55%) pushes emergence to DEGRADING.
(4) Minimal viable architecture: ~55-60% of full structure.
(5) Sector differentiation is highly redundant (CSF=0.997 under blur).
(6) Recursive memory is redundant (CSF=0.995 under 35% memory loss).
(7) Coarse depth preserves full emergence (CSF=1.000).
(8) Low-precision recursion is viable (CSF=0.920 at 50% precision).
(9) Dimensions and interaction channels are least redundant.
(10) Hierarchy is preserved under all compression types except random.
(11) The emergence architecture is NOT overparameterized — compression
    reveals a clear minimal structural requirement.
(12) RIE superlinearity confirms structural compressibility, not
    overfit complexity.

11. ANSWER TO CORE QUESTION

The recursive emergence hierarchy is a DEEPLY COMPRESSIBLE structural
phenomenon. It survives substantial simplification — sector blurring,
memory compression, precision reduction, operator quantization, and
depth coarsening all preserve CSF > 0.90. The architecture exhibits
superlinear information efficiency (RIE > 1.0) for most compressions.

HOWEVER, there is a lower bound: approximately 55-60% of the full
architecture is the minimal viable structure below which emergence
degrades to DEGRADING/FAILED. This irreducible complexity represents
the genuine structural requirements of the emergence hierarchy, not
overparameterized artifacts.

The answer is thus layered:
  -- The architecture is NOT overparameterized (RIE > 1.0, most
     compressions tolerated)
  -- But neither is it fully compressible (minimal architecture ~55-60%)
  -- The structural core is ~55% of the full operator/sector structure
