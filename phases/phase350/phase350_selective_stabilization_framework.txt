================================================================================
THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK: SELECTIVE STABILIZATION
================================================================================

Selective recursive stabilization:
- Preferential reinforcement of specific pathways
- Suppression of unstable configurations
- Pathway competition and dominance
- Recursive structural filtering

Key distinction from prior phases:
- RESONANT STABILIZATION (Phase 349): Synchronized amplification
- SELECTIVE STABILIZATION (Phase 350): Preferential pathway retention

Selection measures pathway discrimination in persistence.
Resonance measures synchronized amplification.
Networks measure cooperative reinforcement.
Binding measures composite formation.

================================================================================
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
================================================================================

1. Initialize competing stabilization pathways
   - Multiple recursive configurations
   - Differential stability baselines
   - Competition initialization

2. Allow pathway interaction
   - Recursive reinforcement of stable pathways
   - Suppression of unstable configurations
   - Competition dynamics
   - Selection pressure

3. Introduce perturbations
   - Degradation pressure on all pathways
   - Instability events
   - Selection stress

4. Measurement framework
   - Selective gain: advantage of preferred pathways
   - Pathway discrimination: stable vs unstable separation
   - Suppression efficiency: degradation filtering
   - Persistence retention: selected pathway survival

================================================================================
METRIC DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

SELECTIVE_GAIN:
- Persistence advantage of preferred over non-preferred pathways
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate strong selection
- Values > 0.20 indicate meaningful selectivity

PATHWAY_DISCRIMINATION:
- Separation between stable and unstable configurations
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate sharp discrimination
- Values > 0.70 indicate strong separation

SUPPRESSION_EFFICIENCY:
- Degradation filtering effectiveness
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate efficient suppression
- Values > 0.60 indicate good filtering

PERSISTENCE_RETENTION:
- Retained organization in selected pathways
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate persistence
- Values > 0.70 indicate strong retention

RECURSIVE_SELECTIVITY:
- Persistence of selective structure across depths
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate stable selection
- Values > 0.70 indicate persistent selectivity

RG_SIMILARITY:
- Fine-grained vs coarse-grained selective preservation
- > 0.90 indicates stable scale invariance

================================================================================
NETWORK-SPECIFIC PREDICTIONS
================================================================================

P-A-N COMPOSITE:
- Strongest selective stabilization
- Best pathway discrimination
- Efficient suppression
- Persistent selection
- Forms strongest selective regime

P-A PAIR:
- Strong selective gain
- Good discrimination
- Moderate suppression
- Stable selection
- Strong pair selectivity

P-N PAIR:
- Moderate selective gain
- Some discrimination loss
- Partial suppression
- Saturating selection
- Weaker pair selectivity

A-N PAIR:
- Weak or negative selective gain
- Low discrimination
- Inefficient suppression
- Collapsing selection
- Weakest pair selectivity
