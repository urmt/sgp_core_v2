- Intentionality, purpose, goal-directed, designed
- Learning, adaptation, memory, recognition
- Meaning, significance, interpretation, representation

- Evolution, emergence (beyond structural pattern description)

- Bifurcation as "branching paths" with agency
- Merge/split as "social" or "cooperative" behavior
- Collapse as "failure" with emotional valence
- Perturbation migration as "escape" or "avoidance"
- Transition entropy as "disorder" or "chaos"
- Class attractors as "preferences" or "goals"

## REQUIRED LANGUAGE
- Structural, geometric, topological, algebraic
- Relational, organizational, pattern-based
- Metric-driven, threshold-based, classification-based
- Depth-dependent, operator-dependent, condition-dependent

## CORE PRINCIPLE
Phase 372 analyzes relational class transition dynamics as structural patterns in recursive computational systems. All descriptions must remain strictly mathematical and geometric.
