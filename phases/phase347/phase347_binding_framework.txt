THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK: ORGANIZATIONAL BINDING
================================================================================

Organizational binding:
- Multiple differentiated recursive structures remain jointly organized
- Partial identity differentiation preserved
- Fragmentation resistance maintained
- Composite coherence persists

Key distinction from prior phases:
- COEXISTENCE (Phase 341): Entities maintain separation
- CORRELATION (Phase 345): Synchronized variation across domains
- PROTECTION (Phase 346): Active resistance to degradation
- BINDING (Phase 347): Composite organizational persistence

Binding measures composite formation and persistence.
Protection measures resistance to degradation.
Correlation measures co-variation signal.
Coexistence measures entity separation.

METRIC DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

BINDING_STRENGTH:
- Persistence of unified organization across entities
- Range: [0, 1]
- High values indicate strong binding
- Values > 0.70 indicate bound organization

FRAGMENTATION_RESISTANCE:
- Resistance to recursive separation
- Measures how well structure maintains unity
- Values > 0.70 indicate high resistance

INTERNAL_DRIFT:
- Degradation within bound organization
- Range: [0, 1]
- Low values indicate bounded drift
- Values < 0.30 indicate good stability

COMPOSITE_COHERENCE:
- Persistence of higher-order integration
- Measures structural unity
- Values > 0.70 indicate good coherence

RECURSIVE_BINDING:
- Depth retention of bound structure
- Measures persistence across depths
- Values > 0.70 indicate persistent binding

RG_SIMILARITY:
- Fine-grained vs coarse-grained binding preservation
- > 0.90 indicates stable scale invariance
