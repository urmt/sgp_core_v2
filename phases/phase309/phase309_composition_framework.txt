A. COMPOSITIONAL ORGANIZATION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistent recursive organizational structure maintained under bounded
composition operations.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which universality classes and invariant regimes preserve
their defining properties when composed into larger organizational structures.

QUANTIFICATION:
Composition strength measures the coherence of compositional organization.

================================================================================
B. DECOMPOSITION STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Retention of organizational identity under bounded decomposition
transformations.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which compositional structure remains identifiable
and consistent when decomposed into constituent parts.

QUANTIFICATION:
Decomposition stability measures the preservation of compositional identity.

================================================================================
C. COMPOSITIONAL RETENTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistence of identifiable compositional structure across recursive
evolution.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which compositional organization maintains its defining
properties as recursion increases.

QUANTIFICATION:
Recursive retention measures the persistence of compositional structure.

================================================================================
D. COMPOSITIONAL TRANSITION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Bounded organizational transition between compositional regimes.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which transitions between compositional states remain
bounded and controlled rather than abrupt or catastrophic.

QUANTIFICATION:
Transition control measures the boundedness of compositional changes.

================================================================================
E. COMPOSITIONAL BOUNDEDNESS
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Constraint on unbounded divergence during recursive composition/decomposition.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which compositional operations maintain bounded structure
without uncontrolled expansion or collapse.

QUANTIFICATION:
Boundedness measures the containment of compositional organization.
Dispersion measures the degree of unbounded spread.

================================================================================
F. COMPOSITIONAL RG STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Scale-stable preservation of compositional organization.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which compositional structure survives coarse-graining
operations at different scales.

QUANTIFICATION:
RG stability measures the correlation between fine and coarse
compositional metrics.
