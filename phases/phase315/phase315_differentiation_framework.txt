================================================================================
A. RECURSIVE DIFFERENTIATION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Bounded emergence of distinguishable organizational structure preserving
recursive continuity across recursive evolution.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which organizational structures develop distinguishable
properties while maintaining bounded recursive continuity.

QUANTIFICATION:
Differentiation strength measures the coherence of organizational divergence.

================================================================================
B. DIFFERENTIATION STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Retention of differentiated structure during recursive restructuring.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which differentiated organization remains stable when
subjected to recursive evolution.

QUANTIFICATION:
Differentiation stability measures the preservation of distinguishability.

================================================================================
C. DIFFERENTIATION RETENTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistence of distinguishable organization across recursive depth.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which differentiation maintains its defining
properties as recursive depth increases.

QUANTIFICATION:
Recursive retention measures the persistence of differentiated structure.

================================================================================
D. DIFFERENTIATION TRANSITION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Bounded transition between differentiation-preserving regimes.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which transitions between differentiation states remain
bounded and controlled rather than abrupt or catastrophic.

QUANTIFICATION:
Transition control measures the boundedness of differentiation changes.

================================================================================
E. DIFFERENTIATION BOUNDEDNESS
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Constraint on unbounded divergence during recursive differentiation.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which recursive differentiation maintains bounded structure
without uncontrolled expansion or divergence.

QUANTIFICATION:
Boundedness measures the containment of differentiated organization.
Dispersion measures the degree of unbounded spread.

================================================================================
F. DIFFERENTIATION RG STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Scale-stable preservation of differentiated organizational geometry.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which differentiated structure survives coarse-graining
operations at different scales.

QUANTIFICATION:
RG stability measures the correlation between fine and coarse
differentiation metrics.

================================================================================
DIFFERENTIATION FRAMEWORK FORMALIZATION
================================================================================

DIFFERENTIATION = (D_str, D_stab, D_ret, Bound, T_ctrl, Disp, RG_stab)

Where:
- D_str: Differentiation strength
- D_stab: Differentiation stability
- D_ret: Differentiation retention
- Bound: Boundedness
- T_ctrl: Transition control
- Disp: Dispersion
- RG_stab: RG stability

================================================================================
HYPOTHESES

H1: Differentiation strength > 0.50
H2: Differentiation stability > 0.50
H3: Dispersion < 1.00 (bounded)
H4: Recursive retention > 0 across all sectors

DIFFERENTIATION-NEUTRAL: Moderate differentiation (strength > 0.40, stability > 0.40)
