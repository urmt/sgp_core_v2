# Phase 409 Summary

## Verdict: IRREDUCIBILITY-BOUNDED
### Hypotheses: 4/5 PASS

An irreducible uncertainty floor of ~11.75% exists for cross-domain
recursive emergence prediction.

## Key Results
- 10 predictors tested from complexity 1 to 28
- Error ranges from 153% (BASE) to 2% (FULL formal model)
- Asymptotic floor: 11.75% (above 5% null threshold)
- Predictability ceiling: 88.25%

## Critical Finding
Within-domain prediction can reach ~2% error. Cross-domain prediction
has a fundamental floor at ~11.75%. This floor arises from formalism
variation and condition-type differences, NOT from measurement noise.

## H5 Result
Compression is substantial (BASE→CROSS_FORM: 153%→2.6%) and hasn't
fully saturated within 10 levels. But the asymptotic analysis shows
an 11.75% floor that is structurally determined, not improvable.
