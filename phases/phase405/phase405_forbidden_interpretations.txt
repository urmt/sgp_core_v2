# Phase 405: Forbidden Interpretations

## STRICTLY FORBIDDEN

1. **"We have proven the axioms."** Axioms are assumed, not proven.
   The formalization establishes axioms as starting points, and the
   theorem candidates are conjectures with computational evidence,
   not formal proofs.

2. **"This proves emergence is real physics."** The formalization
   provides a mathematical framework for recursive relational
   emergence. It does not assert that the framework corresponds to
   physical reality. The empirical/formal separation explicitly
   maintains this boundary.

3. **"The theorem candidates are theorems."** They are candidates.
   Full proof construction is a separate phase (not yet completed).
   Calling them theorems before formal proof is misleading.

4. **"This completes the mathematical theory."** Formalization
   identifies structure, but proof dependency clarity (0.6473)
   indicates proof bridges are not yet fully specified. The
   mathematical theory is structurally identified but not fully
   derived.

5. **"Universality is proven."** Universality candidates are stated
   as candidates, not proven universals. Their confirmation requires
   independent verification across fundamentally different systems.

6. **"The axioms are minimal."** Minimality of the axiom set (3 axioms)
   is asserted but not proven. A future phase may identify redundant
   axioms or missing foundational elements.

7. **"The framework is a complete formalization of emergence."** The
   formalization covers the specific recursive relational framework,
   not emergence in general. Claims of general emergence
   formalization exceed scope.

## PERMITTED INTERPRETATIONS

1. "The recursive emergence framework has been expressed as a
   mathematical formalism with explicit axioms, invariants, and
   theorem candidates."
2. "All computational findings from Phases 391-404 have been
   translated into formal mathematical statements."
3. "A clear distinction has been established between empirically
   confirmed properties and provable theorem candidates."
4. "The formal dependency structure identifies which theorems depend
   on which axioms and prior theorems."
5. "The formalization is internally consistent across all 15 domains."
