SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. CONSTRAINT PROPAGATION
--------------------------
Definition: Persistence of restricted future state accessibility
under recursive composition.

Operationalization:
- Compute sector persistence probabilities across depths
- Measure constraint retention = persistence × accessible_fraction
- Track stability of constraint structure

B. ACCESSIBLE STATE REGION
--------------------------
Definition: Subset of embedding configurations reachable from
a prior relational sector.

Operationalization:
- Compute embedding distances between initial and terminal states
- Measure accessible_fraction = mean_distance / max_distance
- Track changes across recursive depth

C. CONSTRAINT RETENTION
-----------------------
Definition: Degree to which future states remain restricted
by prior sector structure.

Operationalization:
- retention = sector_persistence × accessible_fraction
- Higher retention = stronger constraint propagation
- Track across depths to measure propagation stability

D. PROPAGATION STABILITY
-------------------------
Definition: Consistency of constraint retention across
recursive depth.

Operationalization:
- Compute variance of constraint retention across depths
- Lower variance = more stable propagation
- RG correlation test for scale invariance

E. SECTOR MEMORY
----------------
Definition: Residual dependence of future sector membership
on prior sector history.

Operationalization:
- sector_persistence = P(terminal_sector = initial_sector)
- Higher persistence = stronger sector memory
- Classify as STRONG (>0.50), WEAK (0.30-0.50), MEMORYLESS (<0.30)
