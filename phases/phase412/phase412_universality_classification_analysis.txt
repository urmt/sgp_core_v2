fall into PERIPHERAL (4) or PARTIAL_MEMBER (2) categories.
None are fully excluded.

## Two Attractor Families

The distance matrix reveals a clear separation:
- Family 1 (SFH-SGP): isolated at distance 1.57-2.45 from all others
- Family 2 (Synthetics): cluster at inter-distances 0.45-1.40

SFH-SGP is NOT part of the synthetic cluster. It IS its own
attractor family. The 6 synthetic systems form a separate,
internally diverse cluster.

## Exclusion Classes

Three distinct exclusion class types identified:

1. **Class 1: NO_OPERATORS + NO_COMPOSITION** (3 systems)
   Most distant from SFH-SGP. Lack formal operator algebra AND
   operator composition. CA, GPS, EOS.
   
2. **Class 2: NON_RELATIONAL + NON_SIMILARITY** (6 systems)
   Universal across ALL synthetic systems. None use relational
   states or similarity-based emergence measures. This is the
   defining gap.
   
3. **Class 3: NON_MONOTONIC + NO_HIERARCHY** (4-5 systems)
   Neither monotonic depth dependence nor stable hierarchy.
   Affects most synthetic systems.

## H1 Failure Analysis

H1 (SFH-SGP requires specific constraints) failed because no
system achieved < 0.20 score (EXCLUDED). The lowest score was
0.27 (AgentInteraction), classified as PERIPHERAL.

This is because NO system is completely incompatible with
SFH-SGP features. Even the most distant system shares some
structural properties (e.g., recursive depth, partial determinism).

The real boundary is between CORE_MEMBER (1.00) and the rest
(0.27-0.55). The gap from 0.55 to 1.00 is the universality gap.

## Correspondence Gradient

Membership score correlates with Phase 411 correspondence:
r = 0.70, p = 0.08. The gradient is positive but does not
reach significance (n = 7, underpowered).

## Boundary Stability

84.9% classification stability under weight perturbation.
The boundary is reproducible but soft (3 classes seen for
most systems under different weightings).

## What SFH-SGP Represents

SFH-SGP is a specific universality class defined by:
1. Formal operator algebra (P, A, N)
2. Operator composition (generates sector hierarchy)
3. Relational state space (ordered triples)
4. Similarity-based emergence measure (rg_similarity)
5. Monotonic depth dependence (gamma(d) factor)
6. Stable hierarchy across depth

No synthetic system satisfies all 6 conditions. The two
partial members (SRS, TRS) satisfy 2-3 of them.

## Scientific Significance

SFH-SGP represents a BOUNDED universality class, not generic
emergence. It is one attractor family among many possible
recursive system families. The features that define it are
specific and measurable. Systems that share more features
show higher correspondence (r = 0.70 gradient).

This phase replaces the question "Is emergence universal?"
with the more precise: "What specific systems belong to the
SFH-SGP universality class, and what features define membership?"
