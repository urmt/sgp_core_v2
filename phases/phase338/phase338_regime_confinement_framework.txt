================================================================================
REGIME CONFINEMENT FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

This framework establishes operational definitions for analyzing whether
recursively stabilized organizational regimes exhibit confinement persistence,
transition resistance, bounded escape behavior, and protected recursive
occupancy.

================================================================================
CORE CONCEPT
================================================================================

A recursive regime is considered "confined" if:

1. PERSISTENCE INSIDE REGIME
   Recursive trajectories preferentially remain inside bounded stability
   regions rather than diffusing outward.

2. ESCAPE FREQUENCY SUPPRESSION
   The probability of leaving the regime remains low across recursion depths.

3. OCCUPANCY PERSISTENCE
   Regime occupancy persists across depth without significant depletion.

4. BOUNDED TRANSITIONS
   Regime transitions remain bounded rather than diffusive, meaning
   transitions occur within limited regions of state space.

================================================================================
KEY HYPOTHESES
================================================================================

H1: CONFINEMENT EXISTS
   Recursive trajectories remain inside stability regions.
   PASS if confinement_strength > 0.70 for at least one sector at deep recursion.

H2: ESCAPE SUPPRESSED
   Transitions between regimes remain rare.
   PASS if escape_rate < 0.25.

H3: OCCUPANCY RETAINED
   Recursive occupancy maintains stability.
   PASS if occupancy_retention > 0.80 through maximum depth.

H4: RG STABILITY
   Confinement structure preserved under coarse-graining.
   PASS if rg_similarity > 0.90.

H5: SECTOR HIERARCHY PERSISTS
   Sector ordering preserved across depths.
   PASS if Projection > Antisymmetry > Neutral ordering maintained.

================================================================================
OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1. CONFINEMENT STRENGTH (c)
   Measures persistence inside regime.
   Computed via:
   - Trajectory retention probability
   - Boundary crossing frequency
   - State space localization
   Range: [0, 1]
   c > 0.70 indicates strong confinement.

2. ESCAPE RATE (e)
   Probability of leaving regime per recursion step.
   Computed via:
   - Transition frequency measurement
   - Boundary escape probability
   - Diffusive loss rate
   Range: [0, 1]
   e < 0.25 indicates suppressed escape.

3. OCCUPANCY RETENTION (o)
   Recursive occupancy persistence.
   Computed via:
   - Occupancy decay rate
   - Retention probability
   - Stability maintenance
   Range: [0, 1]
   o > 0.80 indicates high retention.

4. TRANSITION DIFFUSION (d)
   Regime transition spread measurement.
   Computed via:
   - Transition variance
   - Diffusive spread
   - State space dispersion
   Range: [0, 1]
   Lower values indicate bounded transitions.

5. DRIFT VARIANCE (v)
   Bounded deformation under recursion.
   Range: [0, 1]
   Lower values indicate more stable organization.

6. RG SIMILARITY (r)
   Fine/coarse preservation of confinement structure.
   Range: [0, 1]
   r > 0.90 indicates stable confinement persistence.

7. CLASSIFICATION (cls)
   CONFINED: Strong confinement, low escape, high retention
   WEAKLY_CONFINED: Moderate confinement, some escape
   DIFFUSIVE: Weak confinement, significant diffusion
   UNSTABLE: No confinement detected

================================================================================
SECTOR DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

PROJECTION:
- Role: Strongest confinement preservation
- Expected: CONFINED or WEAKLY_CONFINED
- Confinement should be most pronounced

ANTISYMMETRY:
- Role: Moderate confinement with degradation
- Expected: WEAKLY_CONFINED or DIFFUSIVE
- Confinement degradation moderate

NEUTRAL:
- Role: Weakest confinement, more diffusion
- Expected: DIFFUSIVE or UNSTABLE
- Confinement may be absent

================================================================================
DEPTH ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Analyzed depths: [1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20]

Depth behavior:
- Shallow depths (1-4): Initial confinement formation
- Middle depths (6-12): Confinement stabilization
- Deep recursion (16-20): Confinement persistence or degradation

Expected pattern:
- Confinement should strengthen with depth (trajectories settle)
- Escape should remain suppressed (H2)
- Occupancy should remain stable (H3)

================================================================================
CONFINEMENT GEOMETRY
================================================================================

Confinement geometry describes how organizational trajectories remain
localized within bounded regions of state space:

1. TRAJECTORY LOCALIZATION
   Recursive trajectories cluster around stable configurations rather
   than spreading across the state space.

2. BOUNDED TRANSITIONS
   When transitions occur, they remain within limited regions rather
   than diffusing broadly.

3. ESCAPE SUPPRESSION
   The probability of escaping the confined region remains low.

4. OCCUPANCY PERSISTENCE
   The confined region maintains its occupancy over recursion depth.

================================================================================
VERDICT CRITERIA
================================================================================

VERDICT: HIGHLY_CONFINED
  - At least 4/5 hypotheses pass
  - Confinement strength > 0.75 at depth 20
  - Escape rate < 0.20 maintained

VERDICT: WEAKLY_CONFINED
  - 3/5 hypotheses pass
  - Confinement strength > 0.70 at depth 16
  - Some escape suppression observed

VERDICT: DIFFUSIVE
  - At most 2/5 hypotheses pass
  - Confinement degrades significantly
  - Diffusive transitions observed

VERDICT: UNSTABLE
  - No hypotheses pass
  - No confinement detected
  - Organization spreads without retention
