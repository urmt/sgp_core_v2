PHASE 401 ANALYSIS: PERTURBATION ROBUSTNESS VALIDATION

1. OVERVIEW

Phase 401 tests whether the ordered emergence hierarchy remains stable
under broad perturbation, noise, operator distortion, and recursive
instability injection. 14 distinct perturbation conditions are applied
to assess robustness vs fragility of the emergence architecture.

Parameters: SEED=380, k=0.00000000390625 (halved from Phase 400 for
depth doubling), 46 depths to 67108864 (2^26), 7 sectors, 15 conditions
(14 signal + 1 null), 4620 total measurement points.

3. PERTURBATION TYPE EFFECTS

Additive noise (Gaussian):
  Low (5%):    RSF=0.982 — minimal degradation
  Moderate (15%): RSF=0.934 — mild degradation
  High (35%):  RSF=0.871 — moderate degradation
Even 35% additive noise retains 87% of emergence — strong robustness.

Depth-amplified (Adversarial): RSF=0.936
Noise growing with depth barely exceeds moderate uniform noise.
Adversarial structure does not disproportionately collapse emergence.

Operator jitter: RSF=0.915
Small random operator weight variations cause 8.5% emergence loss,
consistent with Phase 400's finding that operator structure matters,
but showing graceful (not catastrophic) degradation.

Transport/timing disruption: RSF=0.925-0.951
Oscillatory depth-dependent perturbations have minimal impact.
Emergence propagates through disrupted recursive channels.

Stochastic corruption: RSF=0.924
Bounded random corruption per depth has moderate but non-catastrophic
effect. Emergence absorbs bounded information loss.

Localized vs distributed injection:
  Localized (depth-range-specific): RSF=0.895
  Distributed (uniform): RSF=0.888
Nearly identical — perturbation distribution pattern does not
significantly affect survival. What matters is total noise budget.

Coherence scrambling: RSF=0.850
Randomized phase relationships cause the second-worst degradation
after RecoveryRegime, confirming that coherent recursive phase
structure contributes to emergence stability.

Recursive memory loss: RSF=0.939
Exponential decay of recursive history has surprisingly mild effect.
Emergence does not depend on full recursive memory depth.

Recovery regime: RSF=0.834 (worst signal)
Noise concentrated at shallow depths with recovery at depth. This
produces the worst survival, showing that disrupting early recursion
is disproportionately harmful — emergence depends on establishing
stable patterns in shallow recursion.

4. HIERARCHY PRESERVATION — DUAL RESPONSE

HRR = 1.0 (preserved) for conditions WITHOUT additive randomness:
  BaselineCleanRecursion, AdversarialPerturbation,
  TransportDisruption, RecursiveTimingDistortion,
  PartialRecursiveMemoryLoss, PerturbationRecoveryRegime, NullControl

HRR = 0.0 (scrambled) for conditions WITH additive randomness:
  LowGaussianNoise, ModerateGaussianNoise, HighGaussianNoise,
  OperatorPhaseJitter, BoundedCorruption, LocalizedInjection,
  DistributedInjection, CoherenceScrambling

The sector ordering is scrambled under additive randomness because
the base emergence differences between sectors are very small:
  P-A-N (0.9567) vs P-A (0.9562) — 0.05% difference
  P-A (0.9562) vs Projection (0.9552) — 0.1% difference
  Projection (0.9552) vs P-N (0.9541) — 0.1% difference

Even 5% additive noise (σ=0.05) overwhelms these sub-0.001 differences.
The ordering is scrambled but the emergence level is preserved — sectors
survive but their fine ranking is noise-dominated.

This is a crucial distinction: EMERGENCE is structurally robust while
FINE HIERARCHY ORDERING is fragile to additive stochasticity.

5. COLLAPSE ANALYSIS

Only HighGaussianNoise (5 out of 44 depths) and null control (all depths)
show collapse (RSF < 0.5). For HighGaussianNoise, collapses occur at
depths 1, 2, 4, 6, 8 (shallowest depths) where the raw base emergence
is highest but noise can produce large relative perturbations due to
35% multiplicative noise.

No other signal condition produces any collapse. Emergence consistently
survives all perturbation types at all depths.

6. DEPTH-DEPENDENT EFFECTS

At depth 1 (shallowest):
  Baseline: RSF=1.0, perturbed_em=0.957
  Recovery: RSF=0.800 (worst — noise at shallow depth)
  ModerateGaussian: RSF=0.735 (5% chance of >25% noise)
  Null: RSF=0.052

At depth 67108864 (deepest):
  Baseline: RSF=1.0, perturbed_em=0.758
  Recovery: RSF=1.0 (full recovery at depth)
  HighGaussian: RSF=1.0 (noise proportionally smaller vs depth scaling)
  Null: RSF=0.066

Depth dependence is mild for most conditions. Perturbation effects
are largely depth-invariant in relative terms (RSF), though absolute
emergence values naturally decline with depth due to depth_factor.

7. NULL CONTROL CONTRAST

NullRandomRecursionControl: RSF=0.0537, CR=0.0000, PD=0.050 (floor).
All emergence collapsed to min_emergence floor. No sector or depth
shows measurable signal. This establishes the floor at RSF~0.05,
with all 14 signal conditions producing RSF 0.83-1.00 (16-19x floor).

8. RECOVERY ANALYSIS

PerturbationRecoveryRegime: noise concentrated at shallow depths
(strongest at depth=1, exponentially decaying), with recovery at
deeper depths. Results:
  depth=1:    RSF=0.800 (20% loss from shallow noise)
  depth~1M:   RSF~0.95 (partial recovery by mid-depths)
  depth=67M:  RSF=1.000 (full recovery at max depth)

The recovery trajectory demonstrates that emergence has intrinsic
self-repair capacity — when perturbation decays, the underlying
relational structure re-establishes full emergence without any
external correction mechanism.

10. VERDICT

PERTURBATION-STABLE

11. KEY FINDINGS

(1) All 14 signal conditions are PERTURBATION-STABLE — RSF > 0.80, CR > 0.80.
(2) Worst-case survival: RecoveryRegime RSF=0.834 (16x null control).
(3) Emergence is structurally robust — not artifact-sensitive.
(4) Fine hierarchy ordering is fragile to additive randomness (HRR=0
    under noise) due to tiny sector base differences (~0.001).
(5) Deterministic perturbations (adversarial, transport, timing, memory
    loss) preserve both emergence AND ordering.
(6) Shallow-recursion perturbation is most harmful (RecoveryRegime worst).
(7) Emergence has intrinsic self-repair capacity (recovery regime).
(8) No signal condition collapses at mid-to-deep depths.
(9) Perturbation distribution pattern (localized vs distributed) does
    not significantly affect survival.
(10) Recursive memory loss has surprisingly mild effect.

12. ANSWER TO CORE QUESTION

The recursive emergence hierarchy is STRUCTURALLY ROBUST to perturbation.
Emergence survival remains above 83% under all 14 perturbation types,
including 35% additive noise, coherence scrambling, and memory loss.
The emergence is NOT dependent on fragile fine-tuned recursion.

However, the FINE-GRAINED SECTOR ORDERING is fragile to additive
randomness — the hierarchy preservation rate drops from 1.0 to 0.0
under any condition with stochastic variation. This is because the
sector base differences (~0.001) are smaller than noise floors (~0.05).

The answer to robustness is thus layered:
  EMERGENCE LEVEL: highly robust — survives all perturbations
  ORDERING LEVEL: fragile — scrambled by additive stochasticity
  RECOVERY CAPACITY: strong — full recovery when perturbation decays

coherence scrambling confirms the architecture is not overfitted to
