# Phase 401 Framework: Perturbation Robustness Validation

## Purpose
Determine whether the ordered emergence hierarchy remains stable under broad perturbation, noise, operator distortion, and recursive instability injection. Distinguish artifact-sensitive emergence from structurally robust emergence.

## Core Distinction
artifact-sensitive emergence vs structurally robust emergence

## Perturbation Conditions
1. BaselineCleanRecursion — no perturbation
2. LowGaussianNoise — additive noise at 5% strength
3. ModerateGaussianNoise — additive noise at 15% strength
4. HighGaussianNoise — additive noise at 35% strength
5. AdversarialRecursivePerturbation — depth-amplified noise
6. OperatorPhaseJitter — small random operator weight variations
7. TransportDisruption — depth-dependent transport degradation
8. RecursiveTimingDistortion — oscillatory depth distortion
9. BoundedStochasticCorruption — bounded random corruption per depth
10. LocalizedPerturbationInjection — perturbation at specific depth ranges
11. DistributedPerturbationInjection — uniform across all depths
12. CoherenceScrambling — randomized phase relationships
13. PartialRecursiveMemoryLoss — exponential recursive decay
14. PerturbationRecoveryRegime — noise followed by recovery
15. NullRandomRecursionControl — complete randomization

## Metrics
- robustness_survival_fraction (RSF) — emergence surviving perturbation
- perturbation_resilience_index (PRI) — 1 minus normalized degradation
- collapse_threshold_depth (CTD) — depth where RSF falls below 0.5
- hierarchy_resilience_rate (HRR) — sector ordering preservation
- recursive_recovery_strength (RRS) — recovery under bounded corruption
- coherence_retention (CR) — fraction of coherence retained
- perturbation_drift_bound (PDB) — maximum drift under perturbation

## Classification Thresholds
- PERTURBATION-STABLE: RSF > 0.80, CR > 0.80
- PERTURBATION-BOUNDED: RSF > 0.60, CR > 0.60
- PERTURBATION-DEGRADING: RSF > 0.40, CR > 0.40
- PERTURBATION-FAILED: RSF <= 0.40
