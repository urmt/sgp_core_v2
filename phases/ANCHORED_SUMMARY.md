## Phase R — Minimal Generative Mechanisms (NEW)

**Question:** What is the absolute minimum generative mechanism required for recursive continuity?

**Discovery:** **Phase coupling is the single ESSENTIAL mechanism.** All other mechanisms (closure dynamics, cross-coupling, reconstruction, topology, temporal ordering, frequency alignment) are individually removable without catastrophic failure. **Mechanism Taxonomy:**
1. **ESSENTIAL (1):** Phase coupling — removal destroys continuity (17.5% survival)

### T091: Construct Validity Audit — metrics do not match their labels

The final audit phase examined whether the 9 operational measurements actually measure the theoretical constructs they claim to represent.

**Key findings:**
- **Inversion errors (2/9):** OC1 measures repetitiveness, not structure. IC1 measures branching, not extractable information. Both metrics give their highest scores to systems that least exhibit the claimed property.
- **Parametric contamination (3/9):** CD2, EC1, SR1 are 70-80% driven by simulator configuration parameters (self_model_level, self_model_influence). These measure how the simulator was configured, not what the system does.
- **Floor artifact (1/9):** IS2 has a built-in min=0.5, ensuring universal prevalence but measuring almost nothing.
- **Parameter leakage (1/9):** CD1 is 40% determinism parameter. Its empirical component (60%) is moderately valid.
- **Narrowly valid (1/9):** OC2 measures state distinguishability — a valid but narrow version of its theoretical label.

**Cross-reference with T090:** The construct validity failures explain the substrate-independence findings. Parametric contamination produces Markov artifacts. Inversion errors produce implementation-dependent variance. Floor artifacts produce spurious universality.

**Implication:** The 9 assumptions should not be treated as discovered universal properties. They are design commitments of T082's specific simulator, operationalized through metrics that only partially (and sometimes inversely) capture their theoretical labels.
