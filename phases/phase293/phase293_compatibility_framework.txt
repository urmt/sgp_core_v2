================================================================================
SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. RECURSIVE COMPATIBILITY
---------------------------
Definition: Statistical coexistence stability between recursive
propagation structures.

Operationalization:
- Compute overlap stability between sector pairs
- compatibility_strength = 1 - divergence_rate
- Higher compatibility = more stable coexistence

B. COMPATIBILITY REGIME
------------------------
Definition: A recursive sector configuration supporting bounded
coexistence dynamics.

Operationalization:
- Identify sector pair configurations
- Regime stable if overlap bounded and retention high
- Multiple regime types: compatible, weakly_compatible, incompatible

C. INCOMPATIBLE STRUCTURE
--------------------------
Definition: A recursive configuration exhibiting unstable overlap
or persistent divergence.

Operationalization:
- divergence_rate > threshold
- Overlap variance increases with depth
- No stable coexistence regime

D. COEXISTENCE STABILITY
-------------------------
Definition: Retention of sector organization under recursive overlap.

Operationalization:
- Track sector membership during overlap
- coexistence_retention = P(same sector | overlap)
- Higher retention = more stable coexistence

E. RG-STABLE COMPATIBILITY
----------------------------
Definition: Compatibility structure preserved under coarse-graining.

Operationalization:
- Compute fine-scale compatibility at multiple depths
- Compare coarse-scale (RG block) compatibility
- Correlation > 0.90 = RG_STABLE
