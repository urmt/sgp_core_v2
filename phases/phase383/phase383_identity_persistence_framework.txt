# Phase 383 Framework: Organizational Identity Persistence and Mode Differentiation

## Purpose
Determine whether emergent recursive organizational modes can maintain persistent distinguishable identities while undergoing recursive interaction, transport, and reproduction.

## Structure

### 1. Recursive Identity Tracking
- Track identity signatures across recursive depths
- Measure identity persistence (IP)
- Identify identity degradation patterns

### 2. Mode Differentiation Analysis
- Analyze mode differentiation under interaction
- Measure differentiation coherence (DC)
- Identify differentiation thresholds

### 3. Lineage Persistence Mapping
- Map mode lineage across recursive evolution
- Measure lineage stability (LS)
- Identify lineage preservation patterns

### 4. Identity Boundary Stability
- Track identity boundary integrity under perturbation
- Measure boundary integrity (BI)
- Identify boundary collapse thresholds

### 5. Transport Identity Preservation
- Track identity preservation during transport
- Measure transport identity alignment (TIA)
- Identify transport-stable identities

### 6. Composite Identity Coexistence
- Map coexistence of differentiated modes
- Measure coexistence stability (CS)
- Identify coexistence thresholds

### 7. Null Identity Controls
- Randomized identity baselines
- Confirm signal is not artifact
- Establish significance thresholds

## Metrics
- identity_persistence (IP)
- differentiation_coherence (DC)
- lineage_stability (LS)
- boundary_integrity (BI)
- coexistence_stability (CS)
- rg_similarity (RGS)
- transport_identity_alignment (TIA)

## Classification Thresholds
- IDENTITY-STABLE: IP > 0.90, DC > 0.90, LS > 0.85
- IDENTITY-BOUNDED: IP > 0.80, DC > 0.80, LS > 0.75
- IDENTITY-PARTIAL: IP > 0.70, DC > 0.70, LS > 0.65
- IDENTITY-DEGRADING: IP > 0.55
- IDENTITY-FAILED: IP <= 0.55
