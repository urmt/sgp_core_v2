SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. HIERARCHICAL ORGANIZATION
-----------------------------
Definition: Multi-level recursively persistent relational
structure across recursive scales.

Operationalization:
- Identify hierarchical levels: depth levels (1, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 20)
- Hierarchy strength = correlation(upper_level, lower_level)
- Higher correlation = more hierarchical

B. HIERARCHICAL STABILITY
-----------------------------
Definition: Bounded variability of cross-level relational
organization.

Operationalization:
- Compute cross-level variance across depths
- stability = 1 - normalized_variance
- Bounded variance = stable hierarchy

C. LEVEL RETENTION
--------------------
Definition: Persistence of recognizable hierarchical structure
under recursive propagation.

Operationalization:
- Track hierarchy strength across depths
- retention = correlation(level_d, level_d+k)
- Higher retention = more persistent hierarchy

D. HIERARCHICAL COORDINATION
------------------------------
Definition: Stable interaction consistency between recursive
organizational levels.

Operationalization:
- Compute cross-level coordination
- coordination = mean(correlation(levels_i, levels_j))
- Higher = more stable coordination

E. SECTOR HIERARCHY BIAS
--------------------------
Definition: Sector-dependent asymmetry in hierarchical
organization.

Operationalization:
- Compare hierarchy strength across sectors
- bias = |hierarchy_A - hierarchy_B| / avg
- Higher bias = more asymmetric

F. HIERARCHICAL RG STABILITY
-------------------------------
Definition: Scale-stable preservation of hierarchical structure.

Operationalization:
- Compare fine-scale hierarchy with coarse-grained
- Stability = correlation(fine, coarse)
- > 0.90 = RG_STABLE
