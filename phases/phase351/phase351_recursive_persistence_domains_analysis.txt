1. DOMAIN FORMATION IN PERSISTENCE SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Phase 351 tests whether recursive persistence systems can organize into
bounded coherent domains. This extends Phase 350's selective stabilization
by adding spatial localization of organizational persistence.

Key finding: PERSISTENCE DOMAINS EXIST for strong networks.

- P-A-N composite achieves DOMAIN-STABLE at depths 1-6
- P-A pair achieves DOMAIN-STABLE at depths 1-4
- Domain coherence for P-A-N: 0.9489 at depth 1, declining to 0.6356 at depth 20
- This represents bounded degradation within a persistent domain structure

Domain coherence > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (depths 1-8)
- P-A (depths 1-6)
- Projection (depths 1-6)
- Antisymmetry (depths 1-4)

11. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (DOMAIN COHERENCE > 0.75): PASS
- P-A-N, P-A, Projection achieve coherent domains

H2 (BOUNDARY RETENTION > 0.70): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 12
- A-N collapses before depth 8

H3 (DEGRADATION PENETRATION < 0.30): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains < 0.30 through depth 14
- Neutral already exceeds at depth 1

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- Only P-A-N at depth 1 achieves > 0.90
- Scale invariance limited to strong domains at early depths

H5 (DOMAIN HIERARCHY): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across depths

OVERALL VERDICT: WEAKLY_DOMAINED
- Domains form in strong networks (P-A-N, P-A)
- Domain boundaries persist for P-A-N through depth 12
- Degradation penetration bounded for P-A-N
- Weak domains (A-N, Neutral) collapse rapidly
- Scale invariance limited
