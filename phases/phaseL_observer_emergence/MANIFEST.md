## Primary Question
**Under what organizational conditions does recursive identity become self-referential?**

This phase does NOT claim consciousness. It investigates whether certain recursive organizational processes begin to show self-reference, internal continuity modeling, recursive self-maintenance, observer-like persistence, and perspective stability.

## Sub-Phase Summary

| Sub-phase | Title | Key Finding |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| L1 | Self-Reference Metrics | 8 operational metrics constructed from interactions of existing phase outputs. Self-reference composite driven by recursive_identity_score (r=0.963) and reconstruction_ability (r=0.852). |
| L2 | Observer-like Continuity | Passive persistence dominates (0.765). Active continuity is rare (0.130). Observer continuity is extremely rare (0.012). CG active/passive ratio = 0.679; CL = 0.026. |
| L3 | Internal Modeling | Local future prediction is trivial (0.927). Recursive correction (0.062) and reconstruction anticipation (0.114) are rare and distinguish observer-like systems. |
| L4 | Perspective Stability | Identity frame continuity is near zero (0.0015). Perspective drift is universal (0.920). Only CG systems show any perspective stability. |
| L5 | Process Observer Conditions | 4-level taxonomy: 92.5% passive (Level 0), 0% reactive (Level 1), 0.02% adaptive (Level 2), 7.5% observer-like (Level 3). Level 3 exists ONLY in CG regime (40% of CG). |
| L6 | Temporal Self-Modeling | Observer level does NOT significantly extend identity half-life. Half-life is uniformly high (~95 iterations) across all levels. |
| L7 | Failure Modes | Temporal continuity destruction causes near-total collapse (0.9996). For Level 3 systems: reconstruction destruction collapses 97%, closure feedback collapses 74%. |
| L8 | Adversarial Nulls | Composite self-reference collapses completely (1.000) under null. Observer organization requires recursive ordering — it is NOT statistical. |
| L9 | Synthesis | 5 organizational conditions jointly required: recursive closure, reconstruction ability, self-model consistency, temporal coherence, closure persistence. Met by 347/5000 systems (6.9%), ALL in CG. |

## Key Discovery
Observer-like organization is NOT a property of being complex. It is a property of specific organizational conditions that are rare (6.9% of all systems), fragile (temporal continuity collapse is total), and require recursive self-maintenance through five jointly-met conditions.
