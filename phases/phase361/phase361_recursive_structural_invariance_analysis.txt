1. STRUCTURAL INVARIANCE IN RECURSIVE SYSTEMS
================================================================================

Phase 361 tests whether recursively self-maintaining organizational systems
can preserve deep organizational structure despite recursive transformation,
perturbation, and depth scaling. This extends Phase 360's self-maintenance
stability by adding structural invariance dynamics.

Key finding: STRUCTURAL INVARIANCE EXISTS for strong networks.

- P-A-N composite achieves INVARIANT at depths 1-4
- P-A pair achieves WEAKLY_INVARIANT at depths 1-8
- Invariance retention for P-A-N: 0.9123 at depth 1, declining to 0.5989 at depth 20

Invariance retention > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (depths 1-12)
- P-A (depths 1-8)
- Projection (depths 1-8)
- Antisymmetry (depths 1-4)

9. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (INVARIANCE RETENTION > 0.75): PASS
- P-A-N, P-A, Projection achieve invariant structure

H2 (DEFORMATION BOUND < 0.30): PARTIAL
- P-A-N maintains < 0.30 through depth 8
- A-N exceeds 0.30 at depth 1

H3 (RECURSIVE STRUCTURE RETENTION > 0.70): PASS
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 16
- P-A maintains > 0.70 through depth 12

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- Only P-A-N at depth 1 achieves > 0.90
- Scale invariance limited to strong networks at early depths

H5 (STRUCTURAL HIERARCHY): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across depths

OVERALL VERDICT: WEAKLY_INVARIANT
- Structural invariance exists in strong networks (P-A-N, P-A)
- Deformation bounded for P-A-N through depth 8
- Recursive structure retention maintained for P-A-N through depth 16
- Weak systems collapse
- Scale invariance limited
