SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. COMPOSITE ORGANIZATION
--------------------------
Definition: A recursively persistent higher-order relational
configuration formed from interacting sectors.

Operationalization:
- Identify stable sector-pair configurations
- composite = sector_pair_center + bounded_variance
- Higher retention = more stable composite

B. COMPOSITE STABILITY
------------------------
Definition: Bounded variability of composite relational
organization across recursive depth.

Operationalization:
- Compute variance of composite metrics across depths
- Stability = 1 - normalized_variance
- Bounded variance = stable composite

C. COMPOSITE RETENTION
------------------------
Definition: Persistence of identifiable composite structure
under recursive propagation.

Operationalization:
- Track composite structure across depths
- retention = correlation(composite_d, composite_d+k)
- Higher retention = more persistent composite

D. SECTOR COMPOSITING BIAS
----------------------------
Definition: Sector-dependent asymmetry in composite
formation behavior.

Operationalization:
- Compare composite formation across sector pairs
- bias = |composite_A - composite_B| / avg_composite
- Higher bias = more asymmetric

E. METASTABLE COMPOSITE
------------------------
Definition: A bounded but non-fixed composite organizational
structure.

Operationalization:
- Check for bounded variability with nonzero retention
- metastable = bounded_variance AND retention > threshold
- Fixed = very low variance

F. COMPOSITE RG STABILITY
----------------------------
Definition: Scale-stable preservation of composite organization.

Operationalization:
- Compare fine-scale composite with coarse-grained
- Stability = correlation(fine, coarse)
- > 0.90 = RG_STABLE
