================================================================================
RECONSTRUCTION FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

This framework establishes operational definitions for analyzing whether
degraded recursive organizational regimes can recover prior structure,
reconstruct coherence after disruption, restore differentiated identity,
regain compatibility, and re-enter stable recursive organization.

================================================================================
CORE CONCEPT
================================================================================

A reconstructive recursive system exists if:

organizational structure degraded by recursive interaction can later
recover coherent differentiated organization.

Key phenomena tested:
- RECOVERY STRENGTH
  Degree of recovered organization after disruption
- RECONSTRUCTION PERSISTENCE
  Survival of recovered structure through recursion
- POST-DISRUPTION IDENTITY RESTORATION
  Restoration of prior differentiated structure
- BOUNDED RECOVERY DRIFT
  Residual degradation after recovery
- RECURSIVE RECONSTRUCTION STABILITY
  Stability of recovered organization

================================================================================
EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
================================================================================

For sector pairs, reconstruction behavior is analyzed:

1. INITIALIZE STABLE ORGANIZATION
   - Start with pre-disruption stable recursive organization
   - Measure baseline identity and compatibility

2. INTRODUCE CONTROLLED DEGRADATION
   - Recursive interference (from Phase 342)
   - Compatibility disruption
   - Bounded perturbation
   - Identity weakening

3. POST-DISRUPTION RECURSIVE EVOLUTION
   - Allow recursive evolution after disruption
   - Measure recovery trajectory
   - Track reconstruction strength

4. MEASURED RECOVERY METRICS
   - Reconstruction strength (vs pre-disruption)
   - Identity recovery (vs prior differentiation)
   - Recovery drift (residual degradation)
   - Compatibility restoration (vs pre-disruption)
   - Recursive recovery persistence

5. PRE vs POST COMPARISON
   - Pre-disruption baseline
   - Post-reconstruction final state
   - Recovery trajectory

================================================================================
KEY HYPOTHESES

H1: RECONSTRUCTION EXISTS
   Degraded structures recover organizational coherence.
   PASS if reconstruction_strength > 0.70 for at least one sector pair.

H2: IDENTITY RECOVERY PERSISTS
   Differentiated structure is restored.
   PASS if identity_recovery > 0.70 through depth 20.

H3: RECOVERY DRIFT REMAINS BOUNDED
   Residual degradation after recovery is limited.
   PASS if recovery_drift < 0.30 across most depths.

H4: RG RECOVERY STABILITY
   Reconstructed structure preserved under coarse-graining.

H5: RECOVERY HIERARCHY PERSISTS
   Pair recovery ordering mirrors pre-disruption hierarchy.
   PASS if P-A > P-N > A-N ordering maintained.

================================================================================
OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1. RECONSTRUCTION STRENGTH (r)
   Degree of recovered organization.
   r = post_recovery / pre_disruption
   Range: [0, 1]
   r > 0.70 indicates successful reconstruction.

2. IDENTITY RECOVERY (i)
   Restoration of prior differentiated structure.
   i = recovered_identity / original_identity
   Range: [0, 1]
   i > 0.70 indicates identity restoration.

3. RECOVERY DRIFT (d)
   Residual degradation after recovery.
   d = 1 - r
   Range: [0, 1]
   d < 0.30 indicates bounded drift.

4. COMPATIBILITY RESTORATION (c)
   Restored coexistence quality.
   c = post_recovery_compatibility / pre_disruption_compatibility
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate better restoration.

5. RECURSIVE RECOVERY PERSISTENCE (p)
   Persistence of recovered structure.
   p = recovery_stability_across_depth
   Range: [0, 1]
   Higher values indicate stable recovery.

6. RG SIMILARITY (g)
   Fine/coarse recovery preservation.
   Range: [0, 1]
   g > 0.90 indicates stable recovery.

7. CLASSIFICATION (cls)
   RECONSTRUCTING: Strong recovery, stable persistence
   WEAKLY_RECOVERING: Moderate recovery, some persistence
   RECOVERY-DEGRADING: Weak recovery, drift dominant
   IDENTITY-RESTORING: Identity preserved, weak structure
   COLLAPSING: No recovery, structural collapse
