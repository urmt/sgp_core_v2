# Phase 502 Update Summary: Correspondence-Localization Investigation

## What Was Done
Phase 502 determined WHICH specific organizational structures produce correspondence between SFH-SGP and independent mathematical systems. Analyzed localization across seven predefined SFH-SGP components: emergence hierarchy, operator weights, sector bases, depth hierarchy, attractor signatures, information channels, and algebraic constraints.

## Key Results
- **Strong localization (6/7 components)**: Emergence hierarchy (5/8 systems), operator weights (4/8 systems), sector bases (5/8 systems), depth hierarchy (5/8 systems), attractor signatures (5/8 systems), algebraic constraints (5/8 systems) — all with p < 0.05
- **No localization (1/7 component)**: Information channels (0/8 systems)
- **Perfect localization** (ρ = 1.0, p = 0.0) in Renormalization Group, Category Theory, Tensor Systems for emergence/operator/sector/depth components
- **Strong localization** (ρ ≥ 0.96, p ≤ 0.001) in Algebraic Recursion and Attractor Systems for multiple components
- **Unique patterns**: Topology showed perfect anti-correlation (ρ = -1.0) in operator weights; Information Geometry showed no significant localization

## Scientific Meaning
Per Tier 3 rules, this **does not** imply ontology, consciousness, physics derivation, retrofitting, universality, explanation, causation, reductionism, foundations, or metaphysics.
It **does** imply: shared constrained organizational structure at specific localization sites between SFH-SGP and these mathematical systems.

## Verdict
**CORRESPONDENCE-LOCALIZATION-BOUNDED**: Statistically nontrivial bounded correspondence localizes to specific SFH-SGP organizational components.
