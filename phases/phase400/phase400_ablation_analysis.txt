PHASE 400 ANALYSIS: OPERATOR ABLATION AND NECESSITY STRUCTURE

1. OVERVIEW

Phase 400 systematically ablates the three recursive relational operators
(P=Projection, A=Antisymmetry, N=Neutrality) to determine the causal
dependency structure of emergent organizational regimes. The core question
is whether specific operators are NECESSARY, REDUNDANT, or SUFFICIENT for
each emergence layer, or whether ordered emergence is a generic consequence
of arbitrary recursive complexity.

Parameters: SEED=370, k=0.0000000078125 (same as Phase 399), 44 depths to
33554432, 7 sectors, 15 ablation conditions (10 static + 3 dynamic + 1
null + 1 baseline), 4515 total measurement points.

2. OPERATOR NECESSITY HIERARCHY (FIRST FINDING)

Cross-condition ONI computed by comparing P-A-N emergence with and without
each operator:

  P (Projection):   ONI=0.507 — MOST NECESSARY
  A (Antisymmetry): ONI=0.451 — INTERMEDIATE
  N (Neutrality):   ONI=0.332 — LEAST NECESSARY

The hierarchy P > A > N is clear and reproducible. Removing P halves
emergence survival (ESF=0.493); removing A causes moderate degradation
(ESF=0.549); removing N causes the mildest degradation (ESF=0.668).

This confirms that directional relational mapping (P) is the foundational
operator, asymmetric ordering (A) provides structural refinement, and
neutral indifference (N) tunes stability — a dependency structure
consistent with the Phase 391-399 emergence sequence (propagation →
topology → stabilization).

4. DYNAMIC CONDITION ANALYSIS

StochasticDropout (random operator scheduling):
  ESF=0.459 — second-worst after null among non-null conditions.
  Unpredictable operator absence at any depth prevents stable recursive
  propagation, causing near-collapse even though operators are present
  ~50% of the time. This demonstrates that emergence requires consistent
  operator availability across recursive depth.

DelayedActivation (operator ramp-in):
  ESF=0.570 — moderate survival. Initial shallow recursion (where
  operators are weak) fails to establish stable organizational patterns,
  and deep recursion cannot fully compensate.

RecursiveIntermittency (oscillating operator weights):
  ESF=0.415 — worst non-null condition. Operators oscillating in and
  out of effectiveness generate unstable recursive propagation that
  prevents sustained organizational structure. The phase cancellation
  patterns disrupt coherence accumulation.

AsymmetricWeighting (P=1.0, A=0.7, N=0.3):
  ESF=0.632 — moderate boundedness. The reduced-neutrality profile
  (N at 30%) constrains but does not eliminate emergence. This most
  closely resembles operator profiles where weak neutrality is present
  but suboptimal.

6. HIERARCHY PRESERVATION

Sector ordering is preserved across ALL 15 conditions:
  P-A-N > P-A > Projection > P-N > Antisymmetry > Neutral > A-N

HPR=1.0 for every condition, including null control (where the values
are all driven to floor but the relative ordering persists). This
demonstrates that the sector hierarchy is structurally encoded in the
base sector configuration itself, not dependent on operator activation.

7. MINIMAL SUFFICIENCY ANALYSIS

Minimal Operator Sufficiency (MOS) scores for P-A-N:
  FullPABaseline:  1.000  — all operators, fully sufficient
  WeakenedN:       0.766  — N halved, still mostly sufficient
  WeakenedA:       0.681  — A halved, moderate sufficiency
  WeakenedP:       0.640  — P halved, reduced sufficiency
  NRemoved:        0.563  — N absent, limited sufficiency
  ARemoved:        0.423  — A absent, poor sufficiency
  PRemoved:        0.360  — P absent, insufficient

Minimal sufficient operator set: any single operator is insufficient
(MOS < 0.5). The minimal viable set requires at least two operators,
with P+A > P+N > A+N. This maps to:
  P+A (MOS=0.563): viable but degraded
  P+N (MOS=0.423): poor
  A+N (MOS=0.360): insufficient

Thus operator necessity is not binary but graded: P is the dominant
constraint, A provides secondary scaffolding, N fine-tunes stability.

12. KEY FINDINGS

(1) Operator necessity: P (0.507) > A (0.451) > N (0.332).
(2) P removal halves emergence (ESF=0.493); N removal retains 2/3 (0.668).
(3) Dynamic unpredictability (intermittency, dropout) is more destructive
    than static removal of any single operator except P.
(4) Ablation effects are depth-invariant — operator necessity is a
    structural property, not depth-dependent.
(5) No single operator is sufficient; at least two operators required.
(6) P+A > P+N > A+N in sufficiency, confirming P as dominant constraint.
(7) Sector hierarchy preserved under all 15 conditions.
(8) Weakening is less destructive than removal (graceful degradation).
(9) Delayed activation prevents pattern establishment in shallow recursion.
(10) Null control ESF=0.053 establishes artifact-free noise floor.

13. ANSWER TO CORE QUESTION

Emergent organizational regimes are not generic consequences of arbitrary
recursive complexity. They are STRUCTURALLY CONSTRAINED by specific
recursive relational operators with a clear necessity hierarchy:
Projection (P) is the most critical operator, Antisymmetry (A) provides
secondary ordering, and Neutrality (N) tunes stability. Removing or
significantly weakening any operator degrades emergence proportionally
to that operator's importance weight. The ordered emergence sequence
from Phase 391-399 depends specifically on the full P-A-N operator
complement — particularly the P operator.
