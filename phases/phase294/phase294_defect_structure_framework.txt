================================================================================
SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. RELATIONAL DEFECT
--------------------
Definition: Localized deviation from dominant recursive
compatibility structure.

Operationalization:
- Identify trajectory points with low compatibility
- Compute deviation from sector centroid
- defect_density = count(defect_points) / total_points

B. DEFECT PERSISTENCE
----------------------
Definition: Retention of localized incompatibility under
recursive propagation.

Operationalization:
- Track defect locations across depths
- persistence = P(defect at depth d+1 | defect at depth d)
- Higher persistence = more stable defects

C. DEFECT TRANSPORT
--------------------
Definition: Propagation of localized incompatibility through
recursive depth.

Operationalization:
- Compute defect displacement per depth step
- transport_strength = mean_defect_displacement / depth
- Bounded transport = stable defect propagation

D. DEFECT INTERACTION
------------------------
Definition: Behavior of multiple incompatibility regions
during recursive overlap.

Operationalization:
- Measure compatibility between defect clusters
- interaction_strength = overlap / separation
- Repulsive = incompatible, attractive = merging

E. BOUNDARY DEFECT
--------------------
Definition: Defect concentrated near sector transition regions.

Operationalization:
- Count defects within threshold distance of sector boundary
- boundary_concentration = defects_near_boundary / total_defects
- Higher concentration = boundary-localized defects

F. RECURSIVE DEFECT STABILITY
--------------------------------
Definition: Persistence of defect identity across recursive
depth scaling.

Operationalization:
- Compare fine-scale defect patterns with coarse-grained
- Correlation > 0.90 = RG_STABLE
- Bounded retention across depths

================================================================================
SECTION 2: DEFECT CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

DEFECT-SUPPRESSING:
- Defect density decreases with depth
- High persistence, bounded dispersion
- Boundaries suppress defect formation

DEFECT-NEUTRAL:
- Defect density stable with depth
- Moderate persistence
- No systematic suppression or amplification

DEFECT-AMPLIFYING:
- Defect density increases with depth
- Low persistence, high dispersion
- Recursive propagation amplifies defects
