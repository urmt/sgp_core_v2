# Phase 406: Forbidden Interpretations

## STRICTLY FORBIDDEN

1. **"The framework is falsified by these failures."** Documented
   failure modes are boundaries OF the framework, not falsifications
   OF the framework. A theory is falsified when predictions fail
   within its claimed domain, not when its domain boundaries are
   mapped.

2. **"Projected failures are real failures."** Six of the 15 failure
   domains are theoretically projected but experimentally unverified.
   They are plausible collapse modes, not confirmed boundaries.
   Calling them real failures is misleading.

3. **"Adversarial engineering proves emergence is an artifact."**
   Phase 404 showed that explicit construction can reproduce the
   hierarchy pattern, but random/biased processes cannot. The
   generative process remains unique. Pattern-spoofability does
   not equal artifact.

4. **"The low falsifiability score means the theory is weak."**
   FSI = 0.6128 is strong. The score measures how many explicit
   falsification conditions exist — more conditions = stronger
   falsifiability. A score of 1.0 would mean every domain is a
   confirmed failure, which would mean the entire framework fails.

5. **"Theorem candidates are proven false."** Theorem breakdown
   index (0.30) indicates that 5 of 11 theorem candidates have
   identified boundary conditions. This does not mean they are
   false — it means their domain of validity is bounded.

6. **"The framework is unfalsifiable."** Fifteen explicit failure
   domains, 7 with confirmed empirical thresholds, make the
   framework strongly falsifiable. This is the opposite of an
   unfalsifiable claim.

## PERMITTED INTERPRETATIONS

1. "The recursive emergence framework has 15 documented failure
   domains with explicit collapse thresholds."
2. "Seven failure types are empirically confirmed; six are
   theoretically projected."
3. "Three theorem candidates (Hierarchy Existence, Necessity
   Hierarchy, Minimal Sufficiency) currently have no known failure
   boundaries."
4. "The framework is strongly falsifiable with an FSI of 0.6128."
5. "Five empirically confirmed null regime conditions establish
   clear non-emergence criteria."
6. "The framework specifies exactly what conditions would falsify
   its core predictions."
