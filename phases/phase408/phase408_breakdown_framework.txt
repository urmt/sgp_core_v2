# Phase 408: Predictive Breakdown

## Objective: Map where predictive emergence forecasting fails

Phase 407 established that prediction works within the training
distribution. Phase 408 tests whether prediction failure regions
are lawfully structured or arbitrarily noisy.

---

## Adversarial Conditions (7)

Each condition violates a different assumption of the Phase 407
predictor:

| Condition | Violated Assumption | Parameter Range |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Extreme operator amplification | Linear weight scaling | 1.0x – 5.0x |
| Asymmetric recursive weighting | Uniform depth decay | k_ratio 0.1 – 10 |
| Hidden recursive coupling | Independent operators | Coupling 0 – 1 |
| Discontinuous operator switching | Stationary operators | Switch depth varied |
| Depth-chaotic recursion | Sequential depth order | Chaos 0 – 1 |
| Recursive phase inversion | Positive-only operators | Inversion depth varied |
| Stochastic bifurcation injection | Deterministic application | Probability 0 – 1 |

---

## Critical Questions

1. Do predictive errors grow monotonically with condition extremity?
2. Are there sharp bifurcation points or smooth transitions?
3. Do any conditions produce irreducible uncertainty (error that
   does not decrease with more data)?
4. Are predictive failure regions themselves structurally organized?
5. Can predictive failure be predicted?

---

## Metrics

- predictive_breakdown_threshold: parameter value at which
  prediction error exceeds 50%
- bifurcation_error_growth: rate of error increase near boundaries
- uncertainty_inflation_rate: how much error grows per unit of
  adversarial parameter change
- coupling_nonlinearity_index: deviation from linear error growth
- irreducibility_score: proportion of error that persists even
  with perfect training data
- chaos_transition_depth: depth at which chaotic order causes
  prediction breakdown

---

## Success Criteria

- All 7 conditions produce structured (not arbitrary) error growth
- Breakdown thresholds are reproducible
- Error growth follows lawful patterns
- At least 5 of 7 conditions have identifiable thresholds
