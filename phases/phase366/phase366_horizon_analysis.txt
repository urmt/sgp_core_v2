1. FINITE-HORIZON BEHAVIOR
================================================================================

Phase 366 investigates why recursive closure degrades gracefully beyond depth 20
rather than collapsing catastrophically. This phase characterizes the finite
stability horizon and identifies the mechanisms responsible for degradation.

Key finding: DEGRADATION IS GRADUAL, not catastrophic.

- P-A-N degradation slope: 0.025 mean, max 0.085 at depth 40
- No abrupt collapse observed at any depth
- RG similarity declines smoothly from 0.9012 (depth 1) to 0.3812 (depth 40)

6. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

H1 (DEGRADATION IS GRADUAL): PASS
- Degradation slope remains bounded (max 0.085 at depth 40)
- No abrupt collapse observed

H2 (DRIFT EXPLAINS HORIZON): PASS
- Drift accumulation correlates 0.92 with RG degradation

H3 (SCALE FRAGMENTATION EMERGES): PASS
- Fragmentation index increases from 0.020 to 0.085 beyond depth 20

H4 (HORIZON EXTENSION IMPROVES): PASS
- HorizonExtension: +0.12 mean gain; RG > 0.70 through depth 28

H5 (HIERARCHY PERSISTS): PASS
- P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N preserved across all conditions and depths

OVERALL VERDICT: HORIZON-CHARACTERIZED
- Finite stability horizon is a genuine property of recursive closure
- Degradation is gradual, not catastrophic
- Drift accumulation is the primary degradation mechanism
- Scale fragmentation emerges beyond depth 20
- Horizon extension operators can delay degradation by 4-8 depths
