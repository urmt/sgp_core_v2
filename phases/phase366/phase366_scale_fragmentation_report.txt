5. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

SCALE FRAGMENTATION SUMMARY:
- Fine/coarse decoupling increases linearly from 1% to 26% (depths 1-40)
- Hierarchy ordering is preserved across all scales (100% consistency)
- Cross-scale correlation declines from 0.98 to 0.48
- Scale isolation emerges at depths 14-28 depending on network strength
- Scale fragmentation contributes ~15% to total RG degradation

IMPLICATION:
Scale fragmentation is a tertiary degradation mechanism.
Cross-scale drift (35%) and local drift (28%) are primary mechanisms.
Scale fragmentation becomes significant beyond depth 20.
