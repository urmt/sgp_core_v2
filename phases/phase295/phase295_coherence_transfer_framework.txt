SECTION 1: OPERATIONAL DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

A. RELATIONAL COHERENCE
-----------------------
Definition: Persistence of coordinated relational structure
under recursive interaction.

Operationalization:
- Compute trajectory alignment across depths
- coherence_strength = mean(tangent_correlation)
- Higher coherence = more coordinated structure

B. COHERENCE TRANSFER
----------------------
Definition: Retention of coordinated organization after
recursive propagation across sectors.

Operationalization:
- Track coordinated structure across sector boundaries
- transfer_retention = P(coherent | sector_boundary_crossing)
- Higher retention = better transfer

C. COHERENCE DEGRADATION
-------------------------
Definition: Loss of coordinated organization under
recursive depth increase.

Operationalization:
- Compare coherence at depth d vs depth d+k
- degradation_rate = (coherence_d - coherence_d+k) / k
- Bounded degradation = stable transfer

D. INTERFACE COHERENCE
-----------------------
Definition: Transfer behavior occurring near sector
transition boundaries.

Operationalization:
- Compute coherence near boundary regions
- boundary_transfer = coherence_at_boundary / coherence_at_center
- Exchange efficiency at interfaces

E. RECURSIVE COHERENCE STABILITY
---------------------------------
Definition: Depth-invariant coherence retention behavior.

Operationalization:
- Track coherence across all recursive depths
- Stability = 1 - variance(coherence_across_depths)
- Higher stability = better recursive preservation

F. COHERENCE SUPPRESSION
-------------------------
Definition: Sector-dependent reduction of transferable
coordinated structure.

Operationalization:
- Compare transfer efficiency across sectors
- suppression = 1 - (sector_transfer / max_transfer)
- Higher suppression = weaker transfer
