# Phase 410: Forbidden Interpretations

## STRICTLY FORBIDDEN

1. **"68% empirical means the framework is unscientific."**
   Empirical evidence is the foundation of science. Most scientific
   theories are predominantly empirical. A formal theory with thin
   empirical core is mathematics, not science.

2. **"H1 failed, so the framework has no provable content."**
   H1 failed only for the non-axiom threshold. Three axioms and
   two conditional invariants are formally derivable. The framework
   has a formal skeleton — it's just limited.

3. **"IRREDUCIBLE elements mean the framework is wrong."**
   Irreducible uncertainty is a feature of bounded theories.
   Quantum mechanics has irreducible uncertainty. It marks the
   boundary of what the framework can determine, not a flaw.

4. **"CONTINGENT elements should be discarded."**
   Contingent findings are valid within their domain. They are not
   universal, but they are not useless. Context-specific knowledge
   is valuable.

5. **"This proves the framework is 'just empirical pattern-matching'."**
   The formal skeleton (axioms + conditional invariants) captures
   necessary structure. The empirical body captures observed
   regularities. Both are required for a complete theory.

## PERMITTED INTERPRETATIONS

1. "The framework is primarily empirical with a limited formal core."
2. "Most theorem candidates are contingent on specific assumptions."
3. "Two universality candidates are in irreducible uncertainty."
4. "The formal skeleton (23%) provides structural necessity."
5. "The empirical body (68%) provides content and specificity."
