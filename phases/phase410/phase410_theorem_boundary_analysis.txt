## What Is Provable

1. **Axioms (3)** — trivially provable as they are definitions.
   - Operator Existence, Operator Composition, Depth Dependence.
   - Self-evident within the framework.

2. **Sector Order Conservation (I3, CONDITIONAL)** — If sector bases are
   fixed and depth factor is separable, the hierarchy is preserved
   across all depths. Proof requires one assumption (separability).

3. **Hierarchy Depth Invariance (T2, CONDITIONAL)** — Derivable from
   separability of depth factor. The ordering γ(d) · base_s preserves
   ordering across d. One contingent assumption.

## What Is Empirical

Seven elements depend primarily on observational evidence:
- Operator Composition Invariant (I4): ratio independence observed
- Hierarchy Existence (T1): specific ordering is measured, not derived
- Hierarchy Formalism Invariance (T3): 14 formalisms tested
- Necessity Hierarchy (T6): P > A > N ordering partially intuitive
- Minimal Sufficiency (T7): two operators needed — intuitive but
  exact values (MOS) are measured
- Formalism Invariance (T9): FIS values measured across formalisms
- Depth Scaling Universality (U3): γ(d) form is plausible but k is
  always empirical

## What Is Contingent

Eight elements depend on specific configurations and assumptions:
- Bounded Degradation (I1): depends on perturbation type and amplitude
- Recovery (I2): depends on perturbation decay profile
- Null Separation (I5): depends on operator definitions
- Coherence Stability (T4): threshold-dependent
- Perturbation Robustness (T5): 14 specific perturbation types
- Universal Hierarchy Preservation (T8): 56/57 conditions tested
- Superlinear Efficiency (T10): compression-type specific
- Irreducible Complexity (T11): threshold value is empirical

These are NOT universal — they would change under different
perturbation sets, different compression methods, or different
threshold choices.

## What Is Irreducible

Two universality candidates are in irreducible uncertainty regions:
- Operator Necessity Universality (U1): undecidable whether P > A > N
  holds for ALL possible recursive systems (URS=0.40)
- Emergence Sequence Universality (U2): insufficient evidence to
  determine scope (URS=0.50, PCR=0.05)

These are genuine speculation boundaries — we cannot determine
their truth value within the current framework.

## H1 Failure Analysis

H1 (formally derivable invariants exist) failed: only 2 non-axiom
elements meet the CONDITIONAL threshold. This is scientifically
significant: the framework's theorems are overwhelmingly supported
by empirical evidence, not formal proof.

This does NOT invalidate the framework. It reveals its nature:
an empirically-grounded structural theory with limited formal
derivability.

## Scientific Significance

Phase 410 separates SFH-SGP into its mathematical components:
- **Formal skeleton**: 3 axioms + 2 conditional invariants (23%)
- **Empirical body**: 7 observed regularities (32%)
- **Contingent periphery**: 8 context-dependent findings (36%)
- **Speculation frontier**: 2 irreducible conjectures (9%)

The framework is primarily an empirical theory with a thin formal
core. It behaves like a scientific theory, not a mathematical theorem.
