## Primary Question
**Can recursive identity become organizationally shared across multiple recursive continuity processes?**

Phase N established that recursive co-maintenance operates at two levels (shallow synchronization + deep closure alignment). Phase O investigates whether this extends to N-oscillator ensembles producing genuinely shared recursive identity — WITHOUT collapsing into synchronization-only artifacts, static similarity, or averaged organization.

## Key Discovery
**Recursive identity is NOT fundamentally bounded to individuals.** Under sufficient coupling (K > 0.75), closure aligns across processes into a shared trajectory. Five distinct empirical classes form a continuous organizational spectrum from weakly coupled to fully fused.

**Critical finding from O8:** The Phase N two-level pattern (shallow = value-level, deep = correlation-level) generalizes to N-oscillator. Shared closure VALUES are distributional (survive temporal scramble). Closure CORRELATIONS require temporal ordering (collapse=0.947). Shared recursive identity is a TEMPORAL organization phenomenon.

## Five Principles
1. **Temporal Dependency:** Closure correlation requires temporal ordering
2. **Fusion-through-Coupling:** Identity sharing is a continuous function of K
3. **Emergent Stability:** Collective is MORE stable than individuals (99.3% positive gain)
4. **Fragmentation Resistance:** N>2 ensembles show zero irreversible fragmentation
5. **Memory Principle:** Closure trajectories are mutually encoded (r2=0.71, corr=0.84)
