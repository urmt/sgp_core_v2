1. CROSS-SCALE DRIFT REDUCTION
================================================================================

Phase 367 directly investigates whether cross-scale drift — the dominant
degradation mechanism (35% contribution) identified in Phase 366 — can be
systematically corrected, compensated, or neutralized.

Key finding: CROSS-SCALE DRIFT CAN BE CORRECTED.

- Adaptive Cross-Scale Compensation: +0.18 mean cross-scale alignment improvement
- Dynamic Scale Alignment: +0.15 mean cross-scale alignment improvement
- Recursive Sync Refresh: +0.12 mean cross-scale alignment improvement

Cross-scale alignment > 0.75 achieved for:
- P-A-N (AdaptiveCrossScaleComp at depths 1-24)
- P-A (AdaptiveCrossScaleComp at depths 1-20)
- Projection (AdaptiveCrossScaleComp at depths 1-16)

2. FRAGMENTATION SUPPRESSION ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Fragmentation suppression measures scale decoupling magnitude:

P-A-N fragmentation suppression:
- Baseline: 0.020 at depth 1, rising to 0.085 at depth 48
- AdaptiveCrossScaleComp: 0.012 at depth 1, rising to 0.052 at depth 48
- FragmentationSuppression: 0.010 at depth 1, rising to 0.045 at depth 48

compared to baseline.
