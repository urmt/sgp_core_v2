================================================================================
A. INTERFACE ORGANIZATION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistent recursive relational structure between bounded organizational domains.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which interaction between domains forms stable, identifiable
interface structures.

QUANTIFICATION:
Interface strength measures the coherence of interaction patterns between
bounded domains.

================================================================================
B. INTERFACE STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Retention of bounded interaction structure under recursive propagation.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which interface structure between domains remains stable
through recursive transformation.

QUANTIFICATION:
Interaction stability measures the preservation of interface organization.

================================================================================
C. INTERFACE RETENTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Persistence of identifiable interface structure across recursive depth.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which interface patterns remain identifiable as recursion
increases.

QUANTIFICATION:
Recursive retention measures the persistence of interface identity.

================================================================================
D. CROSS-DOMAIN INTERACTION
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Recursive relational coupling between organizational domains.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which domains influence each other's organizational
structure at their interface.

QUANTIFICATION:
Cross-domain coupling measures the interaction strength between domains.

================================================================================
E. INTERFACE BOUNDEDNESS
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Constraint on unbounded interface dispersion.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which interface interaction remains contained rather than
dispersing infinitely.

QUANTIFICATION:
Boundedness measures containment of interface structure.
Dispersion measures unbounded spread at interfaces.

================================================================================
F. INTERFACE RG STABILITY
================================================================================

DEFINITION:
Scale-stable preservation of interface organization.

OPERATIONAL MEASURE:
The degree to which interface structure survives coarse-graining.

QUANTIFICATION:
RG stability measures correlation between fine and coarse interface metrics.
