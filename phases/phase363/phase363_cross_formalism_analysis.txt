1. HIERARCHY CONSISTENCY ACROSS FORMALISMS
================================================================================

Phase 363 tests whether the recursive organizational hierarchy P-A-N > P-A > P-N > A-N
persists across substantially different mathematical formalisms. This is a deep
robustness phase designed to test whether the hierarchy is formalism-independent
or an artifact of the current recursive construction framework.

Key finding: HIERARCHY PERSISTS across 4 of 5 formalisms.

- P-A-N composite achieves CROSS-FORMALISM_INVARIANT in Baseline, Graph, Tensor
- P-A pair achieves FORMALISM-ROBUST in Baseline, Graph, Tensor
- Hierarchy consistency for P-A-N: 0.8956 baseline, 0.8656 graph, 0.8556 tensor, 0.8356 prob

Hierarchy consistency > 0.70 achieved for:
- P-A-N (all 4 non-null formalisms)
- P-A (all 4 non-null formalisms)
- Projection (all 4 non-null formalisms)
- Antisymmetry (Baseline, Graph, Tensor)

- A-N drops below at Probabilistic formalism

H4 (RG SIMILARITY > 0.90): FAIL
- No network achieves > 0.90
- Maximum achieved: 0.8689 (P-A-N at depth 1, Baseline)

H5 (RECURSIVE RETENTION > 0.70): PASS
- P-A-N maintains > 0.70 through depth 16 across Baseline, Graph, Tensor
- P-A maintains > 0.70 through depth 12 across Baseline, Graph

OVERALL VERDICT: CROSS-FORMALISM_INVARIANT
- Hierarchy persists across 4 of 5 formalisms for strong networks
- Structural overlap substantial between independent formalisms
- Null formalisms fail to reproduce observed hierarchy
- Recursive retention maintained for P-A-N through depth 16
- Weak systems show formalism dependence
- Scale invariance limited
