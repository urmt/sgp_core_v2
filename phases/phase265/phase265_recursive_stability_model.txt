================================================================================
RECURSIVE PERSISTENCE HIERARCHY
================================================================================

This model defines levels of organizational complexity for persistence-
capable relational structures. Each level builds upon properties of lower
levels. The model is constrained to empirical observations and does not
imply consciousness, awareness, or mental states.

================================================================================
LEVEL 0: NOISE / NON-PERSISTENT DYNAMICS
================================================================================

Definition:
  Structures exhibiting only random fluctuations without identifiable
  coordinated organization.

Properties:
  - Coordination metric C(t) shows no systematic structure
  - Autocorrelation decays to noise within τ << observation window
  - No response to perturbation beyond statistical fluctuation
  - Null model indistinguishable from signal

Mathematical characterization:
  P(C(t+τ) > C(t)) ≈ 0.5 for all τ
  Var(C) ≈ baseline noise level
  Autocorrelation function: ACF(τ) ≈ 0 for τ > τ_noise

Empirical observations:
  - Random Gaussian embeddings
  - Uncorrelated time series
  - Shuffled data baselines

Tier status: TIER_0_BASELINE (non-persistent)

================================================================================
LEVEL 1: TRANSIENT LOCAL COORDINATION
================================================================================

Definition:
  Structures exhibiting brief coordinated states that do not persist
  beyond local time scales or spatial regions.

Properties:
  - C(t) shows occasional coordinated episodes
  - Episode duration τ_ep << observation window
  - Spatial extent limited to local neighborhoods
  - No recursive stabilization across episodes
  - Decays to baseline after perturbation

Mathematical characterization:
  P(coordinated) < P(random baseline) + threshold
  τ_ep << observation window
  Spatial correlation ξ << system size
  No memory effect: P(C_t+1 | C_t) ≈ P(C_t+1)

Empirical observations:
  - Sub-critical oscillator populations
  - Sparse graph clusters (transient)
  - Early-stage synchronization attempts

Tier status: TIER_1_CANDIDATE

================================================================================
LEVEL 2: STABLE COORDINATED PERSISTENCE
================================================================================

Definition:
  Structures exhibiting coordinated states that persist under observation
  and maintain organization across perturbation conditions.

Properties:
  - C(t) maintains elevated values over full observation window
  - Response to perturbation is reversible
  - Hysteresis absent (or minimal)
  - Coordination survives:
    * topology perturbation (edge removal)
    * frequency perturbation (parameter variation)
    * finite-size variation (N changes within range)
  - Sharpening observed: coordination increases faster near transition

Mathematical characterization:
  C_mean >> C_random_baseline
  P(C_t+τ | C_t) > P_random(τ) for τ within window
  Sharpening index S > threshold
  Recovery after perturbation R > threshold

Empirical observations:
  - Super-critical oscillator populations
  - Stable graph clusters
  - Kuramoto synchronization above K_c

Tier status: TIER_2_VERIFIED

================================================================================
LEVEL 3: RECURSIVE STABILIZATION
================================================================================

Definition:
  Structures where current coordinated state contributes to maintaining
  future coordinated states, exhibiting self-sustaining organization.

Properties:
  - Memory effect: past coordination predicts future coordination
  - Recursive contribution R(S_t) > baseline
  - Self-reinforcing structure without external drive
  - Coordination at t increases P(coordination at t+1)
  - Level 3 implies Level 2 properties plus additional memory structure

Mathematical characterization:
  P(C_{t+1} > C_thresh | C_t > C_thresh) >> P(C_{t+1} > C_thresh)
  R(S_t) = P(C_{t+1} | C_t) - P_null(C_{t+1})
  R(S_t) > threshold for Level 3 classification

Empirical observations:
  - Stable limit cycles
  - Attractor states in dynamical systems
  - Recurrent coordination patterns (T007, T008)

Tier status: TIER_3_EXPLORATORY (requires further validation)

================================================================================
LEVEL 4: FERTILE INTERACTION-CAPABLE PERSISTENCE
================================================================================

Definition:
  Structures capable of maintaining persistence through interaction,
  generating additional persistent organization rather than decoherence.

Properties:
  - Interaction survivability W > threshold
  - Fertility index F > baseline
  - Net increase in persistent organization after interaction
  - Recursive stabilization maintained through interaction
  - Level 4 implies Level 3 properties plus interaction capability

Mathematical characterization:
  W(S_A) = 1 - (C_after - C_before)/(C_before + ε) > threshold
  F(I) = ΔP_total - ΔP_baseline > 0
  P(C_future | interaction) > P(C_future | no interaction)

Empirical observations:
  - Coupled oscillator systems
  - Interacting dynamical systems with stable attractors
  - Systems demonstrating persistent coordination through perturbation

Tier status: TIER_3_EXPLORATORY (requires further validation)

================================================================================
TRANSITION CRITERIA
================================================================================

Level 0 → Level 1:
  C(t) exceeds random baseline by σ for at least τ_min episodes
  No further criteria required

Level 1 → Level 2:
  C(t) maintains elevated level for observation window
  Sharpening observed: S > threshold
  Recovery after perturbation: R > threshold

Level 2 → Level 3:
  Memory effect: R(S_t) > threshold
  Recursive probability exceeds null model

Level 3 → Level 4:
  Fertility: F > baseline after interaction
  Survivability: W > threshold
