SECTION A: PERSISTENCE DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A structure exhibits persistence if and only if it maintains identifiable
coordinated organization under perturbation while preserving relational
geometry properties observable across finite sample sizes.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:

Let S be a relational structure with coordination metric C(S, t) at time t.

S is persistent if:
  ∀ perturbation P ∈ P_set:
    ∃ ε > 0: ||C(S, t) - C(S, t + δt)|| < ε after P perturbation

where P_set represents:
  - topology perturbation (edge removal/addition)
  - frequency perturbation (oscillator parameter variation)
  - finite-size variation (N changes)
  - temporal perturbation (time window shifts)

OBSERVATIONAL CONSTRAINT:
Persistence is measured relative to baseline coordination in identical
systems without perturbation. Properties must be empirically verifiable.

SECTION B: RECURSIVE STABILIZATION DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A persistent structure exhibits recursive stabilization when its coordinated
state contributes to maintaining future coordinated states, independent of
initial conditions and implementation details.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:

Let S_t be coordination state at time t.
Let R(S_t) be the recursive contribution function.

Recursive stabilization exists if:
  ∃ δ > 0: P(C(S_{t+1}) > C(S_t)) > baseline when R(S_t) is high

where baseline is the probability under random coordination.

HIERARCHICAL STRUCTURE:
  Level 0: Noise / non-persistent dynamics
  Level 1: Transient local coordination
  Level 2: Stable coordinated persistence
  Level 3: Recursive stabilization achieved
  Level 4: Fertile interaction-capable persistence

OBSERVATIONAL CONSTRAINT:
Recursive stabilization requires showing that current coordination
probability exceeds chance level given past coordination history.

SECTION C: INTERACTION SURVIVABILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A persistent structure demonstrates interaction survivability when coherent
organized structure remains identifiable after interaction with external
systems or perturbations.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:

Let S_A and S_B be two structures.
Let I(S_A, S_B) be the interaction operation.

Survivability metric W:
  W(S_A) = 1 - (C_after - C_before) / (C_before + ε)

where C_before is pre-interaction coordination, C_after is post-interaction,
and ε prevents division by zero.

W ∈ [0, 1]: higher values indicate greater survivability.

OBSERVATIONAL CONSTRAINT:
Must measure coordination before and after interaction perturbation
in controlled experimental conditions.

================================================================================
SECTION D: FERTILITY DEFINITION
================================================================================

OPERATIONAL DEFINITION:
A persistent structure possesses fertility when interacting structures
generate additional persistent organization rather than decoherence.

MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION:

Let ΔP be change in total persistent organization after interaction I.

Fertility F(I) = ΔP_total - ΔP_baseline

where ΔP_baseline is expected change from random interaction.

F > 0 indicates fertile interaction.
F ≈ 0 indicates neutral interaction.
F < 0 indicates destructive interaction.

OBSERVATIONAL CONSTRAINT:
Fertility requires comparing actual interaction outcomes against
null model predictions (random shuffle baseline).

   - Appears as:
     * correlation time increase
     * variance amplification
   - Independent of specific slowing rate
