# Phase 373 Framework: Recursive Relational Attractor Geometry

## Purpose
Determine whether invariant relational classes and their transition pathways converge toward stable higher-order attractor geometries.

## Structure

### 1. Attractor Basin Mapping
- Identify attractor basins in relational class space
- Map basin boundaries and volumes
- Measure attractor convergence (AC)

### 2. Recursive Trajectory Convergence
- Track recursive trajectories toward attractors
- Measure convergence rate and stability
- Identify convergence thresholds

### 3. Hysteresis Analysis
- Measure hysteresis in attractor transitions
- Identify reversible vs. irreversible transitions
- Quantify hysteresis gap (HG)

### 4. Collapse Avoidance Dynamics
- Map collapse pathways relative to attractor basins
- Measure collapse avoidance index (CAI)
- Identify attractor shielding effects

### 5. Attractor Persistence Tracking
- Track attractor stability across recursive depths
- Measure attractor persistence (AP)
- Identify attractor degradation patterns

### 6. Perturbation Displacement Recovery
- Apply perturbations to displace from attractors
- Measure recovery trajectories
- Identify basin depth and resilience

### 7. Null Attractor Controls
- Randomized attractor baselines
- Confirm signal is not artifact
- Establish significance thresholds

## Metrics
- attractor_convergence (AC)
- basin_stability (BS)
- hysteresis_gap (HG)
- trajectory_coherence (TC)
- collapse_avoidance_index (CAI)
- rg_similarity (RGS)
- attractor_persistence (AP)

## Classification Thresholds
- ATTRACTOR-STABLE: AC > 0.75, BS > 0.75, CAI > 0.75
- ATTRACTOR-BOUNDED: AC > 0.65, BS > 0.65, CAI > 0.65
- ATTRACTOR-PARTIAL: AC > 0.55, BS > 0.55, CAI > 0.55
- ATTRACTOR-DEGRADING: AC > 0.40
- ATTRACTOR-FAILED: AC <= 0.40
