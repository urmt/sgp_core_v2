## Primary Question
**Can recursive continuity persist THROUGH organizational regime transitions, or is it fundamentally tied to stable regimes?**

Phase O established that recursive identity can become organizationally shared. Phase P tests whether this shared identity — and individual recursive continuity — survives regime-level transformation. This is the critical test of whether continuity is more fundamental than the regime it occupies.

## Key Discovery
**Recursive continuity persists through organizational regime transitions at 73.6% (individual) and 83.5% (shared identity).** This extends Phase K (identity > geometry) into the temporal domain: identity-through-transition > regime identity.

**Critical finding: Closure persists even when ORDER collapses.** The "collapsing" taxonomic class (21.2%) shows order dropping from 0.877→0.476 but closure survives at 100%. This confirms closure is ontologically more fundamental than the current regime geometry.

**Shared identity is MORE robust than individual under transition** (83.5% vs 73.6%). The collective buffers individual disruption.

## Six Principles
1. **Closure Primacy:** Closure persists through order collapse
2. **Transition Continuity:** Most transitions preserve identity
3. **Shared Robustness:** Distributed identity > individual under transition
4. **Smooth Transition:** Closure space transitions are smoother than phase
5. **Null Robustness:** Closure VALUE survives temporal scramble (unlike closure CORRELATION)
6. **Fragmentation Threshold:** Only K→0 destroys continuity at >50%
